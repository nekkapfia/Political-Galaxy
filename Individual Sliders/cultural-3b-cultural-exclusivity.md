# Cultural 3 – Culural Exclusivity
## 3B – Cultural

## 1. Definition

Note that this slider is intended to be scored in conjunction with Core 3A, Ethnic Exclusivity.
Cultural Exclusivity measures the strictness of the cultural adoption and transformation required for full membership of the core in-group. It covers the difficulty and extent of immersion in the group's culture and way of life, together with the degree of severance from previous cultural groups, traditions and heritage.

**Core continuum**

- **100** = Complete abandonment of old ancestral roots/culture, full immersion in our culture and way of life, loyalty tests, severance of old ties. Maximum cultural transformation is required for full membership.
- **Low** = Light or optional cultural alignment.
- **0** = We do not care whether you adopt our culture or align with us at all. Cultural adoption is irrelevant to full membership.

**Bright-line tests**
- If a person must completely sever old cultural ties, fully immerse in our culture, pass loyalty tests and demonstrate total transformation to achieve full membership → high score.
- If cultural adoption is optional or light and full membership is available without meaningful transformation → low score.
- If cultural adoption is simply irrelevant → near 0.

Scoring uses only observable reality concerning the cultural requirements for **full membership**. Immigration, deportation and resident non-members are irrelevant (see Document 1).


---

## 2. Avoidance Rules

Do not use as evidence:
- The ethnic/ancestral criterion itself (that is Ethnicity / 3A)
- Immigration or border policy
- Deportation
- Treatment of resident non-members
- Mere existence of a culture or pride in it (only the *requirement* that others adopt it for full membership counts)

## 3. Scoring Bands

### 90–100 — Maximum Cultural Transformation Required
Complete abandonment of prior culture and full immersion in our culture is mandatory for full membership.

- 100: Absolute requirement. Complete severance of old roots, full cultural immersion, loyalty tests and total transformation are mandatory. Partial adoption is insufficient.
- 95–99: Extremely demanding cultural transformation. Only the most thorough adopters achieve full membership.
- 90–94: Very high cultural barriers. Deep immersion and demonstrable break with prior identity are required for full membership.

### 80–89 — High Cultural Demand
Strong requirements for language mastery, behavioural conformity, loyalty and cultural immersion. Partial or superficial adoption is insufficient for full membership.

- Full membership requires demonstrable immersion in the group's culture and way of life.
- Loyalty tests and behavioural conformity are mandatory.
- Old cultural ties must be substantially abandoned.

### 70–79 — Predominantly Demanding
Clear and significant cultural adoption requirements exist. Full membership is difficult without substantial transformation.

- Core institutions and definitional claims treat cultural immersion as the normal and preferred path to full membership.
- A person must demonstrate clear alignment with the group's culture, values and way of life.
- Partial adoption is not enough.

### 60–69 — Moderate-to-High Demand
Noticeable cultural alignment, language and loyalty expectations. Effort is required but complete severance is not demanded.

- Cultural adoption carries significant weight in granting full membership.
- Language mastery, behavioural conformity and loyalty are expected.
- Old cultural ties can be retained to some degree but must not conflict with the group's culture.

### 50–59 — Moderate Demand
Cultural expectations exist and carry weight, but are not extreme. Multiple pathways with varying levels of adoption are possible.

- Some cultural alignment is required but not transformative.
- Loyalty and behavioural conformity are expected in key areas.
- Old cultural ties are tolerated as long as they do not conflict with core group norms.

### 40–49 — Light-to-Moderate Demand
Some cultural alignment is preferred or expected, but requirements are manageable and not transformative.

- Cultural adoption is encouraged but not mandatory for full membership.
- Light loyalty and behavioural expectations exist.
- Old cultural ties are largely compatible with full membership.

### 30–39 — Light Demand
Only light cultural expectations. Full membership is available with modest alignment.

- Cultural adoption is optional in most areas.
- Basic loyalty and behavioural norms are expected but not rigorously enforced.
- Old cultural ties are fully compatible with full membership.

### 20–29 — Minimal Demand
Cultural adoption is optional or very light. Full membership does not require meaningful transformation.

- Cultural alignment is not a decisive criterion.
- Loyalty and behavioural expectations are minimal.
- Old cultural ties have no impact on full membership.

### 10–19 — Near-Indifference to Culture
Cultural alignment has almost no role in granting full membership.

- Cultural adoption is irrelevant or purely voluntary.
- No meaningful loyalty or behavioural tests exist for full membership.
- Old cultural ties have zero impact.

### 6–9 — Near-Indifference with Residual Trace
Cultural adoption has almost no role. The faintest residual expectation of cultural alignment remains.

- Full membership is determined almost entirely by non-cultural criteria.
- Any remaining cultural language is purely historical or decorative and carries no binding force.
- In practice, a person of any background is treated as a full member without cultural transformation.

### 5 — Borderline Indifference
Cultural adoption has no role in defining full membership except in rare symbolic contexts with no binding force.

### 1–4 — Complete Indifference
Cultural adoption has zero role in determining full membership of the core in-group.

- No cultural criterion exists in law, institutions, public definition, or meaningful practice.
- Definitional claims about membership make no reference to cultural adoption, loyalty or way of life as a requirement.
- Old cultural ties are treated as private or irrelevant facts for the purposes of full membership.

### 0 — Absolute Indifference
Cultural adoption has zero role and the system actively rejects cultural conformity as a basis for full membership.
