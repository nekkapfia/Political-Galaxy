# Cultural 2A: Individuality

**Source Document**  
**Date:** April 16, 2026

## Cultural 2A Definition

This guideline measures the observable degree to which a system treats individuals as sovereign moral and legal agents versus subordinating them to group identity, collective outcomes, or ascribed categories. 

It tracks whether outcomes, rights, responsibilities, and status are determined primarily by individual actions, merit, character, and choices or by group membership (ethnic, racial, class, gender, caste, etc.). 

Scoring is based exclusively on empirical reality (observable enforcement in law, education curricula, employment and promotion practices, family and inheritance policy, media narratives, institutional governance, public rituals, and statistical outcomes of group vs individual allocation) — never on claims, justifications, official rhetoric, or moral framing.

## Scoring Bands

### High-End Guideline (90–100) — Compressed zone
Absolute or near-absolute treatment of individuals as sovereign agents with zero tolerance for group-based allocation of outcomes, rights, or status. This allows observable variation within different individualist systems (different mechanisms of merit and personal responsibility while retaining total rejection of group primacy).

- **100** = Pure individualism: every outcome, right, responsibility, and status is determined solely by individual actions, merit, character, and voluntary choices with no group-based differentiation in any domain.
- **95–99** = Near-pure individualism: group considerations are absent except in the most marginal, temporary, and narrowly defined cases.
- **90–94** = Extreme individualism legitimation: individual agency dominates law, education, employment, and institutions with maximum enforcement and cultural reinforcement.

### 80–89 (High – Strict individual treatment with minimal group exceptions)
- Law and institutions recognise only individual rights and responsibilities; group quotas, affirmative action, or collective guilt are absent or extremely limited.
- Outcomes in education, employment, and justice are determined by individual merit and behaviour.

### 70–79 (Mid-High – Predominantly individualist)
- Individual treatment is the norm, with only narrow, targeted exceptions for group considerations.

### 51–69 (Mid-Range – Mixed individual vs group treatment)
- Observable balance or tension between individual merit and group-based policies.

### 31–50 (Mid-Low – Noticeable group-based allocation)
- Group identity significantly influences outcomes in multiple domains (education, employment, law, etc.).

### 11–30 (Low – Strong group primacy)
- Group membership is a primary determinant of rights, status, and outcomes across most domains.

### 0–10 (Floor – Pure group/collectivist allocation)
- Almost all outcomes, rights, and status are determined by group identity with minimal or no regard for individual merit.
