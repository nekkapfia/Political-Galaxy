# Cultural 3A: Ethnicity

**Source Document**  
**Date:** April 16, 2026

## Cultural 3A Definition

This guideline measures the observable basis on which a culture defines its core in-group (“us”) — the degree to which group membership and belonging are determined primarily by shared ethnicity, ancestry, and descent versus primarily by shared ideas, values, behaviours, citizenship, or voluntary adoption. 

Scoring is based exclusively on empirical reality (observable enforcement in immigration law, citizenship policy, education curricula, family and inheritance rules, media narratives, public rituals, institutional preferences, and statistical outcomes of group continuity or dissolution) — never on claims, justifications, official rhetoric, or moral framing.

## Scoring Bands

### High-End Guideline (90–100) — Compressed zone
Absolute or near-absolute definition of the in-group by shared ethnicity, ancestry, and descent. This allows observable variation within different ethnic-based systems (different mechanisms of ethnic preference and continuity while retaining total rejection of purely civic or ideological belonging).

- **100** = Pure ethnic foundation: every core aspect of belonging, citizenship, and group continuity is determined exclusively by blood, ancestry, and descent with no observable civic or ideological alternatives permitted.
- **95–99** = Near-pure ethnic foundation: ethnicity overwhelmingly determines group membership; only the most marginal, temporary, and narrowly defined non-ethnic pathways exist.
- **90–94** = Strong ethnic foundation: ethnic criteria dominate law, education, immigration, and institutions with maximum enforcement and cultural reinforcement.

### 80–89 (High – Strict ethnic definition with minimal non-ethnic entry)
- Citizenship and immigration policy strongly prioritises ethnic ancestry, language tied to ethnic heritage, and cultural continuity.
- Institutional and social preferences for ethnic in-group members are overt and widespread.

### 70–79 (Mid-High – Predominantly ethnic with limited civic pathways)
- Ethnic criteria remain dominant but some managed non-ethnic entry exists with strong assimilation requirements.

### 51–69 (Mid-Range – Mixed ethnic vs civic definition)
- Observable tension or balance between ethnic descent and civic/ideological belonging.

### 31–50 (Mid-Low – Predominantly civic with ethnic remnants)
- Citizenship and belonging increasingly defined by ideas, values, or legal status rather than descent.

### 11–30 (Low – Strong civic/ideological foundation)
- Group membership is primarily determined by shared ideas, behaviours, or voluntary adoption with minimal ethnic weighting.

### 0–10 (Floor – Pure civic/ideological foundation)
- Ethnicity and descent play almost no role in defining the in-group; belonging is entirely based on ideas, citizenship, or voluntary assimilation.
