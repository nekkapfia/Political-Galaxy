### Democratic Party (as a totality)
**Final Score: 49**

The Democratic Party positions itself as the defender of fairness, opportunity and inclusive progress within a constitutional framework. Its core aims are reducing inequality, expanding rights and opportunity, and demonstrating competent governance, while managing deep internal tensions between transformative justice currents and more managerial ones.

#### Moral Justification
* Justice, fairness and the moral imperative to expand opportunity and reduce inequality appear regularly and often function as higher-order moral requirements.
* Evidence-based policy, economic outcomes, fiscal sustainability and governability remain central to the detailed defence of programmes.
* Natural-rights and constitutional language is retained but frequently subordinated to progressive fairness and equity claims.
* International and domestic policy blends moral-duty language with pragmatic assessments of national interest and institutional capacity.

The party’s language is mixed and contested. It claims both moral seriousness on justice and the practical capacity to govern. Justice and fairness are presented as substantive duties by its left, while competence, evidence and electability dominate the centre. Neither source fully displaces the other, producing a genuinely mixed profile.

#### Why this produces a final score of 49
Both transcendent (justice and fairness as moral goods) and mundane (evidence, outcomes, pragmatic governance) sources are active and recurring. Transcendent framing is more prominent than in the Republican Party but does not fully dominate detailed justification.

### Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, Progressive Left, Justice Democrats (Primary Faction)
**Final Score: 65**

This current treats systemic justice and the correction of structural inequalities as binding moral requirements. Its aim is transformative change pursued through the Democratic Party rather than pure outsider rupture.

#### Moral Justification
* Systemic justice and the moral imperative to correct structural inequalities are central.
* Equity, inclusion and solidarity with marginalised groups are treated as binding moral requirements.
* Existing economic and political arrangements are frequently criticised in moral terms.
* Evidence and feasibility are acknowledged but usually subordinated to the prior ethical framework of justice.
* Dignity-based and universalist language is prominent.

The language is moralised and justice-centred. Structural inequalities are framed as moral failures that demand correction, not merely as policy problems to be managed. The faction presents itself as the ethical core of the Democratic Party, insisting that competence and electability must not become substitutes for moral purpose.

#### Why this produces a final score of 65
Transcendent sources (systemic justice and equity as moral imperatives) carry substantial weight. Mundane considerations of evidence and governability are present but secondary, producing a clear transcendent lean.

### Bernie Sanders / DSA-aligned, Democratic Socialists, Hard Left (Primary Faction)
**Final Score: 72**

This current frames capitalism and concentrated economic power as fundamentally unjust systems requiring structural transformation. Its aim is economic rights, social ownership and solidarity as moral requirements.

#### Moral Justification
* Capitalism and concentrated economic power are treated as fundamentally unjust systems.
* Economic rights, social ownership and solidarity are framed as moral requirements rather than policy preferences.
* Class analysis and systemic critique supply a strong moral framework.
* Pragmatic and electoral considerations exist but are secondary to the ethical rejection of the current economic order.

The language is systemic and condemnatory. The existing economic order is presented as a moral failure that incrementalism cannot adequately address. The faction presents itself as the bearer of transformative justice against both capitalism and moderate accommodation within the Democratic Party.

#### Why this produces a final score of 72
The transcendent source is stronger than in the broader Progressive Left because the critique of capitalism is framed as a fundamental moral and systemic indictment.

### Hakeem Jeffries / Moderate Centrist Democrats (Primary Faction)
**Final Score: 42**

This current emphasises pragmatism, electability and workable governance. Fairness language is retained but tempered by feasibility and broader electoral appeal.

#### Moral Justification
* Pragmatism, electability and workable governance are central.
* Fairness and opportunity language is retained but tempered by concerns about feasibility and broader appeal.
* Evidence, outcomes and institutional stability carry substantial weight.
* Progressive moral claims are accepted selectively rather than as overriding imperatives.

The language is managerial and electoral. Justice claims are present but filtered through the requirements of winning and governing. The faction presents itself as the serious, electable face of the Democratic Party against both left maximalism and Republican populism.

#### Why this produces a final score of 42
Mundane sources (pragmatism, evidence, outcomes, institutional stability) are stronger than on the party’s left. Residual fairness language prevents a strongly mundane score.

### Institutional / Party Establishment (Secondary Faction)
**Final Score: 45**

This current prioritises institutional stability, legislative effectiveness and protection of the party’s governing capacity.

#### Moral Justification
* Institutional stability and legislative effectiveness are central.
* Moral and justice language is used but frequently subordinated to considerations of what can actually be passed and sustained.
* Donor, organisational and coalition-management realities shape justificatory emphasis.

The language is institutional and power-focused. The faction presents itself as the guardian of the party’s ability to hold and use power within the existing system.

#### Why this produces a final score of 45
Mundane sources (institutional effectiveness, pragmatic coalition management) are stronger than in the progressive primary factions. Residual justice language keeps the score from becoming strongly mundane.
