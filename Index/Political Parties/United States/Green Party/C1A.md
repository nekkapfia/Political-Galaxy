# Green Party – Cultural 1A (Foundation)

**Score: 80**

**Final Score: 80**

The Green Party advocates systemic transformation grounded in ecological and social justice. Its core aim is to subordinate present economic preferences and short-term majorities to duties owed to the climate, future generations and non-human nature.

#### Moral Justification
* Ecological justice and duties to future generations and non-human nature are presented as binding moral requirements.
* Present democratic majorities and short-term economic preferences are treated as subordinate.
* Social and economic policy is framed as part of a comprehensive moral project of systemic transformation.
* Scientific evidence is used extensively but functions primarily as diagnostic support for the ethical imperative.

The language is strongly duty-based and future-oriented. Ecological and intergenerational obligations are treated as moral requirements that existing institutions and majorities have no right to ignore. The party presents itself as the political expression of an overriding moral obligation that conventional politics has failed to honour.

#### Why this produces a final score of 80
The transcendent source is primary and organising. Mundane sources are present but clearly subordinate, producing a strong transcendent lean.
