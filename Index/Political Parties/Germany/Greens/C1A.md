# Greens – Cultural 1A (Foundation)

**Score: 76**

**Final Score: 76**

The Greens present ecological breakdown and intergenerational injustice as defining moral crises. Their core aim is systemic transformation to protect the climate, future generations and the natural world.

#### Moral Justification
* Ecological and intergenerational duties are treated as binding moral requirements that can override present majorities and short-term economic preferences.
* Economic arrangements are criticised in moral terms for their destructive impact.
* Scientific evidence is used extensively but functions primarily as support for a prior ethical imperative.
* Progressive social justice claims operate alongside the ecological core.

The language is strongly duty-based and future-oriented. The party presents itself as the political expression of moral obligations that conventional democratic and economic decision-making has failed to honour.

#### Why this produces a final score of 76
The transcendent source is primary and organising. Present democratic will and economic interests are regularly subordinated, producing a strong transcendent lean.
