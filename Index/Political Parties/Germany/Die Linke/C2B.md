# Die Linke – Cultural 2B (Pride / National Identity)

**Score: 19**

Die Linke maintains one of the most consistently critical stances toward traditional German national identity and history. It treats German nationalism and militarism as deeply suspect and prioritises historical guilt and internationalist frameworks.

### Emotional and Cultural Stance – Expanded Analysis

- The party views traditional German national identity as largely illegitimate and associated with aggression and authoritarianism.
- It emphasises atonement, anti-militarism, and the need to move beyond national frameworks.
- Cultural and historical figures are almost never claimed as sources of national pride; when discussed, they are usually universalised or critically contextualised.
- Some East German nostalgia exists, but this is framed as socialist rather than national pride.
- It fully supports the institutional prioritisation of atonement memorials and the absence of traditional national statues.

Die Linke sits at the bottom of the Guilt band. Its stance is one of active de-emphasis and suspicion of traditional German national identity.
