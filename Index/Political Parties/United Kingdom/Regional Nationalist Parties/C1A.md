# Regional Nationalist Parties (SNP + Plaid Cymru) – Cultural 1A (Foundation)

**Score: 59**

The SNP and Plaid Cymru ground their politics in the claim that the Scottish and Welsh peoples constitute distinct national communities with a right to self-determination. Their core aim is to maximise the self-government, and ultimately the independence, of their respective nations.

### Moral Justification

* The Scottish or Welsh people are treated as a distinct political community whose democratic will has independent moral and political standing.
* Self-determination is presented as a fundamental right that can override simple UK-wide majoritarianism.
* Policy is frequently justified by reference to the interests and dignity of the national community.
* Progressive economic and social positions are common but operate inside the prior framework of national self-determination.

The language of both parties is particularist and national. The Scottish and Welsh peoples are treated as moral and political subjects in their own right, possessing a claim to self-determination that is not reducible to their status as regions within the United Kingdom. Progressive policy preferences are real, but they are advanced inside the prior framework of national community rather than as the primary source of authority. Both parties present themselves as the political expression of a people whose distinctive claims cannot be adequately represented through purely UK-wide democratic processes.

### Why this produces a final score of 59
The national community is treated as a moral and political subject whose claims can override UK-wide popular sovereignty. This introduces a clear particularist-transcendent element while remaining distinct from universalist progressive or religious-transcendent patterns.
