# Generic Islamic / Muslim-Interest Independents – Cultural 1A (Foundation)

**Score: 74**

This current consists of independent candidates and local mobilisation networks that prioritise the interests, dignity, and moral claims of the Muslim community, particularly on foreign policy issues such as Gaza and on domestic community concerns.

### Moral Justification

* Positions on Gaza and related foreign-policy issues are frequently justified by reference to Islamic ethical teachings, solidarity with the ummah, and the moral obligation to oppose injustice against Muslims.
* The dignity, safety, and political voice of the Muslim community are treated as primary moral goods.
* Broader British popular sovereignty and secular institutions are accepted as the current operating environment but are secondary to religious-communal sources of authority.
* Justice claims are often framed in religious or communal-moral terms rather than in secular progressive or liberal-universalist language.

The language of this current is religious-communal and particularist. It consistently grounds political legitimacy in the moral and religious obligations of the Muslim community and in solidarity with co-religionists, especially in relation to foreign conflicts. Secular democratic procedures and the wider British demos are treated as the existing institutional environment rather than as the primary source of moral authority. The current presents its candidates as authentic representatives of a distinct moral constituency whose claims arise from religious and communal sources that cannot be reduced to ordinary interest-group politics or to secular progressive justice language.

### Why this produces a final score of 74
The dominant justificatory pattern relies on religious and communal-moral sources that stand independently of the wider non-Muslim demos. This produces a clear transcendent profile, distinct from both secular progressive justice claims and conservative particularist patterns.
