# Restore Britain – Cultural 1A (Foundation)

**Score: 2**

Restore Britain presents itself as a vehicle for democratic revolution to reverse national decline. It argues that Britain has been hollowed out by mass immigration, woke ideology, and institutional failure, and that only a fundamental reset can restore the country’s character, identity, and continuity for future generations.

### Moral Justification

* Generational continuity is framed as a human project of stewardship — thinking “in seasons” and in terms of what is left to those who come after.
* Practical virtues (hard work, responsibility, duty, stewardship) are presented as the defining qualities of “proper Britain”.
* The right of ordinary British citizens to reject the status quo and reclaim their country is treated as fundamental.
* Mass deportation, reversal of demographic change, and rejection of “woke ideology” and radical Islam are justified as necessary to prevent the country becoming “a shell of our former selves”.
* No appeals are made to divine command, sacred texts, natural law, or moral duties that exist independently of the British people as a mortal, historical community.

The language of Restore Britain is earthly, identitarian, and generational. Every major claim is grounded in the lived continuity and self-preservation of the British people as a historical and demographic entity. Generational stewardship, practical virtues, and the democratic right of the current population to reclaim their country are all presented without reference to any higher or sacred authority. Even the strong emphasis on what is left to future generations remains a human, this-worldly project of a specific people rather than a duty owed to an abstract future or to nature itself. The party presents itself as the instrument of a people determined to remain itself across time.

### Why this produces a final score of 2
The transcendent source has zero meaningful presence in moral justification. Every element of the party’s argumentation is grounded exclusively in the lived continuity and self-preservation of the British people as a historical and demographic project. This represents the highest level of practical absence of the transcendent source short of the theoretical extreme.
