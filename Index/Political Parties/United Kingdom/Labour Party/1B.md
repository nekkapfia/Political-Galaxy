# Labour Party – Core 1B (Economic Autonomy)

**Final Score: 59**  
**Regulation Score: 52**  
**Taxation Score: 69**

## Brief Overview

Labour’s Core 1B position combines day-one unfair dismissal rights, strengthened collective bargaining, expanded flexible working, Great British Energy / National Wealth Fund, and a green industrial strategy. The net effect is a modest increase in state coercion relative to the current baseline, producing a final score of 59.

## Expanded Analysis

### Key Policy Movements (Regulation)

- Day-one unfair dismissal rights and restrictions on zero-hours / fire-and-rehire
- Strengthened collective bargaining
- Expanded flexible working and parental leave
- Great British Energy (public ownership) and National Wealth Fund
- Borrowing for green investment
- Planning reform to accelerate housebuilding (with retained oversight)

These movements pull the Regulation Score down to 52.

### Taxation

Taxation Score remains relatively high at 69.

## Why This Produces a Final Score of 59

The labour-market expansions and public-ownership / green-industrial elements increase state influence over contracts, labour allocation, and industrial direction. Limited planning liberalisation does not fully offset this. Average of Regulation 52 and Taxation 69 = 59.
