# Conservative Party – Cultural 4A (Hierarchy)

**Score: 29**

### Conservative Party
**Brief Overview**  
The Conservatives have generally favoured expanding parental choice, maintaining or expanding selective schooling options, and focusing on standards and competition rather than large-scale redistribution of educational resources.

**Impact on Social Determinism – Expanded Analysis**
- Support for grammar school expansion in principle, though limited delivery in office.
- Emphasis on academies and free schools as mechanisms for improvement through autonomy and competition.
- Resistance to policies perceived as penalising private education or high-aspiration families.

This approach tends to accept existing quality gradients while seeking to improve the floor through competition and targeted interventions rather than fundamentally narrowing birth-based differences in pathway quality.

**Why This Produces a Final Score of 29**  
The party’s record and platform lean toward preserving or modestly increasing mechanisms that allow higher-resource families to secure better educational environments, resulting in a slightly higher level of social determinism than the current average.
