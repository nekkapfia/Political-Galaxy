# Conservative Party – Cultural 5A (Tradition vs Progress)

**Score: 52**

### Conservative Party
**Brief Overview**  
The Conservative Party has historically combined rhetorical commitment to tradition with significant modernisation in government. Recent platforms emphasise heritage, British history in education, family stability, and sovereignty while accepting many post-1960s institutional changes.

**Observable Patterns of Tradition Preservation versus Modernisation and Replacement – Expanded Analysis**
- Platforms and recent manifestos call for strengthened British history teaching, heritage protection, and “levelling up” of traditional communities and places.
- In government (2010–2024) the party delivered Brexit (sovereignty restoration) and some curriculum strengthening but left progressive education and media frameworks largely intact.
- Family policy rhetoric supports traditional structures but legislative record shows continuity with earlier liberal frameworks.
- Heritage and conservation policy receives consistent rhetorical and some legislative support (listed buildings, high streets).
- Internal contestation exists between traditionalist and one-nation/modernising wings.

**Why This Produces a Final Score of 52**  
The Conservative Party operates in the low-to-mid 50s because it combines observable preservationist commitments (Brexit delivery, heritage rhetoric, some educational pushback) with acceptance of the broader post-1960s modernisation settlement in law, education, and culture. It does not pursue systematic reversal of progressive transmission mechanisms when in power, resulting in a contested rather than high-preservation score.
