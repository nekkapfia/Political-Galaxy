# Conservative Party – Cultural 5B (Gradualism vs Radicalism)

**Score: 62**

### Conservative Party

**Brief Overview**  
The Conservative Party has governed for most of the period since 2010 and has demonstrated a pragmatic, selective approach to the pace of change — combining accelerated delivery in areas such as Brexit and fiscal crisis response with more incremental or constrained approaches in domestic structural reform.

**Observable Patterns of Pace and Intensity of Change – Expanded Analysis**
- Supported and implemented compressed timelines for Brexit delivery (2019–2020) and COVID emergency measures.
- Austerity and welfare reforms (2010–2016) used relatively fast fiscal mechanisms but faced significant implementation delays and judicial constraints on structural elements.
- Education policy continued academisation at pace in some periods while other reforms encountered extended review.
- Recent leadership has emphasised both "bold action" in selected areas and the need for stability and careful sequencing after periods of high disruption.
- Internal party culture contains both radical accelerationist and traditional gradualist factions, producing inconsistent enforcement of speed across domains.

**Why This Produces a Final Score of 62**  
The Conservative Party’s record and platform show consistent willingness to use accelerated and disruptive mechanisms when politically prioritised (Brexit, emergency response), but also frequent accommodation of gradualist constraints and internal resistance to sustained high-intensity change across all domains. This places it in the mid-to-mid-high range, above pure gradualism but well below the sustained radical acceleration of the Thatcher period or the most disruption-focused current actors.
