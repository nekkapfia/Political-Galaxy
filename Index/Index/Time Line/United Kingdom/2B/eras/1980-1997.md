**Summary:**  
The 1990–1991 Gulf War was fought to reverse Iraq’s invasion and occupation of Kuwait. While the overall campaign involved offensive military operations against Iraq, the core objective was the defence and liberation of Kuwait at the request of its legitimate government. The UK played a significant role in the international coalition, contributing military forces, diplomatic leadership, and support for economic sanctions. The operation was time-limited, UN-authorised, and did not involve regime change in Iraq or long-term occupation. Due to its defensive nature and the fact that it was conducted in support of the defender at their request, the event has been treated with heavy mitigating factors. As a result, the final score for this event is **81**.
