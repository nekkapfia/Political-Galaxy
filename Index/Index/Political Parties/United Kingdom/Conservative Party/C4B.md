# Conservative Party – Cultural 4B (Equity Lens)

**Score: 48**

## Brief Overview

The Conservative Party’s Cultural 4B position centres on support for the core anti-discrimination architecture of the Equality Act 2010 and the Public Sector Equality Duty, combined with explicit resistance to outcome-engineering, hard quotas, and the primacy of systemic-oppression explanations. The resulting score of 48 reflects a mid-range stance that retains procedural equity obligations while prioritising individual agency, socio-economic factors, and colour-blind merit.

## Expanded Analysis

### Relevant Policy and Institutional Stance

- Retention and defence of the Equality Act 2010 framework and Public Sector Equality Duty.
- Commissioning and endorsement of the Sewell Report (2021), which challenged the primacy of systemic racism narratives and emphasised progress data, family structure, culture, and individual agency.
- Opposition to identity-based preferential treatment beyond the narrow positive-action provisions already permitted in law.
- Criticism of expansive DEI bureaucracies, unconscious-bias training mandates, and outcome targets that treat statistical disparities as automatic evidence of systemic rigging.
- Emphasis on economic growth, skills, and socio-economic mobility as the primary routes to reducing group disparities.

The score is produced under the system’s empirical rules: observable institutional practice and formal policy take precedence over rhetoric. The continued existence of PSED reporting duties and residual diversity practices prevents a lower score, while the explicit contestation of equity primacy and rejection of hard quotas prevent a higher score.

## Why This Produces a Final Score of 48

1. The Equality Act / PSED baseline is retained and therefore scored as an active equity mechanism (preventing scores in the low 20s).
2. Active political and intellectual push-back against systemic-oppression primacy (Sewell Report and subsequent positioning) pulls the score downward from the peak-DEI 70s range occupied by parties that treat disparities as primarily structural.
3. No credit is given for colour-blind rhetoric alone; the mechanical weight of retained legal duties sets the floor, while the resistance to further equity expansion sets the ceiling, producing the observed 48.

This placement is consistent with the broader UK trajectory after the 2021 contestation phase and with the party’s relative position versus Labour (65), Liberal Democrats (62), Green (78), Reform UK (22) and Restore Britain (25).
