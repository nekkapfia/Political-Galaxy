# Reform UK – Core 1A (Personal Autonomy)

**Score: 78**

## Brief Overview

Reform UK’s Core 1A position is defined by explicit commitment to repeal of the Online Safety Act and substantial narrowing of non-violent hate-speech and public-order speech offences. The score of 78 is the highest among major UK parties and reflects a clear reduction in the volume and scope of systemic restrictions on personal autonomy relative to the current baseline.

## Expanded Analysis

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

- Explicit policy of repealing the Online Safety Act 2023.
- Commitment to replace broad communications offences with narrower, more tightly defined provisions focused on genuine threats and incitement.
- Rejection of institutional speech codes and expansion of non-crime hate incident recording.
- Opposition to new identity-based hostility definitions and election-period speech controls.
- Support for adult free expression while retaining narrowly tailored child-protection measures.

Under the system’s negative-only weighting, the proposed reductions are scored as the removal of existing restrictive elements. No positive credit is given for rhetoric; the score is produced by the mechanical reduction in the weight of the remaining restrictions.

## Why This Produces a Final Score of 78

1. The Online Safety Act and associated broad speech offences constitute a substantial portion of the modern UK restriction baseline. Their proposed removal produces a large upward movement.
2. Residual public-order and narrowly defined offences still prevent scores in the high 80s–90s.
3. The absence of the progressive identity package (EDI mandates, self-ID institutional effects, expanded hostility definitions) that depresses Labour/Green/Lib Dem scores is decisive.

The result is a clear outlier position within the contemporary UK party distribution.
