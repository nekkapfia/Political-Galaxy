## **2005–2013: Hardliner Reassertion – Final Score: 74**

The Ahmadinejad presidency and the reassertion of more confrontational ideological language marked a return toward higher transcendent intensity, especially in justice, anti-imperialism, and the defence of the system against internal and external enemies.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Justice for the oppressed, resistance to arrogance, and the defence of the Islamic system regained prominence.
* Electoral controversy (2009) and subsequent repression were justified as defence of the Revolution and the Leader’s authority.
* Nuclear policy and foreign defiance were framed in terms of national dignity and resistance as moral duties.
* Clerical and revolutionary legitimacy were reasserted against reformist and popular-protest challenges.

The transcendent source strengthened relative to the reformist period. Popular consent was treated as secondary when it conflicted with the system’s ideological red lines.

**Key Shift:** This era reversed much of the reformist opening and restored a more confrontational ideological posture.

### Why this produces a final score of 74
Transcendent sources regained clear primacy. The score reflects a partial return toward revolutionary-era intensity within a more institutionalised system.
