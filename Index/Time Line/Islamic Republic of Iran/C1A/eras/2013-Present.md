## **2013–Present: Mixed Pragmatism and Enduring Theocracy – Final Score: 69**

The Rouhani and subsequent periods have combined pragmatic engagement on economic and diplomatic issues with the unchanged theocratic core and periodic reassertion of ideological control, especially after popular protests.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* *Wilayat al-Faqih* and the Islamic character of the state remain the non-negotiable foundation.
* Economic performance, sanctions relief, and pragmatic diplomacy have been important legitimating themes at times.
* Popular protests have been met with a mixture of limited concession and forceful reassertion of the system’s religious-political red lines.
* Resistance, anti-imperialism, and the defence of the oppressed continue to supply moral language, especially in regional policy.

The profile remains mixed but still clearly on the transcendent side of the spectrum. The theocratic core has not been relinquished.

**Key Shift:** This era has shown the regime’s capacity to alternate between pragmatic and ideological registers without abandoning the ultimate authority of divine law and the Guardian Jurist.

### Why this produces a final score of 69
Transcendent sources (divine law, clerical guardianship) remain primary and constitutionally entrenched. Mundane performance and limited popular input influence policy but do not displace the higher religious-juridical framework.
