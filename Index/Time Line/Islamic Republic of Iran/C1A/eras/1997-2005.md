## **1997–2005: Reformist Opening – Final Score: 58**

The election of Mohammad Khatami brought a significant, though limited, elevation of popular participation, civil society, rule of law, and dialogue. The theocratic structure was not dismantled, but the relative weight of consent and legal-rational language increased.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Popular participation, electoral legitimacy, and the rights of the people within an Islamic framework were given greater emphasis.
* Rule of law, civil society, and dialogue among civilizations introduced more procedural and consent-oriented language.
* The Supreme Leader and the Guardian Council continued to exercise decisive oversight; reform remained bounded.
* Religious and revolutionary foundations were still affirmed by all major actors.

Mundane and consent-based sources gained substantial ground within a still-theocratic system, producing the lowest transcendent intensity of the Islamic Republic to that point.

**Key Shift:** This era demonstrated the maximum scope for popular and legal-rational justification inside the *Wilayat al-Faqih* framework.

### Why this produces a final score of 58
Transcendent sources (divine law and clerical guardianship) remained constitutionally primary, but popular participation and rule-of-law language acquired real weight, producing a mixed profile.
