## **1989–1997: Post-Khomeini Reconstruction – Final Score: 71**

After Khomeini’s death the regime under Khamenei and Rafsanjani emphasised reconstruction, state-building, and economic recovery while preserving the theocratic core.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* *Wilayat al-Faqih* and the Islamic character of the state remained non-negotiable.
* Reconstruction, economic development, and the normalisation of state institutions gained greater justificatory weight.
* Revolutionary mobilisation language was partially de-emphasised in favour of expertise and pragmatism.
* Popular participation continued within the controlled electoral and clerical oversight system.

The transcendent core persisted, but mundane performance and state-consolidation considerations became more prominent.

**Key Shift:** This era reduced revolutionary intensity while locking in the institutional theocratic framework.

### Why this produces a final score of 71
Transcendent sources remained primary. Increased weight on reconstruction and pragmatism produced a moderate reduction from the revolutionary peak.
