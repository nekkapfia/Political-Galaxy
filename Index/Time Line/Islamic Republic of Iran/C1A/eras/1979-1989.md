## **1979–1989: Revolution, War, and Khomeini’s Leadership – Final Score: 83**

The Islamic Revolution and the eight-year war with Iraq established the core justificatory framework. Divine law, the authority of the jurist, and the moral drama of resistance against internal hypocrites and external arrogance were primary.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Sovereignty belongs to God; human law is valid only insofar as it conforms to Islamic criteria as interpreted by the qualified jurist.
* *Wilayat al-Faqih* located authoritative guardianship in Khomeini and the clerical system, standing above ordinary popular will.
* The Revolution itself was framed as a moral and religious uprising against tyranny, corruption, and foreign domination.
* The war with Iraq was presented as an imposed struggle and a test of faith; martyrdom and sacrifice carried high moral value.
* Popular mobilisation was real and intense, yet it was orchestrated and interpreted within the religious-revolutionary framework rather than as independent popular sovereignty.

The transcendent source was primary and highly mobilising. Mundane considerations of state survival and popular support were important but framed through religious duty and revolutionary justice.

**Key Shift:** This era institutionalised divine law and clerical guardianship as the ultimate sources of political legitimacy.

### Why this produces a final score of 83
Transcendent sources (divine law, Wilayat al-Faqih, revolutionary religious duty) were dominant and organising. The score reflects high ideological intensity during the founding and wartime period.
