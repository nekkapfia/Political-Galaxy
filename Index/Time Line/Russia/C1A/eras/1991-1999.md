## **1991–1999: Yeltsin Transition – Final Score: 38**

The first post-Soviet decade was defined by rapid liberalisation, economic shock therapy, and the search for a new legitimating framework after the collapse of Marxism-Leninism.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

- Market reform and democratisation were justified through a mix of pragmatic necessity (avoiding collapse) and liberal claims about freedom and normalcy.
- Residual Soviet-era language of social protection and great-power status persisted.
- Western models were treated both as practical templates and as moral examples.
- Authority remained fragile; justifications often shifted with political survival needs.

A relatively mixed but unstable profile, with mundane and liberal-individualist sources prominent.

**Key Shift:** This era replaced Marxist-Leninist transcendent ideology with an unstable combination of liberal, market-pragmatic, and residual state-interest justifications.
