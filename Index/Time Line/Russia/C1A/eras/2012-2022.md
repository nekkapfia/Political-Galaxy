## **2012–2022: Conservative Turn and Civilizational Framing – Final Score: 14**

The return of Putin to the presidency and the subsequent years saw stronger emphasis on traditional values, resistance to Western liberalism, and Russia as a distinct civilization.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

- Traditional values, historical continuity, and the defence of Russian civilizational identity became more prominent.
- These were framed primarily as practical and historical goods for the Russian people rather than as transcendent universal commands.
- Sovereignty and resistance to external pressure were justified through the interests of the existing Russian state and population.
- Universalist human-rights language was increasingly rejected as foreign and instrumental.

Mundane sources remained dominant, now reinforced by civilizational and traditional-values rhetoric treated as empirical and historical inheritance.

**Key Shift:** This era added a civilizational and traditional-values layer to an already strongly mundane foundation centred on state and national interest.
