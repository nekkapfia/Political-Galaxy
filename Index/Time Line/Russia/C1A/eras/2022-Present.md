## **2022–Present: Wartime and Existential Framing – Final Score: 11**

The full-scale invasion of Ukraine and subsequent confrontation with the West have intensified justifications based on existential state interest and civilizational survival.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

- Policy is justified overwhelmingly through the existential interests of the Russian state and people.
- Historical continuity, resistance to external domination, and the defence of a distinct way of life are central.
- Universalist and liberal moral claims are treated as hostile instruments.
- Popular support and national resilience are invoked as sources of legitimacy.

Transcendent universalist sources are almost entirely absent. Moral authority rests on state survival, national continuity, and the interests of the existing political community.

**Key Shift:** This era has further intensified the primacy of existential state and national interest, producing one of the strongest mundane profiles in the post-Soviet period.
