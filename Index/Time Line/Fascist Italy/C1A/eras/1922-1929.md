## **1922–1929: Seizure of Power and Early Consolidation – Final Score: 68**

The March on Rome and the early years of the regime established the core Fascist claim that the liberal state had failed and that a new ethical and national order was required. Mussolini still governed within a formally monarchical framework and relied on traditional elites, the Church, and pragmatic appeals to order.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* The liberal parliamentary system was rejected as weak, corrupt, and incapable of expressing the true will of the nation.
* The state was increasingly presented as an ethical entity that stood above individuals and classes.
* National renewal, order, and the restoration of Italian greatness after the “mutilated victory” of the First World War supplied powerful mobilising language.
* The Duce was already positioned as the unique interpreter of the nation’s needs, though the full cult of personality and totalitarian structure were not yet complete.
* Pragmatic appeals to economic recovery, social peace, and the ending of political violence remained important in consolidating support.

The transcendent source (ethical state, national historical mission, leader as interpreter) was already primary, but residual traditional, monarchical, Catholic, and pragmatic elements kept the profile from reaching later intensity.

**Key Shift:** This era installed the ethical-state and national-mission framework while still operating within a hybrid institutional environment.

### Why this produces a final score of 68
Transcendent sources were dominant but not yet totalising. Residual pragmatic and traditional justifications remained visible and functionally important.
