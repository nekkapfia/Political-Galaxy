## **1929–1936: Totalitarian Turn and Imperial Ambition – Final Score: 73**

The Lateran Pacts, the construction of the corporative state, the cult of the Duce, and the turn toward imperial expansion marked a clear radicalisation. The regime sought to penetrate and remake society more thoroughly.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* The state as ethical and spiritual entity was asserted more comprehensively; intermediate institutions and individual rights were further subordinated.
* The cult of the Duce intensified; Mussolini was presented as the embodiment of the nation’s will and historical destiny.
* Corporatism was justified as the ethical organisation of the nation rather than as pure economic pragmatism.
* Imperial expansion (most clearly in Ethiopia) was framed as the recovery of Roman greatness and the historical right of a young, vital nation.
* Anti-liberal and anti-Bolshevik language hardened into a more systematic rejection of both Western democracy and Marxist internationalism.

The transcendent source strengthened. The regime’s self-presentation shifted further from pragmatic order toward spiritual and historical regeneration.

**Key Shift:** This era completed the move from hybrid authoritarian consolidation to a more fully totalitarian and imperial self-understanding.

### Why this produces a final score of 73
Transcendent sources (ethical state, historical destiny, leader cult) became more organising. Mundane and traditional residues declined in relative weight.
