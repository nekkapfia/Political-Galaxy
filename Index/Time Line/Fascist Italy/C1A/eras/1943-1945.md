## **1943–1945: Italian Social Republic – Final Score: 71**

After the fall of Mussolini and the German occupation of the north, the Italian Social Republic (Salo) attempted to revive a more “revolutionary” and anti-monarchical Fascism under German protection. The context of civil war and foreign domination limited its autonomy.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Residual Fascist ideology of the ethical state, anti-liberalism, and national destiny continued to be proclaimed.
* The monarchy and the “betrayal” of 1943 were denounced as proof of the need for a purer Fascist order.
* German power and the continuing war against the Allies and the Italian Resistance framed all practical choices.
* Popular consent and ordinary welfare were largely irrelevant; survival of the Fascist remnant and loyalty to the alliance dominated.

The transcendent language of Fascism persisted but was hollowed out by military defeat, foreign control, and civil war. The regime’s ability to generate independent moral authority collapsed.

**Key Shift:** This final phase preserved Fascist ideological claims while revealing their dependence on power and their inability to survive military catastrophe.

### Why this produces a final score of 71
Transcendent Fascist sources remained formally primary, but the extreme circumstances and loss of autonomy reduced the regime’s capacity to function as an independent justificatory system. The score reflects residual ideological intensity under conditions of collapse.
