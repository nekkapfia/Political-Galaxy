## **1936–1943: Axis Alignment and Wartime Radicalisation – Final Score: 77**

Alliance with Nazi Germany, the introduction of racial laws, and entry into the Second World War pushed Fascist justificatory language to its highest intensity. The regime increasingly adopted a more biological and civilizational framing while remaining distinct from National Socialism.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Historical destiny and the mission of the Italian nation were asserted with greater urgency in the context of European war.
* Racial legislation (1938 onward) introduced a biological element previously less central to Italian Fascism, justified as defence of the nation’s integrity.
* The war was framed as a struggle against the decadent democratic and plutocratic powers and for a new European order.
* The ethical state and the Duce’s authority continued to override ordinary legal and popular constraints.
* Performance and military success were interpreted as confirmation of the regime’s historical correctness.

The transcendent source reached its peak within the Fascist period. The regime’s dependence on German power and the military disasters that followed began to erode practical legitimacy, but the official justificatory language remained highly ideological.

**Key Shift:** This era brought Fascist Italy closest to a full transcendent mobilisation of historical destiny, racial integrity, and anti-liberal mission.

### Why this produces a final score of 77
Transcendent sources were primary and organising across foreign policy, racial legislation, and wartime mobilisation. The score remains below the Nazi extreme because Italian Fascism never achieved the same degree of biological totalisation or eliminationist consistency.
