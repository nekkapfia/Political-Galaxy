## **1985–1991: Gorbachev and Collapse – Final Score: 33**

Glasnost, perestroika, and the attempt to revitalise the system through limited democratisation and radical economic reform accelerated the collapse of ideological authority.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Performance, efficiency, and the need to overcome stagnation became central diagnostic and justificatory themes.
* Glasnost elevated truth-telling, historical honesty, and limited public criticism as values, undermining the Party’s monopoly on truth.
* Popular input and electoral mechanisms were introduced, giving practical weight to consent.
* Official ideology was repeatedly revised and ultimately lost coherence as a binding source of authority.
* By the end, the Party’s claim to historical necessity was no longer credible to large parts of the elite or population.

Mundane sources (performance, pragmatism, emerging popular consent) became dominant. The transcendent ideological framework disintegrated.

**Key Shift:** This era marked the terminal crisis of Marxist-Leninist transcendent authority and the rapid rise of pragmatic and consent-based justifications that the system could not contain.

### Why this produces a final score of 33
Mundane sources were clearly primary. The residual formal ideology no longer functioned as an effective transcendent source, producing a strongly mundane profile in the system’s final years.
