## **1917–1928: Revolution, Civil War, and NEP – Final Score: 79**

The Bolshevik seizure of power and the subsequent civil war established Marxism-Leninism as the exclusive source of political truth. The Party claimed to act on the basis of historical science rather than the current preferences of the population.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Historical materialism was treated as scientific law; the Party’s correct line was therefore both true and morally obligatory.
* Class struggle and the dictatorship of the proletariat justified the suppression of “enemy” classes, the Cheka, and the Red Terror.
* War Communism pushed the logic of revolutionary necessity to an extreme; the NEP represented a tactical retreat justified as temporary and instrumental.
* Popular sovereignty in the liberal or even Soviet-democratic sense was subordinated to the Party’s claim to know the objective interests of the working class.
* The future communist society functioned as a transcendent goal that authorised present sacrifices.

The transcendent source was already dominant. The NEP introduced a visible pragmatic layer, but it was explicitly framed as a temporary concession rather than a change in ultimate authority.

**Key Shift:** This era installed Marxism-Leninism as scientific-moral truth and the Party as its exclusive interpreter.

### Why this produces a final score of 79
Transcendent sources (historical materialism, class struggle as moral duty, Party as embodiment of historical necessity) were primary. The NEP’s pragmatism remained secondary and tactical.
