## **1981–1995: Mitterrand Years – Final Score: 48**

François Mitterrand’s long presidency combined socialist justice language with eventual pragmatic economic management and European integration.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

- Early years featured stronger moral language around social justice, equality, and the rights of workers.
- After the 1983 turn, pragmatic economic constraints and European commitments became central.
- Republican and human-rights framing remained available and was used selectively.
- Both justice-oriented and outcome-oriented justifications were employed.

A more mixed profile reappeared.

**Key Shift:** This era temporarily restored greater weight to justice and equality language before returning to pragmatic constraints.
