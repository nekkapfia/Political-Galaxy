## **1945–1958: Fourth Republic Reconstruction – Final Score: 54**

The immediate post-war years and the Fourth Republic were defined by reconstruction, the purge of collaboration, and the reassertion of republican values after the Vichy interlude.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

- Republican principles (liberty, equality, fraternity) and the rejection of Vichy were framed as moral imperatives.
- Anti-fascist and Resistance language carried strong transcendent weight.
- Economic and institutional reconstruction mixed moral claims about national renewal with pragmatic necessity.
- Colonial policy retained elements of civilising-mission language while facing growing practical challenges.

Both republican moral foundations and pragmatic reconstruction needs were actively present.

**Key Shift:** This era re-anchored French political legitimacy in republican moral principles while confronting the practical demands of post-war recovery.
