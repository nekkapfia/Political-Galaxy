## **2005-2009: First Merkel Grand Coalition – Final Score: 72**

The Grand Coalition between CDU/CSU and SPD under Angela Merkel represented a return to cross-party consensus politics after the more conflictual Schröder years.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Very high party discipline within both major parties supported stable coalition governance.
- Major reforms (e.g., retirement age increase, corporate tax reform) were passed with cross-party support.
- Immigration and integration policy remained relatively low-profile compared to later periods.
- The constitutional framework and EU integration continued to enjoy broad elite consensus.

Orthodoxy was relatively high due to the stabilising effect of grand coalition politics and strong party discipline. Tribalism remained moderate, with limited public contestation around core national or European identity questions. The period reinforced institutional continuity.

**Key Shift**: This era shows a modest increase in Orthodoxy compared to 1998–2005 as grand coalition politics reduced ideological confrontation and strengthened institutional stability.
