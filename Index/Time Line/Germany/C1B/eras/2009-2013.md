## **2009-2013: Merkel II (CDU-FDP) – Final Score: 72**

The return to a centre-right coalition between CDU/CSU and FDP saw some liberalising tendencies but also growing internal tensions within the government.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Party discipline remained strong, particularly within the CDU.
- The government pursued some liberal economic policies but faced resistance on nuclear phase-out reversal and later on the Energiewende.
- EU integration and eurozone crisis management became more contested.
- Immigration policy remained relatively stable with limited public debate.

Orthodoxy stayed relatively high due to continued strong party structures. Tribalism increased modestly as eurozone crisis debates introduced more contestation around European integration and German financial responsibility. The period was transitional rather than transformative.

**Key Shift**: This era shows relatively stable Orthodoxy but a slight rise in Tribalism as European economic governance became more politically contested.
