## **2013-2021: Merkel III & IV (Long Grand Coalition) – Final Score: 75**

The long period of grand coalitions from 2013 to 2021 represented the high point of institutional stability and cross-party consensus in the post-1998 era.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Extremely high party discipline and coalition management under Merkel created strong internal closure around centrist governance.
- Major decisions (Energiewende acceleration after Fukushima, 2015 refugee policy, eurozone management) were taken with limited internal party challenge.
- Immigration policy shifted dramatically in 2015–2016, generating significant public and political contestation.
- The Alternative für Deutschland (AfD) emerged as the first significant party to challenge the post-war consensus on immigration and EU integration.

This era showed the highest Orthodoxy of the post-1998 period due to Merkel’s dominant leadership style and the stabilising effect of repeated grand coalitions. Tribalism rose significantly after 2015 as the refugee crisis and the rise of the AfD created the strongest challenge to the established consensus on immigration and national identity since 1998.

**Key Shift**: This era shows the highest Orthodoxy since 1998 alongside a clear rise in Tribalism, driven by the 2015 refugee crisis and the emergence of the AfD as a challenger to the post-war consensus.
