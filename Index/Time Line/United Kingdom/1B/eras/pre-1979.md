# Era: Day Zero – Post-War Consensus (Pre-1979)

**Final Score: 53**  
**Regulation Score: 42**  
**Taxation Score: 66**

## Summary

Extensive state ownership of major industries, strong trade union power, exchange controls, incomes policies, and detailed planning controls produced a low level of voluntary economic autonomy. The taxation burden was moderate by later standards, but the regulatory environment heavily constrained pricing, investment, and labour allocation decisions.

## Key Component Scores (Selected)

- Nationalised Industries: ~34
- Trade Union Power: ~37–38
- Exchange Controls: low autonomy
- Incomes Policies and Planning Controls: significant restrictions

## Lived-Experience Alignment

Firms faced constrained pricing and investment decisions. Trade unions exercised substantial power over labour allocation and workplace rules. Capital controls limited voluntary cross-border activity. Overall, the system imposed high levels of prior restraint on voluntary economic action.
