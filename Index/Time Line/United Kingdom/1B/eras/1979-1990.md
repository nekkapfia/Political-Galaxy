# Era 1: Thatcher Liberalisation (1979–1990)

**Final Score: 64**  
**Regulation Score: 62**  
**Taxation Score: 68**

## Summary

Major privatisation programme, abolition of exchange controls, reduction of trade union power, and deregulation of financial markets produced the highest economic autonomy of the entire post-war period. Taxation also improved modestly through rate reductions and base broadening.

## Key Component Scores (Selected)

- Privatisation: ~69
- Abolition of Exchange Controls: ~75
- Trade Union Reform: significant positive movement
- Financial Deregulation: high autonomy

## Lived-Experience Alignment

Managerial discretion increased substantially. Capital could move freely. Large parts of the economy moved from state ownership to private control. This remains the peak era for Core 1B in Britain.
