# Era 5: COVID Restrictions (2020–early 2022)

**Final Score: 40**  
**Regulation Score: 24**  
**Taxation Score: 62**

## Summary

Extensive state control over production, labour allocation, and consumption. Mandatory business closures, national lockdowns, and the furlough rule prohibiting work directly removed large sections of voluntary economic activity for a sustained period. This is the lowest economic-autonomy era in the post-war record.

## Key Component Scores (Selected)

- Mandatory Business Closures: ~14
- Repeated National Lockdowns and Movement Restrictions: ~15
- Workplace Enforcement – Mandatory Work from Home: ~17
- Furlough Scheme – Prohibition on Working: ~16
- Furlough Scheme – State Wage Subsidy: ~53

## Lived-Experience Alignment

Large parts of the economy were legally prohibited from trading. The rule preventing furloughed employees from working represented direct state prohibition of voluntary labour allocation. This era dominates the low end of the Core 1B scale.
