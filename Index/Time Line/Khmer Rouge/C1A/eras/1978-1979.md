## **1978–1979: Purges, War, and Collapse – Final Score: 88**

Border conflict with Vietnam, internal purges of suspected traitors, and the final Vietnamese invasion occurred while the regime continued to apply its purity logic. The justificatory framework did not moderate; it simply failed under external military power.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Internal purges were justified as the continuing necessity of rooting out contamination and treason within the revolutionary ranks.
* The war with Vietnam was framed in both ideological and national-racial terms.
* Angkar’s claim to total authority and the purity project remained formally intact until the regime’s flight from Phnom Penh.
* Practical military and administrative collapse did not produce a visible turn toward pragmatic or popular-consent justifications.

The transcendent purity logic persisted until the end. The lower score relative to the peak years reflects the regime’s loss of control rather than any internal moderation of its justificatory sources.

**Key Shift:** The final phase demonstrated that the regime’s transcendent framework contained no mechanism for pragmatic self-correction even in the face of total defeat.

### Why this produces a final score of 88
Transcendent sources remained dominant in official and practical reasoning until military collapse. The slight reduction from the peak score reflects the disintegration of the regime’s capacity to implement its vision rather than a change in the vision itself.
