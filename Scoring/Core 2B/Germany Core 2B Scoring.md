## Era Summaries

### Era 1: Post-Cold War Interventionism & EU Leadership (1999–2010) — Score: 58
This era marks Germany’s gradual return to a more active international role after reunification. It is defined by the Kosovo intervention (1999) and the early years of the Afghanistan mission, alongside strong German support for EU enlargement and post-conflict stabilisation in South-Eastern Europe. Germany used its influence within the EU primarily to promote stability and democratic reforms in the Balkans. The score reflects moderate outward engagement that remained relatively restrained.

### Era 2: Selective & Cautious Engagement (2011–2021) — Score: 51
This era is characterised by a more selective approach to direct military engagement following the experiences in Afghanistan and Libya. Germany became increasingly willing to use the European Union as a tool of foreign policy, particularly through sanctions on Russia after 2014 and influence over EU migration and rule of law mechanisms. Diplomatic and economic support for Ukraine grew during this period, though direct military assistance remained limited. The score reflects moderate outward engagement with a clear shift toward EU-based instruments.

### Era 3: EU as Foreign Policy Tool & Proxy Support (2014–2021) — Score: 49
This era saw Germany make more assertive use of the European Union as an instrument of foreign policy. Germany played a leading role in shaping EU sanctions against Russia following the annexation of Crimea and continued to promote liberal democratic governance and rule of law through EU mechanisms. Support for Ukraine increased through diplomatic and economic channels, while direct military engagement remained limited. The score reflects rising outward engagement exercised primarily through multilateral and EU-based tools.

### Era 4: Zeitenwende – High-Intensity Intervention (2022–Present) — Score: 42
This era represents a historic shift in German foreign policy following Russia’s full-scale invasion of Ukraine. Germany significantly increased direct military support to Ukraine and made unprecedented use of the EU to impose extensive economic sanctions on Russia. The period is defined by large-scale military aid, the most comprehensive sanctions regime in EU history, and efforts to reduce energy dependence on Russia. The score reflects a clear and substantial increase in outward engagement and interference compared to previous eras.

## Current Era: Zeitenwende – High-Intensity Intervention (2022–Present)

**Score: 42**

This is the current era. It is characterised by Germany’s dramatic increase in direct military support to Ukraine and its central role in designing and maintaining the largest EU sanctions regime in history against Russia. Germany has actively promoted liberal democratic governance, anti-corruption reforms, and media freedom in Ukraine while using the EU as its primary tool for economic pressure on Russia.

Activity is high across Diplomacy and the EU as a Foreign Policy Tool. Germany has become one of the largest providers of military aid to Ukraine and has played a leading role in coordinating European and international support. Culture and Ideology programming continues at a modest level, focused on supporting democratic resilience and countering Russian disinformation.

The current trajectory suggests sustained high levels of outward engagement through both direct military support and coordinated EU economic measures.

## Scope and Rerouting

### What Core 2B Measures
Core 2B measures the degree to which a nation interferes in the **internal affairs of other states**. It focuses on observable actions that affect the sovereignty, governance, or internal political processes of other countries.

### Key Rerouting Principles
- **Defensive actions**: Military or diplomatic actions taken in direct self-defence or at the explicit request of a legitimate government are treated with significant mitigating credit.
- **Invited interventions**: Actions taken at the request of the legitimate government of another state are viewed as less invasive than uninvited interference.
- **Intra-state vs inter-state**: Actions that occur entirely within a country’s own territory (even during conflict) are generally not counted under Core 2B.
- **Negative-only weighting**: Restraint does not generate positive credit. Only actual increases in interference reduce the score.
- **EU actions**: When Germany uses the European Union to influence other states, these actions are assessed under Core 2B when they affect the internal affairs of other countries. EU-related actions directly tied to a specific event are included in that event’s subsection.

The **AfD** holds the clearest alternative position and would likely produce a significantly higher Core 2B score (less interference abroad). **Die Linke** would also result in a moderately higher score. Among the mainstream parties, differences are relatively modest, with the **Greens** being the most interventionist and the **CDU/CSU** slightly more willing to maintain strong commitments than the current government.
