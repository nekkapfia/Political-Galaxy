# Core 2B Era Scoring Methodology

**Document Purpose**  
This guide documents the methodology used to score eras on the Core 2B (International Sovereignty) slider. It includes a detailed case study of how Era 2 was analysed, followed by a generalised instruction document that can be applied to any era or time period.

---

## 1. Case Study: How Era 2 Was Broken Down and Scored

This section records the actual process used to analyse and score **Era 2: Thatcher/Major Restraint (Mid-1980 – 1997)**.

### Step 1: Identify Major Events and Patterns
- The era was initially defined by one dominant event (the Gulf War, 1990–1991) and a broader pattern of low-to-moderate activity outside of it.
- Other activities included post-Cold War democracy promotion in Eastern Europe, limited sanctions and diplomatic pressure, and modest cultural/ideological engagement.

### Step 2: Break Events into Granular Elements
- The Gulf War was deconstructed into specific actions relevant to Core 2B (e.g. direct military participation, intelligence support, diplomatic pressure, sanctions/embargoes, ideological framing).
- These elements were further split by **location** (actions inside Kuwait vs actions inside Iraq) because Core 2B distinguishes between defensive operations and interference in another nation’s internal affairs.

### Step 3: Score Granular Elements Under Different Assumptions
- Elements were first scored under a **non-defensive / offensive war** framing (removing defensive justification, time-limit credit, and multilateral backing).
- Scores were then adjusted using **mitigating factors**:
  - Heavy mitigation was applied to actions inside Kuwait because the intervention was requested by the legitimate Kuwaiti government.
  - No mitigation was applied to actions inside Iraq.

### Step 4: Group Granular Elements into Broad Categories
- All elements across the era were grouped into three overarching categories:
  - **Diplomacy**
  - **Foreign Aid**
  - **Culture and Ideology**
- A holistic score was calculated for each category.

### Step 5: Determine the Overall Era Score
- The three category scores were combined into a single era score of **68**.
- The Gulf War was treated as a notable outlier. Due to its defensive nature and the fact that it was requested by the defender, heavy mitigating factors were applied (resulting in an event score of 81). However, because it was short relative to the full 17-year era, it was given reduced weight when determining the overall era character.

### Step 6: Apply Standard Formatting
- The final analysis was presented using the agreed structure:
  - Era header with score only
  - Top-level Summary
  - Numbered subsections with descriptive table headers
  - Holistic category scores and summaries
  - Split tables for major events (Defensive vs Offensive operations)

## 2. General Era Scoring Instruction Document

This document provides a repeatable methodology for scoring any era or time period on the Core 2B (International Sovereignty) slider.

### Phase 1: High-Level Mapping
1. Identify the major events, policies, and patterns of behaviour during the era.
2. Determine the dominant character of the era.
3. Note any clear outliers (short but intense events).

### Phase 2: Granular Decomposition (Full Element Review)
1. Review the **complete taxonomy of granular soft power elements** (listed below).
2. For every significant event or policy, identify which elements from the master list were **present and active**.
3. Explicitly note which elements from the master list were **absent**. Absence is analytically relevant, especially for more invasive forms of soft power.
4. Where relevant, split actions by **location** (e.g. inside the defended territory vs inside the opposing state).
5. Record the time period during which each active element was present.

**Master List of Granular Soft Power Elements**

- Passive Media & Entertainment Exports
- Educational & Academic Exchanges
- General Cultural Institutes & Public Diplomacy
- Diplomatic Ideological Promotion
- Aid-Based Ideological Promotion
- Light Bilateral Diplomatic Pressure
- Strong Multilateral/Coordinated Diplomatic Pressure
- General Economic & Governance Aid Conditionality
- General Political & Human Rights Aid Conditionality
- Strong Economic/Structural Aid Conditionality
- Strong Political/Ideological Aid Conditionality
- Gender & Social Norms Programs
- LGBT-Specific Programs
- Democracy & Governance Programs
- Human Rights Programs
- Large-Scale Systematic Thematic Programs
- Long-Term Institutional Capture Programs
- General Civil Society Capacity Building
- Thematic Civil Society Support
- Support for Opposition Political Groups
- Training & Capacity Building for Activists
- Funding for Advocacy & Campaigning
- Significant Funding for Opposition Movements
- Training & Organisational Support for Unrest
- Coordinated Logistical/Financial Support for Regime Change Efforts

### Phase 3: Scoring Granular Elements
1. Score each **active** granular element initially under a baseline offensive/non-defensive framing.
2. Apply mitigating factors where justified:
   - Heavy mitigation for actions taken in defence of an ally at their explicit request.
   - Moderate mitigation for time-limited or multilateral actions.
   - No mitigation for actions inside another sovereign state without invitation or defensive justification.
3. For elements that were **absent**, record them as “Not present” or “No significant activity”.
4. Document the reasoning for every score and every mitigation (or absence) decision.

### Phase 4: Aggregation into Categories
1. Group active granular elements into the standard broad categories:
   - **Diplomacy**
   - **Foreign Aid**
   - **Culture and Ideology**
2. If an entire category has no active elements, state this clearly (e.g. “No significant Foreign Aid activity during this era”).
3. Calculate a holistic score for each active category, giving appropriate weight to lower-scoring (more invasive) elements when gaps are large.

### Phase 5: Determine the Overall Era Score
1. Combine the category scores into a single era score.
2. Treat short but intense events (outliers) with appropriate weighting.
3. Apply the Negative-Only Weighting rule: only actual increases in interference reduce the score.

### Phase 6: Final Presentation (Standard Format)
Every era must follow this structure:

- **Era Header**: Era name + final score only.
- **Summary** (at the top): Concise overview of the era’s character, category performance, treatment of outliers, and justification for the final score. Mention any major categories or elements that were absent.
- **Numbered Subsections** (1., 2., 3.…):
  - Clear descriptive headings (e.g. “1. Diplomacy”, “2. Foreign Aid”, “3. Culture and Ideology”).
  - Tables with relevant column headers.
  - Holistic category score + short summary paragraph.
  - If a category has no activity, include a short statement instead of a table.
- **Major Events** (as additional numbered subsections when relevant):
  - Split tables with clear headings (e.g. “Defensive Military Operations” and “Offensive Military Operations”).
  - Overall event score with justification for any mitigating factors.
- No separate “Overall Assessment” section at the end — all key analysis belongs in the top Summary.

### Core Principles
- Always review the **full master list** of granular elements.
- Explicitly account for both presence **and absence** of elements.
- Absence of invasive elements is analytically significant and should be noted.
- Maintain negative-only weighting and focus on observable mechanical effect.
- Use consistent formatting across all eras for comparability.
