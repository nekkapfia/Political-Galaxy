# Core 2B Era Scoring Methodology Guide

**Purpose**  
This guide provides the complete methodology for scoring eras on the Core 2B (International Sovereignty) slider at the full depth and quality standard. It ensures consistency, analytical rigour, and sufficient granularity across all documents.

---

## 1. Overall Process

When scoring any era, follow these steps in sequence:

1. High-level mapping of the era
2. Full granular decomposition using the master list
3. Scoring of individual elements
4. Aggregation into categories
5. Identification and separation of key events into dedicated subsections
6. Production of the final document to full depth standard
7. Final quality check and looping

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Methodology

### Step 1: High-Level Mapping
- Identify the dominant character of the era.
- List all major events and patterns of behaviour.
- Note the overall direction of travel (increasing, decreasing, or stable engagement).

### Step 2: Granular Decomposition (Full Element Review)
Review the complete master list of granular soft power elements. For every significant policy or event:

- Identify which elements from the master list were **present and active**.
- Explicitly note which elements were **absent**.
- Record the time period and geographic scope of each active element.
- Distinguish between global activity and activity tied to specific major events.

### Step 3: Scoring Granular Elements
- Score each active element initially under a **baseline offensive/non-defensive** framing.
- Apply mitigating factors where justified:
  - Heavy mitigation for actions taken in direct self-defence or at the explicit request of a legitimate government.
  - Moderate mitigation for time-limited or strongly multilateral actions.
  - No mitigation for uninvited interference in another state’s internal affairs.
- Document the reasoning for every score and every mitigation decision.

### Step 4: Aggregation into Categories
Group active elements into the three standard categories:
- **Diplomacy**
- **Foreign Aid**
- **Culture and Ideology**

Calculate a holistic score for each category. Provide clear, specific notes for each element and write an analytical summary for the category.

### Step 5: Identification and Separation of Key Events
Identify all major discrete events within the era.

**Each key event must have its own dedicated subsection.**  
Move all elements directly related to that event into its subsection and remove all references to the event from the main category sections (1, 2, and 3).

### Step 6: Production of the Final Document
Produce the era document using the following structure:

- Era header with final score only
- Top-level category table
- Short analytical Summary of the era
- Section 1: Diplomacy (global/non-event activity only)
- Section 2: Foreign Aid (global/non-event activity only)
- Section 3: Culture and Ideology (global/non-event activity only)
- Dedicated subsections for each key event (with split tables where appropriate)
- Clear justification for event scores and mitigating factors

### Step 7: Final Quality Check and Looping
After drafting, perform the following checks:

- Have all relevant granular elements from the master list been considered?
- Are the notes specific enough (what, where, how, and why)?
- Have all key events been given their own dedicated subsections?
- Have all event-specific elements been moved out of sections 1–3?
- Does the depth and specificity match the established full quality standard?
- Is the language analytical rather than generic?

Loop through Steps 2–7 until no further meaningful improvements are required.

## 3. Master List of Granular Soft Power Elements

**Diplomatic**
- Strong Multilateral/Coordinated Diplomatic Pressure
- Light Bilateral Diplomatic Pressure
- Diplomatic Ideological Promotion
- Coordination with International Organisations
- Public Diplomacy and Strategic Messaging

**Foreign Aid**
- Aid-Based Ideological Promotion
- General Economic & Governance Aid Conditionality
- General Political & Human Rights Aid Conditionality
- General Civil Society Capacity Building
- Democracy & Governance Programs
- Human Rights Programs
- Large-Scale Systematic Thematic Programs
- Long-Term Institutional Capture Programs
- Thematic Civil Society Support
- Support for Independent Media and Media Freedom

**Culture and Ideology**
- Passive Media & Entertainment Pressure
- Educational & Academic Exchanges
- British Council and Cultural Institutes
- People-to-People and Civil Society Cultural Programmes
- Support for Independent Media and Media Freedom

---

## 4. Standards of Depth and Specificity

All final documents must meet the following standards:

- Every category section must contain **multiple granular elements** with specific notes.
- Notes should explain **what** was done, **where** it occurred, and **why** it matters for Core 2B.
- Summaries must be analytical, not merely descriptive.
- The nature of ideological promotion must be specified (e.g. liberal democratic norms, human rights including LGBT+ rights, anti-corruption, etc.).
- Aid conditionality must be described (what conditions were attached and to whom).
- Event-specific activity must be separated into dedicated subsections.
- The final score for each event and category must be clearly justified.

---

## 5. Key Principles

- **Negative-only weighting** — Restraint does not generate positive credit.
- **Observable mechanical effect** — Score based on what was actually done, not rhetoric or claims.
- **Defensive and invited actions** — Apply significant mitigating credit where justified.
- **Separation of global vs event-specific activity** — Keep category sections (1–3) focused on non-event activity.
- **Dedicated event subsections** — Every major event must have its own section.

---

## 6. Final Quality Checklist

Before finalising any era document, confirm:

- [ ] All relevant granular elements from the master list have been considered
- [ ] Each key event has its own dedicated subsection
- [ ] All event-specific elements have been removed from sections 1–3
- [ ] Notes contain sufficient specificity (what, where, how)
- [ ] Summaries are analytical and explanatory
- [ ] The document meets the full depth and quality standard
- [ ] One final review loop has been completed with no further changes required
