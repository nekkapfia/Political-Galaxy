## 1. Overall Document Structure (Strict Order)

The document must follow this exact order:

1. Opening summary paragraph (no title above it)
2. Table of eras
3. All individual era sections (in chronological order)
4. `# Political Parties` heading
5. Political Parties intro paragraph (with bridging reference to eras)
6. Table of parties
7. All individual party sections

**Important formatting rules**:
- Do **not** put a main title (`# Britain Cultural 1B - Dogmatism`) at the very top.
- Do **not** use `---` horizontal rules between eras or between party sections.
- Use consistent heading style: `## **Era/Party Name – Final Score: XX**` (both the name and score are bolded).

## 2. Creating the Eras Section

### Step 2.1 – Write the Opening Summary
- Write **one paragraph** that gives a high-level overview of how dogmatism evolved over the entire period.
- End the paragraph with a bridging sentence that links to the parties section (e.g., “These long-term patterns... are visible in the contemporary dogmatism scores of [Country]’s main political parties.”).

### Step 2.2 – Create the Eras Table
- Place the table **immediately after** the summary paragraph.
- Columns: `Era | Orthodoxy | Tribalism | **Dogmatism**`
- Bold the era names in the first column.
- Bold the values in the **Dogmatism** column.

### Step 2.3 – Write Individual Era Sections

Each era must follow this exact structure:

```markdown

## **StartYear–EndYear: Era Name – Final Score: XX**

[Short intro paragraph – 2 to 4 sentences]

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis

- Bullet point 1
- Bullet point 2
- Bullet point 3
- Bullet point 4 (optional)

[1–2 analysis paragraphs]

```

Rules for Era Sections:

- Keep eras reasonably concise (noticeably shorter than party sections).
- Every single era must have a **Key Shift**: sentence at the end.
- Focus on observable mechanisms (party discipline, institutional enforcement, treatment of dissent, policy reversals, etc.).
- Use 3–5 bullets maximum.

### Section 3: Creating the Political Parties Section

## 3. Creating the Political Parties Section

### Step 3.1 – Write the Intro Paragraph
- Describe the current state of dogmatism in the country.
- Include a bridging sentence that references the historical eras section.

### Step 3.2 – Create the Parties Table
- Columns: `**Party** | Orthodoxy | Tribalism | **Dogmatism**`
- Bold the party names and the Dogmatism scores.

### Step 3.3 – Write Individual Party Sections

Use the same core structure as the eras, but allow slightly more detail:

```

## **Party Name – Final Score: XX**

[Intro paragraph]

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis

- Bullet points (focus on concrete mechanisms)

[1–2 analysis paragraphs]

**Key Shift**: [One clear sentence explaining how this era differs from the previous one in terms of Orthodoxy and/or Tribalism]
```

##Rules for Party Sections:

- They can be moderately longer than era sections.
- Still include clear analysis of Orthodoxy (internal closure) and Tribalism (external rejection).
- End with a strong concluding insight (no need for a formal “Key Shift” sentence here).

### Section 4: General Rules & Best Practices

## 4. General Rules & Best Practices

- **Tone**: Neutral, empirical, and mechanism-focused. Avoid moral language.
- **Consistency**: The structure of era sections and party sections should feel clearly related but not identical.
- **Length balance**: Eras should generally be shorter and more concise than party sections.
- **Key Shift sentences**: These are mandatory for every era. They help show progression across time.
- **Bridging**: The summary paragraph and the parties intro paragraph should both contain bridging language that connects the historical and contemporary parts of the document.
- **Formatting**: 
  - No `---` between sections.
  - Bold era/party names and final Dogmatism scores.
  - Use the exact heading format shown above.
  
## 5. Recommended Workflow

1. Research and divide the timeline into logical eras.
2. Score all eras (Orthodoxy, Tribalism, final Dogmatism).
3. Write the summary paragraph + table.
4. Write all era sections (including Key Shift sentences).
5. Research and score current political parties.
6. Write the Political Parties intro + table.
7. Write all party sections.
8. Review for consistency (especially Key Shift sentences and formatting).
9. Final read-through for flow between the Eras and Parties sections.


  ## 6. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Starting the document with a main title.
- Putting the table before the summary paragraph.
- Using `---` separators between eras.
- Forgetting **Key Shift** sentences in some eras.
- Making eras as long or longer than party sections.
- Weak or missing bridging language between the two halves of the document.
- Inconsistent heading formatting.
- Parties shoudl be arranged alphabetically in both the table and individual sections
