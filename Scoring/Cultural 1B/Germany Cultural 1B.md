# Germany Cultural 1B - Dogmatism

Over the period from October 1998 to the present, Germany moved from a period of significant policy rupture under the first Red-Green government toward a long phase of grand coalition stability under Angela Merkel, followed by a return to more fragmented coalition politics after 2021. Orthodoxy remained relatively high throughout due to Germany’s strong party discipline and institutional continuity, but it fluctuated with the rise and decline of grand coalitions. Tribalism was generally moderate for most of the period, focused around EU integration and economic orthodoxy, but rose noticeably from the mid-2010s onward as immigration, identity, and energy policy became more contested. The system has generally remained in the Moderate to Predominant dogmatism range, with institutional mechanisms continuing to protect core post-war frameworks while political contestation has increased in recent years. These long-term patterns of institutional stability and gradual contestation are visible in the contemporary dogmatism scores of Germany’s main political parties.

| **2005-2009: First Merkel Grand Coalition** | 78 | 65 | **72** |

## **1998-2005: Schröder Red-Green Era – Final Score: 71**

The period from October 1998 marked a clear break from the long Kohl era, with the first Red-Green coalition under Gerhard Schröder introducing significant policy shifts, particularly in economic and social policy.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Strong party discipline within both SPD and Greens helped maintain internal cohesion despite internal tensions.
- The government pursued major reforms (Agenda 2010 and Hartz reforms) that challenged traditional SPD positions, showing some willingness to revise core principles.
- National identity debates remained relatively contained, with focus on economic modernisation rather than cultural change.
- Opposition to the reforms came mainly from within the SPD and trade unions rather than from strong external moral frameworks.

This era showed moderate Orthodoxy as the government was willing to break with traditional social democratic orthodoxy on labour market and welfare reform. Tribalism remained moderate, with limited contestation around national identity. The period represented a pragmatic rather than ideological rupture with the past.

**Key Shift**: This era shows a moderate decline in Orthodoxy compared to the long Kohl period, as the Red-Green government proved willing to revise traditional social democratic positions on welfare and labour markets.

---

## **2005-2009: First Merkel Grand Coalition – Final Score: 72**

The Grand Coalition between CDU/CSU and SPD under Angela Merkel represented a return to cross-party consensus politics after the more conflictual Schröder years.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Very high party discipline within both major parties supported stable coalition governance.
- Major reforms (e.g., retirement age increase, corporate tax reform) were passed with cross-party support.
- Immigration and integration policy remained relatively low-profile compared to later periods.
- The constitutional framework and EU integration continued to enjoy broad elite consensus.

Orthodoxy was relatively high due to the stabilising effect of grand coalition politics and strong party discipline. Tribalism remained moderate, with limited public contestation around core national or European identity questions. The period reinforced institutional continuity.

**Key Shift**: This era shows a modest increase in Orthodoxy compared to 1998–2005 as grand coalition politics reduced ideological confrontation and strengthened institutional stability.

---

## **2009-2013: Merkel II (CDU-FDP) – Final Score: 72**

The return to a centre-right coalition between CDU/CSU and FDP saw some liberalising tendencies but also growing internal tensions within the government.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Party discipline remained strong, particularly within the CDU.
- The government pursued some liberal economic policies but faced resistance on nuclear phase-out reversal and later on the Energiewende.
- EU integration and eurozone crisis management became more contested.
- Immigration policy remained relatively stable with limited public debate.

Orthodoxy stayed relatively high due to continued strong party structures. Tribalism increased modestly as eurozone crisis debates introduced more contestation around European integration and German financial responsibility. The period was transitional rather than transformative.

**Key Shift**: This era shows relatively stable Orthodoxy but a slight rise in Tribalism as European economic governance became more politically contested.

---

## **2013-2021: Merkel III & IV (Long Grand Coalition) – Final Score: 75**

The long period of grand coalitions from 2013 to 2021 represented the high point of institutional stability and cross-party consensus in the post-1998 era.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Extremely high party discipline and coalition management under Merkel created strong internal closure around centrist governance.
- Major decisions (Energiewende acceleration after Fukushima, 2015 refugee policy, eurozone management) were taken with limited internal party challenge.
- Immigration policy shifted dramatically in 2015–2016, generating significant public and political contestation.
- The Alternative für Deutschland (AfD) emerged as the first significant party to challenge the post-war consensus on immigration and EU integration.

This era showed the highest Orthodoxy of the post-1998 period due to Merkel’s dominant leadership style and the stabilising effect of repeated grand coalitions. Tribalism rose significantly after 2015 as the refugee crisis and the rise of the AfD created the strongest challenge to the established consensus on immigration and national identity since 1998.

**Key Shift**: This era shows the highest Orthodoxy since 1998 alongside a clear rise in Tribalism, driven by the 2015 refugee crisis and the emergence of the AfD as a challenger to the post-war consensus.

---

## **2021-present: Scholz Coalition and Aftermath – Final Score: 74**

The period since 2021 has seen a return to three-party coalition politics and increased fragmentation, alongside ongoing challenges to the previous consensus on energy, immigration, and economic policy.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- The “Traffic Light” coalition (SPD-Green-FDP) experienced significant internal tensions, weakening traditional party discipline.
- Major policy reversals occurred on nuclear power and fiscal rules.
- Immigration and asylum policy became highly contested again.
- The rise of the AfD and the emergence of the Sahra Wagenknecht Alliance (BSW) increased political fragmentation.
- Institutional mechanisms continued to protect core post-war frameworks (EU integration, constitutional order, anti-extremism).

Orthodoxy has declined somewhat due to coalition instability and internal government conflicts. Tribalism remains elevated as debates over immigration, energy security, and national identity have become more polarised. The period marks a shift away from the long period of grand coalition stability.

**Key Shift**: This era shows a decline in Orthodoxy compared to the Merkel grand coalition years due to coalition instability, while Tribalism has remained high amid renewed contestation over immigration, energy policy, and national direction.

# Political Parties

Modern Germany exhibits moderate to strong dogmatism in its institutional and political spheres. Core post-war frameworks (EU integration, constitutional order, and rejection of extremism) remain strongly protected by institutions, parties, and large parts of the media. However, the rise of the AfD and increased political fragmentation since 2015 have created more open contestation around immigration, national identity, and economic policy than at any point since 1998. The overall state is Moderate to Predominant dogmatism, with strong institutional Orthodoxy offset by growing political pluralism and populist challenge. These patterns reflect the longer historical trajectory of institutional stability combined with rising contestation since the mid-2010s.

## AfD – Final Score: 84

The AfD maintains very high internal discipline around its nationalist and anti-immigration positions.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Strong internal discipline and rejection of deviation from the party line.
- Clear and consistent rejection of the post-2015 immigration consensus and much of the established party system.
- Strong emphasis on national identity and opposition to supranational (especially EU) frameworks.
- The party has undergone several internal purges to maintain ideological coherence.

The AfD shows very high Orthodoxy and very high Tribalism. It maintains strict internal control and strongly rejects the dominant post-war and post-2015 consensus on immigration, EU integration, and national identity.

---

## CDU/CSU – Final Score: 75

The CDU/CSU maintains relatively high internal discipline and a strong commitment to its traditional principles of economic stability, EU integration, and moderate conservatism.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Strong party discipline and leadership structures limit internal deviation.
- The party has shown willingness to revise positions (e.g., on nuclear power and same-sex marriage) when politically necessary.
- It maintains clear rejection of cooperation with the AfD.
- It defends core post-war frameworks (EU integration, fiscal responsibility, constitutional order).

The CDU/CSU combines relatively high Orthodoxy with moderate Tribalism. It protects its core principles internally while rejecting cooperation with the far right. Its Tribalism is focused more on institutional stability than on strong cultural nationalism.

---

## Die Linke – Final Score: 71

Die Linke maintains moderate Orthodoxy and Tribalism, with a focus on traditional left-wing positions.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Internal divisions between reformist and radical wings have reduced discipline.
- The party maintains clear opposition to NATO, militarisation, and neoliberal economics.
- It rejects cooperation with the AfD.
- It promotes a strongly internationalist and anti-nationalist position.

Die Linke shows moderate Orthodoxy due to internal divisions. Its Tribalism is focused on rejecting neoliberalism, militarism, and nationalism, while maintaining distance from the AfD.

---

## FDP – Final Score: 66

The FDP maintains lower Orthodoxy and Tribalism than the larger parties, with a more pragmatic and liberal approach.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Relatively open internal debate on economic and civil liberties issues.
- The party has frequently changed coalition partners.
- It maintains classical liberal positions but shows limited strong rejection of other frameworks.
- It supports EU integration and market economics without strong cultural nationalism.

The FDP shows lower Orthodoxy and Tribalism than most other parties. It is more open to internal revision and less strongly rejects alternative moral frameworks compared to the CDU, SPD, or Greens.

---

## Greens – Final Score: 75

The Greens maintain relatively high internal discipline around their core environmental and progressive values.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Strong internal culture around climate, gender, and social issues.
- The party has moderated some positions in government (e.g., on nuclear power and debt brake).
- It strongly rejects cooperation with the AfD and maintains clear progressive boundaries.
- It promotes a cosmopolitan and ecological vision of German identity.

The Greens show relatively high Orthodoxy around their core ideological positions. Their Tribalism is high in rejecting nationalist and conservative alternatives, particularly on climate, migration, and identity issues.

---

## SPD – Final Score: 71

The SPD has experienced declining Orthodoxy in recent decades as it has struggled with internal divisions between traditionalists and modernisers.

### Mechanisms of Protection and Rejection – Expanded Analysis
- Internal factionalism between left and centre has reduced party discipline.
- The party has revised traditional positions significantly (Agenda 2010, Hartz reforms).
- It maintains strong rejection of cooperation with the AfD.
- It supports progressive positions on social issues and climate policy.

The SPD shows moderate Orthodoxy due to internal divisions and a history of major policy reversals. Its Tribalism is moderate, focused on defending social democratic and progressive values while rejecting the far right.
