**Long-Term Trend (Mechanical Reading)**  
The highest level of economic autonomy occurred during **Era 2** following the Hartz Reforms. From 2011 onwards, the progressive intensification of the Energiewende and green industrial policy caused a significant decline in economic autonomy. Era 5 (COVID Restrictions) represents the lowest point in the sequence. The post-2022 period has seen only a partial recovery, with economic autonomy remaining well below pre-2011 levels due to continued state direction through green and industrial policy.

### 5. Routing & Exclusion Summary — Germany Core 1B

**Routing Principles**  
All elements were assessed according to **Scope Purity** and **dominant mechanical effect**. Only the primary effect determines routing.

**Routing Decisions**

**Routed to Core 1A (Personal Autonomy)**  
- Individual-level impacts of strict firearms licensing rules (particularly subjective “unreliability” criteria and the exclusion of self-defence as a legitimate need)  
- Certain personal security and daily decision-making aspects of residence allocation rules for non-citizens  
- Individual-level conditionality and sanctions attached to integration requirements for foreigners

**Routed to Core 2A (National Sovereignty)**  
- Aspects of immigration and residence law that primarily concern national control over borders and membership (e.g. general rules on who may enter and remain in the territory)  
- Historical elements of capital and exchange controls where the dominant concern was national authority over external financial flows

**Retained in Core 1B (Voluntary Economic Action)**  
The vast majority of policies across all eras remained in Core 1B because their dominant mechanical effect was on contracts, property rights, production, trade, investment, pricing, or labour allocation between economic actors. This includes most labour market regulation, planning rules, environmental and energy regulation, industrial policy, and general business regulation.

**Note on Taxation**  
All pure taxation measures (rates, thresholds, reliefs, enforcement, and wealth/income tax design) were removed from Regulation scoring and are handled exclusively through the separate Taxation Score.

**Exclusions**  
- Purely rhetorical or symbolic statements with no observable mechanical effect on voluntary economic action  
- Purely administrative or procedural changes with negligible impact on economic decisions  
- Temporary crisis measures (such as certain COVID-era interventions) whose long-term mechanical weight was significantly reduced (these were given reduced weighting rather than full exclusion)

**Summary of Routing Approach**

| Destination     | When Applied                                      | Examples |
|-----------------|---------------------------------------------------|----------|
| **Core 1B**     | Dominant effect on voluntary economic action (contracts, investment, production, allocation) | Most labour market rules, planning law, environmental/energy regulation, industrial policy, general business regulation, firearms licensing (economic components) |
| **Core 1A**     | Dominant effect on individual personal autonomy or security | Subjective firearms licensing criteria, personal aspects of residence allocation for non-citizens, integration conditionality with personal sanctions |
| **Core 2A**     | Dominant concern is national sovereignty or external authority | Core immigration and residence entitlement rules, historical capital/exchange controls |

All routing decisions were made on the basis of observable mechanical effect rather than policy framing, intent, or political justification.
