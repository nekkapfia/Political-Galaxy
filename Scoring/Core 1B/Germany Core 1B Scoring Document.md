**Long-Term Trend (Mechanical Reading)**  
The highest level of economic autonomy occurred during **Era 2** following the Hartz Reforms. From 2011 onwards, the progressive intensification of the Energiewende and green industrial policy caused a significant decline in economic autonomy. Era 5 (COVID Restrictions) represents the lowest point in the sequence. The post-2022 period has seen only a partial recovery, with economic autonomy remaining well below pre-2011 levels due to continued state direction through green and industrial policy.

### 3. Policies Active by Era

## Pre-Oct 1998 Baseline (Pre-1998)

**Regulation Score: 50**  
**Taxation Score: 61**  
**Final Score: 55**

Germany operated with significant structural constraints on voluntary economic action, particularly through strong co-determination, strict dismissal protection, and extensive state influence in key sectors.

**Key Component Scores (Selected)**

| Policy Element                              | Score | Justification |
|---------------------------------------------|-------|---------------|
| Nationalised Industries & Sectoral Steering | **47** | Significant state direction over investment and production. |
| Co-determination & Labour Market Regulation | **53** | Substantial constraints on voluntary workforce decisions. |
| Dismissal Protection (Kündigungsschutz)     | **56** | Strong procedural and substantive restrictions. |
| Agricultural Policy & CAP Implementation    | **52** | Significant steering of production and land use. |
| Banking & Financial Regulation              | **63** | Detailed rules constraining voluntary capital allocation. |
| Competition Policy (GWB)                    | **66** | Structures market behaviour while allowing substantial voluntary activity. |
| IP / Patent Protection & Enforcement        | **74** | Supports voluntary commercialisation with limited ongoing interference. |
| Rent / Tenancy Regulations                  | **60** | Reduces contractual freedom in the housing market. |
| Compulsory Acquisition & Land-Use Planning  | **58** | Creates prior restraint on voluntary property development. |

**Lived-Experience Alignment (Pre-1998)**  
Firms faced notable constraints on workforce flexibility and investment decisions due to strong worker representation and employment protection.

### 4. Routing & Exclusion Summary — Germany Core 1B

**Routing Principles**  
All policies were assessed according to **Scope Purity** and **dominant mechanical effect**.

- All scored elements are routed to **Core 1B**.
- Pure taxation measures have been removed from Regulation scoring and are handled exclusively through the separate Taxation Score.
- Temporary crisis measures were given appropriate weighting based on their mechanical impact while active.
