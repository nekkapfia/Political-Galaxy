### 1. Rule & Methodology (Locked)

**Core 1B Definition**  
Measures the observable degree to which voluntary economic actions (contracts, property rights, production, trade, investment, consumption) are protected from or restricted by state or collective coercion.

**Key Locked Rules**  
- Negative-Only Weighting  
- Element Rule (personal impacts → Core 1A)  
- Scope Purity (dominant mechanical effect decides placement)  
- Core 1B vs Core 1A: Pure economic reorganisation and labour allocation stay in Core 1B  
- Core 1B vs Core 2A: National border/external authority and protection from external forces → Core 2A

### 3. Policies Active by Era

#### Day Zero: Pre-1979 (Baseline) – Strict Rules-Based Scoring

**Neutered Base Policy Descriptions**

**Nationalised Industries**  
Large sections of the UK economy were under direct state ownership and operational control. The government set prices, directed investment, and influenced output and employment levels.

**Trade Union Power, Closed Shops & Secondary Action**  
Trade unions enjoyed strong legal protections, including closed shops and the right to secondary industrial action, with significant statutory rights to recognition and collective bargaining.

**Exchange Controls & Capital Controls**  
Strict legal restrictions existed on the movement of capital, foreign currency transactions, and overseas investment.

**High Marginal Income Tax Rates**  
The income tax system featured very high marginal rates (up to 83% + surcharge), creating substantial fiscal extraction and disincentives.

**Rent Controls & Security of Tenure**  
The Rent Act framework imposed statutory rent controls and strong security of tenure.

**Incomes Policies & Price Controls**  
Governments imposed statutory or quasi-statutory limits on wage and price increases.

**Town & Country Planning Act + Compulsory Purchase**  
Development was subject to comprehensive planning permission with extensive compulsory purchase powers.

**Agricultural Support & Production Steering**  
Agriculture operated under price supports, production grants, and state interventions.

**Health and Safety at Work Act 1974**  
Employers had general statutory duties to ensure health and safety, enforced through criminal sanctions.

**Unfair Dismissal & Employment Protection**  
Employees had the statutory right not to be unfairly dismissed with remedies through tribunals.

**Competition Policy**  
Merger control and monopoly investigations were conducted under the Fair Trading Act 1973 framework.

**Banking & Financial Regulation**  
Banks and building societies operated under prudential supervision and conduct regulation.

**Public Procurement & National Preference**  
Government purchasing followed formal tendering with preference for domestic or nationalised suppliers.

**IP / Patent & Copyright Protection**  
Legal rights existed to protect inventions and creative works with enforcement mechanisms.

**Corporation Tax & Business Taxation**  
Companies were subject to corporation tax with various allowances and interactions with personal taxation.
