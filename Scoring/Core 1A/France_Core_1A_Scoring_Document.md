## 1. Rule & Methodology Changes (Authoritative & Locked)

### Core Rules Applied (Locked)
- **Direct Restriction Only**: Only policies that directly prohibit or compel individual voluntary actions (movement, association, expression, bodily/medical autonomy, etc.) are scored under Core 1A.
- **Negative-Only Weighting (Locked Rule)**: Scores reflect only the presence and weight of invasive/restrictive policies. Removal or narrowing of restrictions grants **no positive credit** above the baseline. Scores only rise when a previously scored restriction is reduced or removed.
- **Punishment vs Restriction (Locked Rule)**: Criminal penalties, fines, and sentences following convictions are treated as punishment and generally excluded. Only the underlying substantive restriction is scored.
- **Chilling Effects (Locked — Permanent Rule)**: Chilling effects, self-censorship, and behavioural changes resulting from fear are **not scored**. These are consequences of restrictions, not restrictions themselves.
- **Era Definition (Locked)**: An era is defined as a period in which the Base Score fluctuates by at most 0.5 points. Significant movement triggers a new era.
- **Long-standing Laws (Locked)**: Stable restrictions (e.g., hate speech laws under the 1881 Press Law and subsequent expansions) are factored into the baseline from when they became meaningfully active, with adjustments for changes in scope or enforcement intensity.
- **Hate Speech Laws Treatment (Locked)**: Retroactively integrated as a persistent restriction on expression from 1945 onward. Different weightings applied across eras based on function (narrow application vs. strong ideological enforcement) and enforcement intensity.
- **COVID Protocol (Locked)**: Treated as a single block using the established UK/Germany COVID methodology. Temporary nature provides only modest upward weighting in holistic scoring.
- **No Positive Buffs for Repeal (Locked)**: Repealing or opposing previous restrictions is never scored as a positive factor. Only active, maintained invasive policies lower the score.

**Protective Baseline**: High protective laws (murder, rape, assault, etc.) establish a strong starting point (~95–96 for core violent crime prohibitions), from which restrictive policies are subtracted.

**Boundary Application (explicit decisions)**:

**Rule References**: Direct Restriction Only.

**Final Era Score Calculation**: Started from previous era baseline, significant downward pressure from new expression suppression measures, with ongoing moderate hate speech and paternalistic age of consent restrictions → **64**.

**Neutered Base Policy**: Major expansion of hate speech laws (Pleven Law) broadening protections and increasing scope of prohibited expression, particularly targeting the far-right.
