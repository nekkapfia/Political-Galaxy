## 1. The Core 1A Slider – Bands & Mechanical Meaning

The slider measures **systemic restrictions on personal autonomy** (0 = total state dominion over body/mind/actions; 100 = complete absence of any laws, including no protection against harm).

**Bands:**

- **0–9**: Total dominion / physiological mind-body control  
- **10–18**: Extreme restrictions on movement / association / family / residence (curfews, forced labour, concentration-camp level controls)  
- **19–27**: Ideological reprogramming / thought control / police-state ideological enforcement  
- **28–36**: Ideological enforcement and police-state monitoring of dissent/deviant behaviour  
- **37–43**: Societal/cultural/behavioural conformity (language & self-presentation policing begins)  
- **44–50**: Early ideological/identity-based controls (institutional rules require conformity in speech/self-presentation)  
- **51–59**: Paternalism / self-betterment laws  
- **60–69**: Betterment of society restrictions (nuisance / anti-social behaviour laws)  
- **70–95**: Protection of harm / vulnerable / prevention-of-harm laws (higher = narrower scope)  
- **96–100**: Minimal / near-absent systemic restrictions (system self-defence laws sit at 97–98)

**Key Rule:** The slider is **strictly negative-only**. Absence of invasive policies does **not** grant positive credit. Repealing or opposing restrictions is rhetoric only — it never raises a score.

## 2. The Entire Scoring Process (Step-by-Step – Must Be Followed Exactly)

1. **Read the raw policy / platform / historical record** in full (use neutered mechanical language only — strip all rhetoric).

2. **Dissect into individual mechanical components** (each law, each restriction type).

3. **Map each component** to an exact scored policy in `Core_1A_Scored_Policies_Only.md` (v2.3) — use the neutered base form.

4. **Apply weighting & special rules**: Heaviest weight to the most invasive elements.  
   - Mandatory EDI/retraining (22) is almost always the dominant driver in progressive packages.  
   - Apply the **Limited Historical / Targeted Exception Downweighting Rule** where relevant (see Section 3).  
   - Apply the **Core 2B Boundary** for external/non-domestic detention policies (Guantanamo-type).

5. **Apply Endos/Xenos split only when the system explicitly creates two classes** (Endos = in-group / full citizens; Xenos = designated out-group with permanent legal divergence). Otherwise use single holistic score. Note differential treatment in rationales even if no split is triggered.

6. **Temporal handling**: Split leaders into distinct eras when policies change dramatically (e.g., Johnson COVID vs non-COVID). Attach exact start/end dates where known. Temporally isolated extreme policies (e.g., COVID lockdowns at 18) are noted as such.

7. **Current-policy basis only** (locked rule): Score on **observable, real-world policies currently maintained/promoted**, **not** unconstrained aspirational goals or “what they would do with total power”. This rule was explicitly applied to align USA Progressives/MAGA with UK equivalents.

8. **Holistic score calculation**: Weighted average dominated by lowest-scoring components. Output exact score + band. Use the three Complete Authoritative Reference documents for country/leader/era context and lived-experience alignment.

9. **Bias / sanity / consistency check**: Explicitly ask “Am I being biased for/against this entity?” and “Does this match every equivalent policy package elsewhere?” (especially cross-UK/USA/Germany consistency). Re-run weighting checks when user flags potential bias.

10. **Lock the score** and add/update the independent entry in the relevant Complete Authoritative Reference document (or the master Scored Policies Only if it is a new pure policy type). Update version numbers as needed.

## 3. Important Rationales & Clarifications (All Locked)

### Core Rules (from the three Complete documents)

- **Current-Policy Basis**: All scoring uses observable, real-world policies currently maintained or promoted. Aspirational/unconstrained goals are not used unless explicitly requested.

- **Strict Negative-Only Weighting**: Good policies never cancel bad ones. Removal/opposition to restrictions grants no positive credit.

- **Limited Historical / Targeted Exception Downweighting Rule** (“one limited bad policy doesn’t cripple everything”): Narrow, targeted, historical exceptions (e.g., Nazi-symbol bans §86/§86a at individual score 32) are explicitly downweighted in holistic era/entity scoring. They are treated as settled post-war constitutional settlements rather than active general ideological restrictions. This prevents a single narrow policy from artificially capping every era score.

- **Core 2B Boundary (Guantanamo / External Enemy Combatants)**: Policies involving indefinite detention, enhanced interrogation, denial of habeas corpus, or executive designation as “enemy combatant” of foreign nationals captured outside domestic sovereign territory and held extraterritorially are classified under **Core 2B** and **excluded from Core 1A**. These individuals are not “living under” the domestic system. Leader/entity scores that would otherwise include them have been recalculated (e.g., Bush 41, Obama 43 after exclusion).

- **Inactive Caveat for GAC / Bathroom Bills / Sex-Realist Policies**: These are ideologically contested. A protection-of-vulnerable caveat exists (data supports protection of vulnerable in both female-only spaces framing and trans-inclusive framings). The caveat is **inactive** for scoring purposes until the ideological debate is resolved. While inactive, these policies are scored as ideological enforcement (44 range).

- **No Positive Buffs for Repeal/Opposition**: Repealing or opposing previous restrictions is never scored as a positive factor.

- **Johnson COVID vs Non-COVID Split (UK)**: Boris Johnson’s tenure is split into two distinct eras because COVID policies (18) represent an unprecedented, uniform, temporary loss of daily autonomy that would otherwise distort the assessment.

- **Future Roundup Rule**: When a politician, party, era, or ideology is discussed but not explicitly scored with an independent entry, note it as “discussed and scored” with the specific policies and rationale from the thread.

### Heaviest Mechanical Drivers (Consistent Across UK/USA/Germany)

- **Mandatory EDI / Sensitivity / Unconscious Bias Training** → **22** (ideological reprogramming / thought control). This is the single heaviest and most consistent downward driver in progressive packages. It explains why progressive entities cluster in the 28–36 band.

- **Self-ID / Self-Declaration Gender Recognition (minimal gatekeeping)** → **44**

- **Compulsory Institutional Recognition of Self-Declared Gender** → **42**

- **Non-Crime Hate Incident Recording + Institutional Monitoring** → **26**

- **COVID-19 Restrictions / Lockdowns (temporally isolated)** → **18** (extreme movement/association restrictions). Temporally isolated but correctly pulls affected eras low.

### Cross-Country Consistency Notes

- The progressive identity package (EDI 22 + Self-ID 44 + Institutional Recognition 42 + Non-Crime Hate Incidents 26) produces consistent scores in the 28–36 band across UK progressive parties and USA Progressives/AOC/Bernie/Biden/Establishment Democrats.

- Right-of-centre opposition clusters (UK Reform UK / Restore Britain / Farage / Lowe + USA MAGA / Trump) consistently score in the 60–69 band when opposition to the above package is strong and consistent.

- Surveillance / police-state elements (RIPA/DRIPA/Patriot Act bulk collection) sit at 29–30.

- Betterment-of-society measures (Race Relations Act 1976, ASBOs, certain Public Order powers) sit at 62–65.

- System self-defence laws (Official Secrets Act) sit at **97**.
