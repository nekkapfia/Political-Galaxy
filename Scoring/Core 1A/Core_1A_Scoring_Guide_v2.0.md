## 1. The Core 1A Slider – Bands & Mechanical Meaning

The slider measures **systemic restrictions on personal autonomy** (0 = total state dominion over body/mind/actions; 100 = complete absence of any laws, including no protection against harm).

**Bands:**

- **0–9**: Total dominion / physiological mind-body control  
- **10–18**: Extreme restrictions on movement / association / family / residence (curfews, forced labour, concentration-camp level controls)  
- **19–27**: Ideological reprogramming / thought control / police-state ideological enforcement  
- **28–36**: Ideological enforcement and police-state monitoring of dissent/deviant behaviour  
- **37–43**: Societal/cultural/behavioural conformity (language & self-presentation policing begins)  
- **44–50**: Early ideological/identity-based controls (institutional rules require conformity in speech/self-presentation)  
- **51–59**: Paternalism / self-betterment laws  
- **60–69**: Betterment of society restrictions (nuisance / anti-social behaviour laws)  
- **70–95**: Protection of harm / vulnerable / prevention-of-harm laws (higher = narrower scope)  
- **96–100**: Minimal / near-absent systemic restrictions (system self-defence laws sit at 97–98)

**Key Rule:** The slider is **strictly negative-only**. Absence of invasive policies does **not** grant positive credit. Repealing or opposing restrictions is rhetoric only — it never raises a score.

## 2. Policy Organization – 5-Category Type-Based Structure

Policies are now organized into **five broad categories** based on the **type of restriction**, not by score band:

1. **Information Control** — Restrictions on accessing, creating, or distributing information
2. **Speech & Expression Restrictions** — Direct limits on what individuals can say or communicate
3. **Movement Restrictions** (including Assembly & Association) — Limits on physical movement, gathering, and collective action
4. **Physical Restrictions** — Interference with the body, medical decisions, personal physical acts, and behavioural regulation
5. **Surveillance & Monitoring** — Systematic data collection and watching of the population

### Element-Level Breakdown Rule

Each policy is broken down into its individual **mechanical elements**. 

- Each element is placed in the most appropriate category.
- If a single policy contains elements belonging to different categories, the policy appears in each relevant category showing **only the elements** that belong there.
- Within each category, policies/elements are listed in **descending score order**.

This approach allows precise mapping and reuse of scored components across different policies and countries.

## 3. The Entire Scoring Process (Step-by-Step – Must Be Followed Exactly)

1. **Read the raw policy / platform / historical record** in full (use neutered mechanical language only — strip all rhetoric).

2. **Dissect into individual mechanical components** (each law, each restriction type).

3. **Map each component** to the most appropriate category in the current `Policies.md` (or `Core_1A_Scored_Policies_Reference_v1.md`) using the neutered base form and element breakdown.

4. **Apply weighting & special rules**: Heaviest weight to the most invasive elements.  
   - Mandatory EDI/retraining (22) is almost always the dominant driver in progressive packages.  
   - Apply the **Limited Historical / Targeted Exception Downweighting Rule** where relevant.  
   - Apply the **Core 2B Boundary** for external/non-domestic detention policies.

5. **Apply Endos/Xenos split only when the system explicitly creates two classes** (Endos = in-group / full citizens; Xenos = designated out-group with permanent legal divergence). Otherwise use single holistic score.

6. **Temporal handling**: Split leaders into distinct eras when policies change dramatically. Attach exact start/end dates where known. Temporally isolated extreme policies are noted as such.

7. **Current-policy basis only** (locked rule): Score on **observable, real-world policies currently maintained/promoted**, **not** unconstrained aspirational goals.

8. **Holistic score calculation**: Weighted average dominated by lowest-scoring components. Output exact score + band.

9. **Bias / sanity / consistency check**: Explicitly ask “Am I being biased for/against this entity?” and “Does this match every equivalent policy package elsewhere?”

10. **Lock the score** and add/update the independent entry in the relevant Complete Authoritative Reference document or the master `Policies.md`.

## 4. Important Rationales & Clarifications (All Locked)

### Core Rules

- **Current-Policy Basis**: Score only observable, real-world policies currently maintained or promoted.
- **Strict Negative-Only Weighting**: Good policies never cancel bad ones. Removal/opposition grants no positive credit.
- **Limited Historical / Targeted Exception Downweighting Rule**: Narrow, targeted, historical exceptions are explicitly downweighted in holistic scoring.
- **Core 2B Boundary**: External enemy combatant / extraterritorial detention policies are excluded from Core 1A.
- **No Positive Buffs for Repeal/Opposition**: Repeals or opposition are never scored as positive factors.

### Heaviest Mechanical Drivers (Consistent Across Countries)

- **Mandatory EDI / Sensitivity / Unconscious Bias Training** → **22** (ideological reprogramming / thought control)
- **Self-ID / Self-Declaration Gender Recognition** → **44**
- **Compulsory Institutional Recognition of Self-Declared Gender** → **42**
- **Non-Crime Hate Incident Recording + Institutional Monitoring** → **26**
- **COVID-19 Restrictions / Lockdowns (temporally isolated)** → **18**

### Cross-Country Consistency

The progressive identity package consistently produces scores in the **28–36** band.  
Right-of-centre opposition consistently clusters in the **60–69** band when opposition to the above package is strong and consistent.
