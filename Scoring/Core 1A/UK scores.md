## 1. UK Political Parties — Locked Scores & Rationale

### Green Party (including under Zac Polanski) — Score: 29 (28–36 band)

**Primary Mechanical Drivers:**
- Strong and consistent alignment with the full progressive identity package.
- Heavy emphasis on mandatory EDI/sensitivity training (ideological retraining at 22) across institutions.
- Support for self-ID / self-declaration gender recognition with minimal gatekeeping.
- Support for compulsory institutional recognition of self-declared gender in services, sports, prisons, and workplaces.
- Support for expanded non-crime hate incident recording and institutional monitoring/alignment requirements.

**Zac Polanski’s Influence:**  
As a prominent co-leader and public face of the party, Zac Polanski has reinforced and made more explicit the Green Party’s commitment to self-ID policies, institutional gender recognition, and opposition to biological sex-based protections in many areas. His leadership has strengthened the party’s alignment with the progressive identity package without fundamentally changing the mechanical score.

**Why this score?**  
The mandatory EDI/sensitivity training element (direct ideological retraining / thought control) is the single heaviest downward driver. Combined with self-ID and institutional compulsion, this places the party firmly in the 28–36 band. The score reflects observable policy positions rather than rhetoric. Zac Polanski’s influence has made these positions more visible and consistent.

### Labour Party — Score: 30 (28–36 band)

**Primary Mechanical Drivers:**
- Strong alignment with the progressive identity package, particularly under current leadership.
- Maintenance and expansion of institutional EDI frameworks, gender recognition policies, and speech regulation.
- Support for self-ID elements and compulsory institutional gender recognition in many contexts.
- Continuation of Blair-era approaches to religious hatred laws and institutional monitoring, with modern identity framing.

**Why this score?**  
Very similar mechanical profile to the Green Party. The dominant factor is the combination of mandatory EDI/retraining (22) + self-ID/institutional compulsion. Slight variation from Greens reflects marginally different emphasis on certain elements, but both sit in the same band.

### Conservative Party — Score: 49 (44–50 band)

**Primary Mechanical Drivers:**
- Mixed record: some resistance to the most extreme progressive identity policies (e.g., post-Badenoch shift toward biological sex in certain areas).
- However, continued maintenance of significant speech restrictions, institutional EDI frameworks in many public bodies, and incremental gender recognition policies.
- Avoidance of hard, blanket prohibitions on self-ID or GAC in many contexts.
- Retention of substantial elements of the previous progressive policy architecture.

**Why this score?**  
Sits in the 44–50 band due to partial resistance to the progressive package combined with continued maintenance of speech/institutional controls. Not as low as Labour/Greens, but not achieving the clearer break seen in Reform UK.

### Reform UK — Score: 66 (60–69 band)

Highest of the major UK parties due to consistent opposition to the core drivers of low scores in the progressive package (especially EDI training at 22 and self-ID compulsion). Places the party in the 60–69 band.

While there is some overlap in policy positioning with Reform UK, Restore Britain has been noted for particularly clear and consistent emphasis on these issues.

The score reflects strong and consistent opposition to the main drivers of low scores in the progressive package (EDI training at 22 and self-ID/institutional compulsion). This places Restore Britain at the high end of UK parties alongside Reform UK.

## 2. UK Political Leaders — Independent Core 1A Scores

This section provides fully independent holistic scores and detailed rationales for every UK political leader discussed and scored in the thread. Each leader has their own unique score based on the specific combination of policies active during their tenure. Scores are strictly negative (presence of invasive policies lowers the score; removal/opposition to invasive policies avoids the penalty but grants no positive bonus).

**Margaret Thatcher (1979–1990)** — Score: **58** (51–59 band)  
**Rationale:** The era combined betterment-of-society measures (Race Relations Act 1976 at 65, Public Order Act 1986 at 45) with police powers (PACE at 38, Official Secrets Act amendments at 97). No heavy ideological retraining or self-ID elements. Holistic score reflects this balance of policies. Explicitly scored in the thread as a distinct era.

**Tony Blair (1997–2007)** — Score: **52** (51–59 band)  
**Rationale:** Heavy surveillance and police-state elements (RIPA at 30, National DNA Database at 33), Blair-era usage of Racial and Religious Hatred Act in practice (24), ASBOs (62). After repeated bias and weighting rechecks, the era is characterised as managerial authoritarianism / surveillance-state paternalism. Explicitly scored with full policy dissection in the thread.

**Gordon Brown (2007–2010)** — Score: **51** (51–59 band)  
**Rationale:** Continuation of Blair-era surveillance and institutional policies (RIPA continuation, DNA database maintenance). No major new low-scoring policies introduced or rolled back. Explicitly discussed and scored as a distinct leader/era in the thread.

**Ed Miliband (2010–2015)** — Score: **48** (44–50 band)  
**Rationale:** Maintenance of progressive identity package and institutional controls during a transitional period. No significant expansion or reduction of the core restrictive drivers (EDI, self-ID, institutional alignment). Explicitly discussed and scored as a distinct leader in the thread.

**David Cameron (2010–2016)** — Score: **48** (44–50 band)  
**Rationale:** Key policy DRIPA (mass communications data retention at 29) plus continuation of Blair-era institutional and surveillance frameworks. No major new ideological retraining or self-ID expansion, but also no hard rollback of existing restrictive policies. Explicitly discussed in the thread (DRIPA scoring); now given independent holistic score.

**Theresa May (2016–2019)** — Score: **47** (44–50 band)  
**Rationale:** Intensified Prevent Strategy institutional monitoring (25) combined with maintenance of existing surveillance and speech-regulation frameworks. Transitional period with no significant new low-scoring policies but no major removals either. Explicitly discussed in the thread (Prevent intensification); now given independent holistic score.

**Boris Johnson – COVID Era (primarily 2020–early 2022)** — Score: **40** (37–43 band)  
**Rationale:** Dominated by COVID-19 restrictions, lockdowns and related compulsion (18 — one of the broadest and lowest mechanical effects recorded in post-war UK history, even after the temporary/emergency upward weighting for parliamentary scrutiny and time-limited framing). Combined with Online Safety Act (24) and Prevent monitoring during this period. The COVID policy is the single dominant driver pulling the score into the low 40s. Explicitly discussed and scored in the thread (COVID policy at 18); now separated as distinct era for clarity.

**Boris Johnson – Excluding COVID Era (2019 & late 2022 tail)** — Score: **47** (44–50 band)  
**Rationale:** Pre-COVID and post-peak policies: Online Safety Act 2023 (24), Prevent intensification (25), and general maintenance of institutional/speech frameworks without the COVID drag. No major new low-scoring policies in these periods. Score aligns closely with the May era (47). Explicitly discussed in the thread; now separated as distinct era for clarity.

**Rishi Sunak (2022–2024)** — Score: **45** (44–50 band)  
**Rationale:** Continuation of Johnson-era policies including the tail-end of COVID compulsion measures and Online Safety Act enforcement. No major new low-scoring policies introduced, but also no substantive rollbacks of the restrictive framework inherited. Explicitly discussed in the thread (COVID/Online Safety continuation); now given independent holistic score.

**Kemi Badenoch (Conservative Leader, 2024–current)** — Score: **55** (51–59 band)  
**Rationale:** As the current leader driving the Conservative shift toward biological sex as the sole legal category, resistance to self-ID expansion, and criticism of mandatory EDI training. This represents a clearer break from previous progressive institutional frameworks than the overall party average of 49. Explicitly discussed and scored in the thread.

**Keir Starmer (Labour Leader, 2024–current)** — Score: **30** (28–36 band)  
**Rationale:** Continuation and mainstreaming of the full progressive identity package (mandatory EDI training at 22, self-ID elements, institutional gender recognition and monitoring). His leadership has kept Labour aligned with the 28–36 band. Explicitly discussed and scored in the thread.

**Nigel Farage (Reform UK)** — Score: **66** (60–69 band)  
**Rationale:** Central role in Reform UK's strong opposition to the progressive identity package, emphasis on biological sex, free speech, and reduced institutional compulsion. His positioning is a primary driver of Reform's 66 score. Explicitly discussed and scored in the thread.

**Rupert Lowe (Restore Britain)** — Score: **66** (60–69 band)  
**Rationale:** Strong public emphasis on biological sex in law and policy, criticism of gender ideology in institutions, repeal of the Gender Recognition Act, and support for free speech. Aligns with and reinforces the high score in the 60–69 band. Explicitly discussed and scored in the thread.

**Zac Polanski (Green co-leader)** — Score: **29** (28–36 band)  
**Rationale:** Reinforcement of the Green Party’s commitment to self-ID with minimal gatekeeping and mandatory institutional EDI training (the heaviest single driver at 22). Explicitly discussed and scored in the thread as influential on the party's score.
