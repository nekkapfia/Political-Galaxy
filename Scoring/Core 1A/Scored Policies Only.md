# Core 1A Scored Policies Reference

**Version:** 2.2 (25 May 2026)  
**Purpose:** Pure reference document for the mechanical policy *types* scored under Core 1A (Personal Autonomy). Contains only the neutered base policy descriptions and the rationale for each score. No ideologies, historical regimes/countries, politicians, entities, overview philosophy, discarded policies, or mapping notes. This is the single source of truth for consistent policy scoring.

---

## System Self-Defence Laws

### Official Secrets Act 1989
**Neutered Base Policy**: Extremely broad criminal offences for unauthorised disclosure of official **SECRET** information relevant to national security by crown servants and journalists.

**Core 1A Score: 97**

**Rationale**: Pure system-protection mechanism with no general speech-restriction intent. It sits in the explicit 97–98 sub-range for laws whose purpose is to allow the system to protect itself from destruction. Not comparable to general speech or identity-based restrictions.

## Identity and Gender Policies

### Gender Recognition Act 2004 – Panel application for legal gender change
**Neutered Base Policy**: Legal gender change requires application to a Gender Recognition Panel.

**Core 1A Score: 72**

**Rationale**: Explicitly limited to official documents only. Does not prevent personal presentation or speech, and does not compel others to affirm.

### Gender Recognition Act 2004 – Medical evidence of gender dysphoria requirement
**Neutered Base Policy**: Application requires medical evidence of gender dysphoria.

**Core 1A Score: 72**

**Rationale**: Explicitly limited to official documents only. Does not prevent personal presentation or speech, and does not compel others to affirm.

### Gender Recognition Act 2004 – Two-year real-life experience requirement
**Neutered Base Policy**: Applicant must live in the acquired gender for two years prior to application.

**Core 1A Score: 72**

**Rationale**: Explicitly limited to official documents only. Does not prevent personal presentation or speech. The two-year requirement explicitly requires active self-expression. Does not compel others to affirm.

### Gender Recognition Act 2004 – Legal sex change via certificate on official documents
**Neutered Base Policy**: Successful applicants receive a Gender Recognition Certificate enabling change of legal sex on official documents (birth certificate, etc.).

**Core 1A Score: 72**

**Rationale**: Explicitly limited to official documents only. Does not prevent personal presentation or speech, and does not compel others to affirm.

**Weighting Note (all four GRA 2004 parts)**: These policies are strictly limited to official documents and do not prevent the individual's own self-expression, presentation, or speech. The two-year requirement explicitly requires active lived expression. No compulsion is placed on third parties to affirm. This distinguishes them from modern self-ID + institutional compulsion packages.

### Compulsory Institutional Recognition of Self-Declared Gender
**Neutered Base Policy**: Requires institutions to treat self-declared gender as legal sex in all relevant contexts (services, sports, prisons, workplaces, etc.).

**Core 1A Score: 42**

### Self-ID / Self-Declaration Gender Recognition
**Neutered Base Policy**: An individual can legally change their recorded sex/gender by self-declaration alone, with no medical gatekeeping or real-life test required.

**Core 1A Score: 44**

## Speech and Expression Policies

### Obscene Publications Act 1959
**Neutered Base Policy**: Criminalises publication of material likely to deprave and corrupt.

**Core 1A Score: 63** (Betterment of society)

**Rationale**: Obscene publications are still limited even under progressive ideology, explicitly targeting the showing of pornography to very young children. The crossing into banning for adults brings it into betterment of society.

### Racial Hatred Offences (Public Order Act 1986 Part 3)
**Neutered Base Policy**: It is an offence to use threatening, abusive or insulting words or behaviour, or display written material which is threatening, abusive or insulting, if the person intends to stir up racial hatred or racial hatred is likely to be stirred up thereby.

**Core 1A Score: 65** (top end of 60–69 band)

**Category**: Betterment of society

### Religious Hatred Offences (Racial and Religious Hatred Act 2006)
**Neutered Base Policy**: It is an offence to use threatening words or behaviour, or display any written material which is threatening, if the person intends thereby to stir up religious hatred.

**Core 1A Score: 32** (28–36 band)

**Category**: Ideological enforcement and police state (religion treated as proto-ideology → ideological suppression)

### Blair-era usage of Racial and Religious Hatred Act 2006
**Core 1A Score: 24** (mid/top 19–27 band)

**Rationale**: Due to how it was used in practice (suppression of legitimate public discussion, including issues such as grooming gangs), rather than its literal statutory content.

### Online Safety Act 2023
**Neutered Base Policy**: Imposes duties on online platforms to mitigate illegal and "legal but harmful" content, including age verification, safety-by-design requirements, and Ofcom enforcement powers.

**Core 1A Score: 24**

**Rationale**: State-mandated restriction/sanctioning of expression deemed harmful. Broader scope ("legal but harmful") and proactive platform duties. Slightly more invasive in systemic reach but comparable overall effect on expression.

**Weighting Rationale (Racial vs Religious split)**: Racial hatred provisions (pre-existing) are treated as betterment of society. Religious hatred provisions (2006 addition) are treated as ideological suppression because religion is classified as a form of proto-ideology. The Blair-era usage adjustment reflects real-world application beyond the text of the statute.

## Protection Against Harm / Security Measures

### Prevention of Terrorism Acts (7-day detention without charge)
**Core 1A Score: 88**

### Control Orders (2005)
**Core 1A Score: 88**

### COVID-19 Restrictions, Lockdowns, and Related Compulsion (2020–2022, Johnson/Sunak era)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Legal compulsion restricting movement, association, education, work, and (via certificates/mandates) medical choice, justified as temporary public health emergency measures.

**Core 1A Score: 18** (within 15–25 range)

**Rationale**: One of the broadest and most direct restrictions on everyday personal autonomy in modern UK history. However, policies were explicitly time-limited, subject to repeated parliamentary renewal/scrutiny, and framed as emergency public health measures rather than permanent ideological or system-self-defence policy. This provides meaningful upward weighting within the 15–25 band.

## Police Powers and Surveillance Policies

### Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act 2000 (RIPA)
**Core 1A Score: 30**

### Data Retention and Investigatory Powers Act 2014 (DRIPA)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Required retention of communications data by telecommunications operators and access by public authorities for investigatory purposes.

**Core 1A Score: 29**

**Rationale**: Functionally very similar mechanical effect to RIPA (mass communications data retention and access). Slightly more targeted in response to legal challenges.

### Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 (PACE)
**Core 1A Score: 38**

### National DNA Database Expansion
**Core 1A Score: 33**

### Prevent Strategy / Counter-Extremism Institutional Monitoring (intensified May/Johnson era)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Requires public institutions (schools, universities, NHS, local authorities, prisons, etc.) to have "due regard" to prevent people from being drawn into terrorism, including monitoring, training staff, and reporting on signs of radicalisation and extremist ideology.

**Core 1A Score: 25**

**Rationale**: The mechanical structure (institutional compulsion to monitor beliefs/ideology, staff training, and reporting duties) is functionally very similar to Mandatory EDI/Sensitivity Training (22). Score of 25 reflects this proximity while giving modest upward weighting for the narrower target (violent extremism/terrorism) and explicit security justification.

## Institutional EDI and Ideological Alignment Policies (Progressive Package)

### Mandatory Sensitivity / EDI / Unconscious Bias Training
**Neutered Base Policy**: Mandates training sessions that instruct participants their existing beliefs on identity are biased/harmful and must be corrected.

**Core 1A Score: 22**

**Rationale**: Direct ideological retraining (thought control). This is the single heaviest mechanical penalty in the system. This is why progressive packages cluster in the low 10–18 / high 28–36 range.

### Non-Crime Hate Incident Recording
**Neutered Base Policy**: Records non-crime hate incidents.

**Core 1A Score: 26**

**Rationale**: Police exercising extra-legal authority.

### Institutional Monitoring & Alignment Requirements
**Neutered Base Policy**: Ongoing monitoring, reporting, and enforcement of EDI compliance across public bodies and workplaces.

**Core 1A Score: 24**

**Weighting Note**: The mandatory EDI/sensitivity training element (listed above in this package) is the single heaviest mechanical driver. It is direct ideological retraining (thought control) and more invasive than speech restrictions because it targets the formation of belief itself. This explains why combinations involving these policies score in the lower bands.
