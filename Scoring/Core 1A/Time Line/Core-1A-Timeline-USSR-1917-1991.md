# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in the USSR (1917–1991)

## Introductory Overview

The Soviet trajectory is one of extreme multi-domain restriction from the earliest years, with internal variation between the revolutionary/Civil War and Stalinist peaks, the post-Stalin partial normalisation, and the late Gorbachev opening. Scores remain very low until the final years. Endos/Xenos differentials are pronounced for designated class, ethnic, religious and political out-groups.

## **1917–1928: Revolutionary Foundations – Endos 15 / Xenos 8**

Establishment of the Cheka, press controls, and suppression of opposition parties and independent association.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Suppression of non-Bolshevik press and parties.
* Red Terror and early concentration-camp system.
* Class-based targeting of “former people” and political opponents.

**Key Shift:** Destruction of the residual Imperial and Provisional Government legal order and construction of the Party monopoly.

### Why this produces Endos 15 / Xenos 8
Immediate multi-domain coercive controls with differential intensity on class and political targets.

---

## **1929–1953: Stalinist Peak – Endos 6 / Xenos 3**

Collectivisation, Great Terror, Gulag expansion, and total ideological monopoly.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Near-total control of speech, association, movement and belief.
* Mass terror and camp system as instruments of conformity.
* Extreme differential targeting of designated ethnic, class and political groups.

**Key Shift:** Descent into the most intensive phase of Soviet restriction.

### Why this produces Endos 6 / Xenos 3
Maximum cumulative weight of multi-domain coercion.

---

## **1953–1985: Post-Stalin System – Endos 20 / Xenos 12**

End of mass terror accompanied by retention of the comprehensive control architecture (censorship, psychiatric abuse, exit visas, ideological monopoly).

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Continued formal and informal speech and association controls.
* Emigration and movement restrictions.
* Targeted repression of dissidents, religious believers and certain ethnic groups.

**Key Shift:** Stabilisation at a still extremely low level of personal autonomy after the removal of the most chaotic terror instruments.

### Why this produces Endos 20 / Xenos 12
Modest upward movement from the Stalinist floor while the core system remains intact.

---

## **1985–1991: Late Opening – Endos 45 / Xenos 35**

Glasnost and perestroika produced the first sustained expansion of speech and association rights in Soviet history.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Dramatic reduction in censorship and ideological enforcement.
* Emergence of independent associations and media.
* Residual restrictions remained until the final collapse.

**Key Shift:** Deliberate political decision to relax the multi-domain control system.

### Why this produces Endos 45 / Xenos 35
Largest upward movement in the Soviet period, still constrained by residual architecture.
