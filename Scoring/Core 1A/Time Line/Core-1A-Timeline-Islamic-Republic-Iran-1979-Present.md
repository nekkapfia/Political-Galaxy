# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in the Islamic Republic of Iran (1979–Present)

## Introductory Overview

The Islamic Republic established a comprehensive theocratic-restrictive system after 1979, combining ideological controls on speech and association with gender-specific bodily and dress restrictions and severe limits on religious and political dissent. The trajectory shows continuity of the core architecture with periodic intensifications (especially after major protest waves) and limited tactical relaxations. Endos (core supportive population) experience a dense baseline of restrictions on expression, association, dress and religious practice; women, religious minorities, and political dissidents face systematically higher restrictions, justifying an Endos/Xenos framing across the period. Scores remain low throughout, with only modest upward movement during the limited post-war normalisation of the 1990s and early 2000s.

The long-term pattern is one of persistent multi-domain theocratic control, punctuated by cycles of protest and reinforced enforcement rather than by structural liberalisation.

## **1979–1989: Revolutionary Foundation – Endos 18 / Xenos 10**

The establishment of the velayat-e faqih system, the suppression of opposition groups, and the imposition of Islamic dress and morality codes created a multi-domain restrictive environment from the first years of the Islamic Republic. The Iran-Iraq War further intensified controls on expression, association and movement.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Comprehensive suppression of independent press, political parties and leftist, liberal and ethnic opposition organisations.
* Imposition of mandatory hijab and gender-segregation rules, creating direct bodily and expressive restrictions that applied differentially by sex.
* Establishment of revolutionary courts and morality enforcement mechanisms that policed public behaviour, dress and speech.
* Wartime emergency measures that further restricted association, movement and political expression.
* Targeting of religious minorities (including Baha’is) and political dissidents with heightened intensity.
* Core criminal prohibitions on ordinary private violence remained, but political and morality-based coercion dominated the restrictive reality.

The restrictive architecture is defined by the rapid construction of a theocratic one-party system with explicit gender and ideological differentials.

**Key Shift:** Replacement of the Pahlavi order with the theocratic restrictive architecture of the Islamic Republic across speech, association, dress and religious practice.

### Why this produces Endos 18 / Xenos 10

Immediate multi-domain ideological and gender-based controls produce low scores. The Xenos differential reflects higher intensity against women (through dress and morality rules), religious minorities and political opponents. Negative-Only Weighting applies.

## **1990–2004: Limited Normalisation – Endos 25 / Xenos 15**

The end of the Iran-Iraq War and the presidencies of Rafsanjani and Khatami produced limited openings in media, associational life and public debate. The core theocratic restrictions on dress, political speech and religious practice remained in force, but enforcement intensity was somewhat reduced relative to the revolutionary decade.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Continued mandatory hijab and morality enforcement, though with periods of somewhat lighter street-level application.
* Residual and still substantial controls on press, political organisation and public criticism of the system of velayat-e faqih.
* Limited expansion of civil-society space and reformist media under Khatami, later partially reversed.
* Ongoing discrimination and restrictions directed at religious minorities and political dissidents.
* The underlying constitutional and legal architecture of theocratic control was not dismantled.

The restrictive profile is characterised by tactical relaxation within an unchanged core framework of ideological, gender and political controls.

**Key Shift:** Tactical relaxation of enforcement intensity after the war, without structural liberalisation of the theocratic restrictive architecture.

### Why this produces Endos 25 / Xenos 15

Modest upward movement from the revolutionary peak under Negative-Only Weighting, reflecting reduced enforcement intensity rather than removal of core restrictions. The Xenos differential persists because dress, morality and minority restrictions remained in force.

## **2005–2019: Hardline Re-assertion – Endos 20 / Xenos 12**

The return of harder-line governments after 2005, the suppression of the 2009 Green Movement, and tightened controls on media, the internet and public morality reversed many of the limited openings of the preceding period.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Tightened media and internet controls, including filtering, arrests of journalists and bloggers, and pressure on reformist outlets.
* Intensified morality-police activity and enforcement of dress codes.
* Severe repression of the 2009 protest movement, including arrests, trials and restrictions on association and expression.
* Continued and in some domains increased pressure on religious minorities and political dissidents.
* Retention of the full theocratic legal and institutional framework.

The restrictive profile is defined by the re-emphasis on ideological conformity and the rollback of the limited post-war openings.

**Key Shift:** Re-assertion of harder enforcement of speech, dress and association rules after the reformist interlude, reinforced by the suppression of the 2009 protests.

### Why this produces Endos 20 / Xenos 12

Renewed intensity of the core restrictions produces a downward movement from the 1990–2004 level. Negative-Only Weighting applies. The Xenos differential remains clear.

---

## **2020–Present: Protest and Enforcement Cycle – Endos 15 / Xenos 8**

The Woman, Life, Freedom protests that began in 2022 and the subsequent tightening of dress, speech and internet controls mark a further downward movement. The regime has responded to mass dissent with reinforced restrictive measures rather than structural concession.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Intensified enforcement of hijab rules and related bodily controls following the 2022 protests, including new legislative and technological measures.
* Severe internet restrictions, platform blocking and expression controls during and after protest waves.
* Continued targeting of dissidents, women’s-rights activists, and religious and ethnic minorities.
* Retention of the full pre-existing theocratic architecture of speech, association and morality controls.
* The cumulative effect of protest-related crackdowns has pushed scores lower than the preceding hardline period.

The restrictive profile is defined by the cycle of mass protest met by reinforced multi-domain enforcement.

**Key Shift:** Cycle of major protest met by reinforced enforcement of dress, speech and internet controls, producing a further reduction in autonomy.

### Why this produces Endos 15 / Xenos 8

Further downward movement driven by the post-2022 enforcement wave. Negative-Only Weighting applies. The Xenos differential is pronounced because women (through dress and morality rules), protesters and minorities bear the highest intensity of restriction. The placement is consistent with the cumulative theocratic architecture still in force.
