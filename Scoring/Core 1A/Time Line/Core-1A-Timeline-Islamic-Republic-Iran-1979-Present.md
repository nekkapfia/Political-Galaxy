# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in the Islamic Republic of Iran (1979–Present)

## Introductory Overview

The Islamic Republic established a comprehensive theocratic-restrictive system after 1979, combining ideological speech and association controls with gender-specific bodily and dress restrictions and severe limits on religious and political dissent. The trajectory shows continuity of the core architecture with periodic intensifications (especially after protests) and limited tactical relaxations. Endos (core supportive population) experience a dense baseline; women, religious minorities, and political dissidents face systematically higher restrictions, justifying an Endos/Xenos framing in several periods.

## **1979–1989: Revolutionary Foundation – Endos 18 / Xenos 10**

Establishment of the velayat-e faqih system, suppression of opposition, and imposition of Islamic dress and morality codes.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Comprehensive press, party and association controls.
* Mandatory hijab and gender segregation rules.
* Suppression of leftist, liberal and ethnic opposition.
* Wartime emergency measures.

**Key Shift:** Replacement of the Pahlavi order with the theocratic restrictive architecture.

### Why this produces Endos 18 / Xenos 10
Immediate multi-domain ideological and gender-based controls.

## **1990–2004: Limited Normalisation – Endos 25 / Xenos 15**

Post-war period with some media and associational space under reformist governments, while the core theocratic restrictions remained.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Continued mandatory dress and morality enforcement.
* Residual press and political controls.
* Periodic openings and closings of expression space.

**Key Shift:** Tactical relaxation within an unchanged constitutional-religious framework.

### Why this produces Endos 25 / Xenos 15
Modest upward movement from the revolutionary peak.

## **2005–2019: Hardline Re-assertion – Endos 20 / Xenos 12**

Return of harder enforcement of speech, dress and association rules.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Tightened media and internet controls.
* Intensified morality-police activity.
* Suppression of the 2009 protest movement.

**Key Shift:** Re-emphasis on ideological conformity after the reformist interlude.

### Why this produces Endos 20 / Xenos 12
Renewed intensity of the core restrictions.

---

## **2020–Present: Protest and Enforcement Cycle – Endos 15 / Xenos 8**

Woman, Life, Freedom protests and subsequent tightening of dress, speech and internet controls.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Intensified enforcement of hijab rules and related bodily controls.
* Severe internet and expression restrictions during and after protests.
* Continued targeting of dissidents and minorities.

**Key Shift:** Cycle of mass protest met by reinforced restrictive measures.

### Why this produces Endos 15 / Xenos 8
Further downward movement driven by the post-protest enforcement wave.
