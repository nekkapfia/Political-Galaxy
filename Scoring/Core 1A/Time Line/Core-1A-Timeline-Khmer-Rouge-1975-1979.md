# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy under the Khmer Rouge (1975–1979)

## Introductory Overview

The Khmer Rouge regime produced one of the most extreme collapses of personal autonomy in recorded history. Within days of taking Phnom Penh in April 1975 the regime emptied the cities, abolished money, markets, religion, family autonomy and virtually all voluntary association and expression. The entire population was subjected to forced labour, total movement control and ideological terror. Designated categories faced systematically higher lethality, but the extreme coercive architecture was applied so broadly that, under the rule requiring identical criteria for Endos and Xenos, the optional split is not used. The period is treated as a single intense era because the restrictive architecture was imposed almost immediately and remained in force until the regime’s overthrow in 1979. The combined score sits at the practical floor of the Core 1A scale.

## **1975–1979: Democratic Kampuchea – Combined Score: 4**

(No Endos/Xenos split)

The Khmer Rouge took Phnom Penh on 17 April 1975 and within days ordered the complete evacuation of the cities. The entire urban population was driven into the countryside under armed guard, abolishing urban life and imposing total control over residence and movement. Money, markets and private exchange were abolished. Religion was comprehensively suppressed. Family authority was subordinated to the organisation. Labour was totally controlled through forced collectivisation. Designated categories faced systematic execution, starvation and overwork. The restrictive architecture was imposed almost instantaneously and applied system-wide.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Forced evacuation of Phnom Penh and other cities within days of the regime’s victory, abolishing urban life and imposing total control over residence and movement.
* Abolition of money, markets and private exchange, eliminating economic autonomy.
* Comprehensive suppression of religion: pagodas closed or destroyed, monks defrocked or killed, religious practice prohibited.
* Destruction of independent family authority; children separated from parents in many cases and subordinated to collective organisation.
* Total control of labour through forced collectivisation and work assignments; refusal or inadequate performance treated as political offence.
* Systematic execution, starvation and lethal overwork directed at urbanites, intellectuals, former officials, ethnic minorities and other designated categories.
* Complete suppression of voluntary political expression, association and independent communication.
* Ordinary protective criminal law against private violence was irrelevant; the central reality was political and administrative coercion on a total scale.

Because the extreme coercive system was applied broadly across the population, and under the rule that Endos and Xenos use identical criteria, the optional split is not applied. A single combined score at the practical floor is used.

**Key Shift:** Instantaneous replacement of the residual Cambodian state and social order with the most radical agrarian-totalitarian project of the twentieth century.

### Why this produces a combined score of 4

The cumulative weight of total movement, labour, association, expression and bodily controls, combined with mass killing, reaches the practical floor of the Core 1A scale (band 4). Under identical criteria for Endos and Xenos, and given that both the general population and the most targeted categories sit at that floor, the split is not applied. Combined score: 4.
