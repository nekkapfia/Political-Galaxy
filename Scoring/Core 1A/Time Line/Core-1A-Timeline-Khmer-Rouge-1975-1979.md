# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy under the Khmer Rouge (1975–1979)

## Introductory Overview

The Khmer Rouge regime produced one of the most extreme collapses of personal autonomy in recorded history. Within days of taking Phnom Penh the regime emptied the cities, abolished money, markets, religion, family autonomy and virtually all voluntary association and expression. The entire population was subjected to forced labour, movement control and ideological terror; designated categories (urbanites, intellectuals, ethnic minorities, former Lon Nol personnel) faced systematically higher lethality. The period is treated as a single intense era.

## **1975–1979: Democratic Kampuchea – Endos 4 / Xenos 2**

Immediate and comprehensive destruction of the prior social and legal order.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Forced evacuation of cities and abolition of urban life.
* Total control of movement, labour, food and association.
* Abolition of religion, money and independent family authority.
* Systematic execution and starvation of designated categories.
* Complete suppression of voluntary expression and association.

**Key Shift:** Instantaneous replacement of the residual Cambodian state with the most radical agrarian-totalitarian project of the twentieth century.

### Why this produces Endos 4 / Xenos 2

The cumulative weight of total movement, labour, association, expression and bodily controls, combined with mass killing of out-groups, reaches the practical floor of the Core 1A scale (Score 4 Floor). The Xenos score reflects the additional intensity directed at designated categories.
