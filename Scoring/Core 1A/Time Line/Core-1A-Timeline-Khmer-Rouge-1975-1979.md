# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy under the Khmer Rouge (1975–1979)

## Introductory Overview

The Khmer Rouge regime produced one of the most extreme collapses of personal autonomy in recorded history. Within days of taking Phnom Penh in April 1975 the regime emptied the cities, abolished money, markets, religion, family autonomy and virtually all voluntary association and expression. The entire population was subjected to forced labour, total movement control and ideological terror. Designated categories (urbanites, intellectuals, ethnic minorities, former Lon Nol personnel and others) faced systematically higher lethality. The period is treated as a single intense era because the restrictive architecture was imposed almost immediately and remained in force, with only internal variations in intensity, until the regime’s overthrow in 1979. Scores reach the practical floor of the Core 1A scale.

## **1975–1979: Democratic Kampuchea – Endos 4 / Xenos 2**

The Khmer Rouge immediately dismantled the residual institutions of the Khmer Republic and imposed a radical agrarian-totalitarian order. Cities were evacuated, money and markets abolished, religion suppressed, family authority subordinated to the organisation, and the population driven into forced-labour collectives. Expression, association and movement were reduced to the minimum compatible with the regime’s production and security goals. Designated enemy categories were subjected to systematic execution, starvation and overwork.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Forced evacuation of Phnom Penh and other cities within days of the regime’s victory, abolishing urban life and imposing total control over residence and movement.
* Abolition of money, markets and private exchange, eliminating economic autonomy.
* Comprehensive suppression of religion: pagodas closed or destroyed, monks defrocked or killed, religious practice prohibited.
* Destruction of independent family authority; children separated from parents in many cases and subordinated to collective organisation.
* Total control of labour through forced collectivisation and work assignments; refusal or inadequate performance was treated as political offence.
* Systematic execution, starvation and lethal overwork directed at urbanites, intellectuals, former officials, ethnic minorities (including Vietnamese, Chinese and Cham) and other designated categories.
* Complete suppression of voluntary political expression, association and independent communication; the regime tolerated no autonomous public sphere.
* Ordinary protective criminal law against private violence was irrelevant; the central reality was political and administrative coercion on a total scale.

The restrictive architecture is defined by the near-instantaneous replacement of the prior social and legal order with a system of total movement, labour, association, expression and bodily control, combined with mass killing of out-groups.

**Key Shift:** Instantaneous replacement of the residual Cambodian state and social order with the most radical agrarian-totalitarian project of the twentieth century.

### Why this produces Endos 4 / Xenos 2

The cumulative weight of total movement, labour, association, expression and bodily controls, combined with mass killing of designated categories, reaches the practical floor of the Core 1A scale. The Score 4 Floor is applied; lower scores are theoretically possible only in conditions approaching the complete physical destruction of a population. The Xenos score of 2 reflects the additional intensity and lethality directed at urbanites, intellectuals, ethnic minorities and former regime personnel. Negative-Only Weighting applies throughout; no residual protective or liberalising elements offset the multi-domain coercion.
