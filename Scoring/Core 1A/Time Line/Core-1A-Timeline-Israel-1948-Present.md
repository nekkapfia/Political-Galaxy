# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in Israel (1948–Present)

## Introductory Overview

Israel maintains a relatively high Core 1A baseline for its Jewish and core citizen population under a democratic system with strong judicial protections for speech and association. At the same time it has applied a markedly more restrictive regime in the occupied territories and, until 1966, under military government to most Arab citizens. The trajectory shows continuity of this dual structure rather than a single linear liberalisation or tightening. COVID produced a temporary nationwide restriction that affected the citizen population. Overall citizen-level scores remain higher than most European comparators; the differential treatment of non-citizen populations in the territories is the principal source of the Endos/Xenos framing used in this document.

The long-term pattern is therefore one of a high-autonomy democratic core coexisting with a durable, security-driven restrictive regime in the territories conquered in 1967. Score movements within the citizen sphere are relatively modest; the larger differentials appear in the contrast between the two legal-political orders.

## **1948–1966: Founding and Military Government – Endos 70 / Territories 35**

The establishment of the state created robust democratic institutions and formal speech and association rights for the Jewish population and for those Arab citizens who were not under military administration. At the same time, the majority of Arab citizens lived under military government until December 1966, based on the Defence (Emergency) Regulations 1945. Travel permits were required; unauthorised movement was a frequent basis for arrest and conviction. Political organisation and expression by the Arab population were subject to military oversight.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Strong formal constitutional and judicial protections for speech and association applicable to citizens inside the ordinary civilian legal order.
* Military government over most Arab citizens (until December 1966) that restricted movement, required permits for travel, and limited political organisation and expression.
* Core criminal prohibitions on violence and direct harm applied across the population.
* Emergency regulations inherited from the Mandate period remained available and were used selectively.
* The dual structure created a clear and stable differential between the civilian democratic order and the military-administered population.
* Ordinary protective criminal law operated in both spheres, but the additional administrative controls produced markedly lower practical autonomy for those under military government.

The restrictive architecture of the founding period is defined by the coexistence of a high-autonomy democratic core with a military-government regime applied to a large portion of the Arab citizen population. Inside the ordinary civilian legal order, formal speech and association rights were robust by comparative standards. The military administration imposed direct restrictions on movement and political organisation that created a stable, discretely targetable differential. Negative-Only Weighting records both the high formal baseline for the Endos population and the substantially lower autonomy of the military-administered population.

**Key Shift:** Creation of the dual legal-political structure of the new state: democratic institutions for the core citizen body alongside military administration for most Arab citizens.

### Why this produces Endos 70 / Territories 35

High formal autonomy for the population under ordinary civilian law produces an Endos score of 70. The military-government regime produces a substantially lower score for the affected population. Negative-Only Weighting applies; the differential is stable and discretely targetable.

## **1967–1987: Occupation Framework – Endos 72 / Territories 30**

The 1967 war extended Israeli control to the West Bank, Gaza, Sinai and Golan. A comprehensive occupation legal regime developed that imposed extensive restrictions on movement, association and expression for the Palestinian population of the territories, while the citizen population inside the Green Line continued to enjoy high formal autonomy. The dual structure hardened into a long-term feature of the political order.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Continued high formal speech and association rights for Israeli citizens under the ordinary legal system.
* Military government and occupation law in the newly controlled territories, including permit regimes, movement restrictions, and controls on political organisation and expression.
* Settlement activity and security measures that further structured the restrictive environment in the territories.
* Retention of core criminal protections inside Israel proper.
* The dual structure of high citizen autonomy and dense territorial controls became a durable feature.
* Ordinary protective criminal law continued to operate inside the civilian legal order.

The restrictive profile is defined by the institutionalisation of the post-1967 territorial regime alongside the maintenance of democratic autonomy for citizens. Inside the Green Line the formal environment for speech and association remained high. In the territories a dense administrative and security framework limited movement, association and expression for the Palestinian population. Negative-Only Weighting records the slight upward drift in the citizen sphere from institutional consolidation and the continued high restrictiveness of the territorial regime.

**Key Shift:** Institutionalisation of the post-1967 occupation regime as a durable second legal-political order.

### Why this produces Endos 72 / Territories 30

Slight upward drift in the citizen sphere from institutional consolidation. The territorial regime remains highly restrictive. Negative-Only Weighting applies.

## **1988–2000: Intifada and Oslo – Endos 74 / Territories 32**

The First Intifada produced temporary intensifications of security controls. The Oslo process then transferred limited authority to the Palestinian Authority in parts of the territories, creating modest variation within the overall dual structure, while citizen autonomy inside Israel remained high. The period is characterised by oscillation within the established dual framework rather than its transcendence.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Temporary intensification of movement and association controls during the First Intifada, including curfews, closures and expanded use of administrative detention.
* Partial transfer of civilian authority to the Palestinian Authority in Areas A and B under Oslo, reducing direct Israeli restriction in those zones while overall security control remained.
* Continued high formal autonomy for Israeli citizens under the ordinary legal system.
* Retention of the underlying dual structure; no transformation into a single high-autonomy order for the entire population under Israeli control.
* Ordinary protective criminal law continued inside the civilian legal order.
* The net movement relative to the peak intensity of the Intifada years was modestly upward in both columns.

The restrictive profile is characterised by oscillation within the established dual framework rather than its elimination. Temporary intensifications during the Intifada were followed by limited transfers of authority under Oslo. Citizen rights inside Israel remained high. Negative-Only Weighting records the modest net upward movement reflecting the partial Oslo arrangements and the absence of sustained nationwide emergency rule inside Israel.

**Key Shift:** Oscillation between intensified security controls and limited Oslo-era transfers of authority, without elimination of the dual structure.

### Why this produces Endos 74 / Territories 32

Modest net upward movement in both columns reflecting the partial Oslo arrangements and the absence of sustained nationwide emergency rule inside Israel. Negative-Only Weighting applies.

## **2001–2019: Security Consolidation – Endos 70 / Territories 28**

The Second Intifada, the construction of the security barrier, and the re-assertion of comprehensive Israeli security control produced a return to a more restrictive equilibrium in the territories while citizen rights inside Israel remained formally robust. Checkpoints, the permit regime, administrative detention and military operations severely limited movement, association and expression for the Palestinian population under occupation.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Heightened security and movement controls in the West Bank and Gaza, including the barrier, checkpoints and permit regimes.
* Military operations and administrative measures that severely restricted association and expression for the Palestinian population under occupation.
* Retention of democratic speech and association rights for Israeli citizens, subject only to ordinary criminal and emergency-law limits.
* Periodic use of emergency regulations, but no permanent suspension of the civilian legal order inside the Green Line.
* Ordinary protective criminal law continued to operate inside the civilian legal order.
* The dual structure continued without structural transformation.

The restrictive profile is defined by the re-assertion of comprehensive security control in the territories after the collapse of the Oslo process. Inside the Green Line formal autonomy remained high. In the territories the security framework intensified. Negative-Only Weighting records a slight downward adjustment in the citizen sphere from the security atmosphere of the period and a clearer downward movement in the territories.

**Key Shift:** Re-assertion of comprehensive security control in the territories following the Second Intifada and the construction of the barrier.

### Why this produces Endos 70 / Territories 28

Slight downward adjustment in the citizen sphere from the security atmosphere of the period; clearer downward movement in the territories. Negative-Only Weighting applies.

## **2020–early 2022: COVID Restrictions Block – Endos 50 / Territories 25**

Nationwide emergency regulations on movement and gathering applied to the citizen population, producing a temporary but material reduction in autonomy. The territorial regime continued under its pre-existing restrictive framework. Single-block treatment is applied for the citizen sphere consistent with the protocol used for other countries.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Lockdowns, movement restrictions and gathering limits imposed on the Israeli citizen population.
* Related public-health mandates conditioning access to ordinary activities.
* Continued occupation and security controls in the territories.
* Temporary character of the COVID measures provides only modest mitigation under the established protocol.
* Ordinary protective criminal law continued to operate.
* Variation in intensity existed, but the national cumulative weight on the citizen population justifies single-block treatment.

The restrictive profile is defined by the temporary nationwide emergency overlay on the citizen population together with the ongoing territorial regime. The cumulative weight of the COVID measures produced a clear temporary drop in the citizen score. Negative-Only Weighting records the full weight of the restrictions while they were in force.

**Key Shift:** Temporary nationwide emergency restrictions applied to the citizen population.

### Why this produces Endos 50 / Territories 25

Protocol-consistent scoring of the COVID measures produces a clear temporary drop in the citizen score. The territorial score remains low. Negative-Only Weighting applies.

## **Mid-2022 onward: Post-COVID Continuity – Endos 68 / Territories 28**

Lifting of the most sweeping COVID measures restored most of the pre-pandemic citizen autonomy. The long-standing dual structure remains intact: high formal rights inside the civilian legal order and a dense security-driven restrictive regime in the territories. No structural transformation of the dual system has occurred.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Termination of the most sweeping COVID movement and gathering restrictions for citizens, raising the score by reducing negative weight.
* Retention of the ordinary democratic legal order for speech and association inside Israel.
* Ongoing occupation, permit, movement and security framework in the West Bank and the separate regime applicable to Gaza.
* No structural transformation of the dual system.
* Ordinary protective criminal law continues to operate inside the civilian legal order.
* The recovery in the citizen sphere is substantial; the territorial score remains low and stable.

The restrictive profile is defined by the removal of the temporary COVID layer and the persistence of the pre-existing dual architecture. Inside the Green Line formal autonomy has largely returned to the pre-pandemic level. In the territories the dense security framework continues without material liberalisation. Negative-Only Weighting records the recovery from COVID as a reduction in negative weight and the retention of the dual structure as continued negative weight for the territorial population.

**Key Shift:** Removal of the temporary COVID layer while the long-standing dual structure of citizen autonomy and territorial restriction continues.

### Why this produces Endos 68 / Territories 28

Substantial recovery from the COVID low in the citizen sphere, limited by the absence of any broader liberalisation of residual emergency or security powers. The territorial score remains low and stable. Negative-Only Weighting applies throughout.
