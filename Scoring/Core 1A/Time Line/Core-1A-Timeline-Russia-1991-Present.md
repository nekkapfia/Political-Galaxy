# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in Russia (1991–Present)

## Introductory Overview

Post-Soviet Russia began with a sharp liberalisation of speech and association relative to the late Soviet period, producing a relatively high Base Score in the 1990s. From the early 2000s the trajectory reversed through successive layers of “extremism”, “foreign agent”, public-morality and, after 2022, wartime speech legislation. The result is a low-autonomy equilibrium in which the formal multi-party system operates inside a dense restrictive framework. Endos/Xenos differentials appear in the targeting of designated categories (LGBTQ expression, certain political dissidents, “foreign agents”).

## **1991–1999: Post-Soviet Opening – Base Score: 65**

Collapse of the Soviet control system produced a rapid expansion of press freedom, political association and public expression.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Dismantling of formal censorship and the Party monopoly on association.
* Retention of residual criminal speech provisions but with sharply reduced enforcement intensity.
* Emergence of independent media and parties.

**Key Shift:** Abrupt removal of the Soviet multi-domain control apparatus.

### Why this produces a final score of 65

Substantial reduction in direct restrictions yields a mid-high score relative to both the Soviet past and the later Russian present.

---

## **2000–2011: Re-consolidation – Endos 48 / Xenos 42**

Recentralisation under Putin included early extremism legislation and pressure on independent media.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Extremism laws used against political and religious expression.
* Media ownership and regulatory pressure.
* Early restrictions on protest and association.

**Key Shift:** Re-introduction of systematic administrative and legal controls on speech and association.

### Why this produces Endos 48 / Xenos 42

Cumulative new restrictive layers pull the score down; modest differential appears.

---

## **2012–2021: Foreign-Agent and Morality Layer – Endos 35 / Xenos 28**

Foreign-agent regime, “gay propaganda” law, and tightened protest rules further densified the restrictive environment.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Foreign-agent and undesirable-organisation statutes.
* Ban on “propaganda of non-traditional sexual relations”.
* Expanded protest and assembly restrictions.
* Continued extremism enforcement.

**Key Shift:** Addition of targeted morality and foreign-influence speech/association controls.

### Why this produces Endos 35 / Xenos 28

Further downward movement with clearer differential intensity on designated categories.

---

## **2022–Present: Wartime Speech Regime – Endos 22 / Xenos 18**

Post-invasion legislation on “discrediting the armed forces”, “fake news” about the military, and related measures produced the lowest post-1991 scores.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Criminalisation of speech deemed to discredit the armed forces or spread false information about the military.
* Intensified foreign-agent and extremism enforcement.
* Broad online and media controls.

**Key Shift:** Introduction of a comprehensive wartime speech-restriction layer.

### Why this produces Endos 22 / Xenos 18

Heavy additional weight of the wartime provisions on top of the prior architecture.
