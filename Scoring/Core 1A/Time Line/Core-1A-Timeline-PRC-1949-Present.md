# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in the People’s Republic of China (1949–Present)

## Introductory Overview

The PRC exhibits one of the lowest Core 1A trajectories among major states. From 1949 the Chinese Communist Party constructed a comprehensive system of ideological, administrative and technological control over speech, association, religion, movement and education. The Maoist period (especially the Cultural Revolution) represents extreme multi-domain restriction. The reform era after 1978 moderated some of the most chaotic mass-mobilisation controls while building a more systematic, technology-enabled apparatus. The Xi period has further intensified ideological and digital controls. Endos (core Han population in political good standing) experience a dense baseline; designated Xenos groups (Uyghurs, Tibetans, Falun Gong, certain religious and political dissidents) face markedly more intensive coercive treatment. Scores remain in the single digits to low teens throughout.

## **1949–1965: Construction of the System – Endos 18 / Xenos 10**

Establishment of Party control over media, association, religion and movement.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Comprehensive suppression of independent media and political speech.
* Land reform, campaign and class-struggle controls on association and expression.
* Early religious and ethnic controls.
* Household registration (hukou) movement restrictions.

**Key Shift:** Replacement of the Republican legal order with the Party-state restrictive architecture.

### Why this produces Endos 18 / Xenos 10

Multi-domain controls from the outset; differential intensity on class and early ethnic targets.

---

## **1966–1976: Cultural Revolution Peak – Endos 8 / Xenos 5**

Mass mobilisation, struggle sessions, and near-total collapse of residual personal autonomy in political and cultural domains.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Extreme ideological speech and association controls.
* Destruction of independent cultural and religious expression.
* Violence and administrative coercion as instruments of conformity.

**Key Shift:** Descent into the most intense phase of multi-domain restriction.

### Why this produces Endos 8 / Xenos 5

Near-total dominance of coercive ideological controls.

---

## **1978–2012: Reform-Era Systematisation – Endos 22 / Xenos 12**

Partial retreat from mass-mobilisation chaos accompanied by construction of a more regularised, technology-supported control system.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Continued comprehensive media and political-speech controls.
* Development of the internet control apparatus.
* Religious and ethnic management systems.
* Hukou and population controls retained.

**Key Shift:** Shift from chaotic mobilisation to systematic administrative and technological restriction.

### Why this produces Endos 22 / Xenos 12

Modest upward movement from the Cultural Revolution floor while the core architecture remains intact.

---

## **2013–Present: Xi-Era Intensification – Endos 12 / Xenos 8**

Re-emphasis on ideological conformity, expansion of digital surveillance and content control, and heightened pressure on designated groups.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Strengthened Party ideological controls over speech, education and culture.
* Advanced digital surveillance and social-credit-related instruments.
* Intensive controls in Xinjiang and Tibet.
* Zero-COVID period as an additional temporary layer of movement and association restriction.

**Key Shift:** Re-centralisation and technological deepening of the restrictive system.

### Why this produces Endos 12 / Xenos 8

Renewed ideological and digital intensity pulls the scores back toward the lower end of the post-1978 range.
