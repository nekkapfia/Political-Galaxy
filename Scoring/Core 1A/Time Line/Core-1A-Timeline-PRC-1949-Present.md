# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in the People’s Republic of China (1949–Present)

## Introductory Overview

The People’s Republic of China exhibits one of the lowest Core 1A trajectories among major states. From 1949 the Chinese Communist Party constructed a comprehensive system of ideological, administrative and technological control over speech, association, religion, movement and education. The Maoist period, and especially the Cultural Revolution, represents extreme multi-domain restriction. The reform era after 1978 moderated some of the most chaotic mass-mobilisation controls while building a more systematic, technology-enabled apparatus. The Xi period has further intensified ideological and digital controls. Endos (core Han population in political good standing) experience a dense baseline; designated Xenos groups (Uyghurs, Tibetans, Falun Gong practitioners, certain religious communities and political dissidents) face markedly more intensive coercive treatment. Scores remain in the single digits to low twenties throughout.

The long-term pattern is therefore one of continuous multi-domain restriction with internal variation in intensity and method: chaotic mass mobilisation in the high Maoist years, more regularised administrative and technological control in the reform era, and renewed ideological plus digital intensification under Xi. No period approaches high-autonomy comparative baselines.

## **1949–1965: Construction of the System – Endos 18 / Xenos 10**

The founding of the People’s Republic rapidly replaced residual Republican legal forms with a Party-directed system of control over media, association, religion and movement. Land reform, suppression of counter-revolutionaries, the Anti-Rightist Campaign and the early development of the household registration system created a multi-domain restrictive environment from the outset. Independent newspapers and political organisations were closed or absorbed.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Comprehensive suppression of independent media and political speech; all major publications and broadcasting brought under Party control.
* Campaigns against “counter-revolutionaries” involving mass arrests, public trials and executions.
* Anti-Rightist Campaign (1957–1959): official figures later acknowledged at least 550,000 people labelled as Rightists; independent estimates range from 1 to 2 million. Those labelled faced struggle sessions, loss of employment, internal exile or labour re-education.
* Early restrictions on unapproved religious practice and the beginning of systematic control over Buddhist, Christian and other communities through state-supervised patriotic associations.
* Introduction and expansion of the household registration (hukou) system that restricted internal movement and tied access to food, housing, education and employment to approved location.
* Class-based targeting of landlords, “rich peasants,” former Nationalist personnel and other designated categories, creating clear differential intensity.

The restrictive architecture is defined by the rapid construction of a Party monopoly over expression, association and movement, with explicit differential treatment of class and political targets. The cumulative multi-domain controls from the earliest years of the PRC produce low scores. Negative-Only Weighting records the full weight of the new system.

**Key Shift:** Replacement of the residual Republican legal and social order with the Party-state restrictive architecture across multiple domains of personal autonomy.

### Why this produces Endos 18 / Xenos 10

Multi-domain controls from the earliest years of the PRC produce low scores. The Xenos differential reflects the higher intensity directed at class enemies and political targets. Negative-Only Weighting applies.

## **1966–1976: Cultural Revolution Peak – Endos 8 / Xenos 5**

The Cultural Revolution, launched by the May 16 Circular of 1966, produced the most intensive phase of multi-domain restriction in PRC history. Red Guards attacked the “Four Olds,” destroying temples, churches, ancestral halls, books and cultural artefacts. Struggle sessions subjected millions to public humiliation, physical violence and forced confessions. Independent political expression was impossible; almost any statement could be labelled counter-revolutionary or revisionist.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Extreme ideological controls on speech and association; almost any expression could be labelled counter-revolutionary or revisionist.
* Struggle sessions, Red Guard violence and administrative detention as instruments of enforced conformity.
* Widespread destruction of temples, churches, ancestral halls and cultural artefacts under the “Destruction of the Four Olds” campaign.
* Forced relocations, rustication of urban youth, and severe disruption of ordinary movement and family autonomy.
* Class-background system (heritable “red” vs “black” categories) determined education access, employment, marriage prospects and physical safety.
* Formal legal protections were effectively suspended in favour of campaign justice.
* Differential intensity reached extreme levels against designated class, political, intellectual and religious targets.

The restrictive profile is defined by the near-total dominance of coercive ideological controls across speech, association, religion, movement and education. Scores reach the extreme low end of the Core 1A scale. Negative-Only Weighting and the Score 4 Floor are both relevant.

**Key Shift:** Descent into the most intensive phase of multi-domain restriction through mass mobilisation and the suspension of residual institutional constraints.

### Why this produces Endos 8 / Xenos 5

Near-total dominance of coercive ideological and campaign controls produces scores at the extreme low end of the Core 1A scale. The Xenos differential reflects the additional lethality and intensity directed at designated categories. Negative-Only Weighting applies.

## **1978–2012: Reform-Era Systematisation – Endos 22 / Xenos 12**

The reform era moderated the chaotic mass-mobilisation methods of the Cultural Revolution while constructing a more regularised, technology-supported system of control. Some space for private economic activity and limited personal expression emerged, but the core political, media, religious and movement controls remained intact and were progressively modernised. The Great Firewall and real-name regimes extended restrictive capacity into the digital domain.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Continued comprehensive control of media, publishing and political speech; no independent opposition media or parties permitted.
* Development of the internet control apparatus (Great Firewall, real-name registration, content censorship).
* Ongoing restrictions on unapproved religious practice, house churches and non-state-sanctioned belief systems; sustained campaign against Falun Gong after 1999.
* Retention and modernisation of the hukou system and related population controls, with gradual but limited reforms allowing some mobility under administrative oversight.
* Differential intensity continued against Tibetans, Uyghurs, Falun Gong practitioners and political dissidents, with episodic intensifications.
* The abandonment of continuous mass campaign terror produced a modest upward movement relative to the Cultural Revolution floor, but the underlying multi-domain architecture was never dismantled.

The restrictive profile is characterised by the shift from chaotic mobilisation to systematic administrative and technological restriction, with only limited expansion of personal autonomy in non-political domains. Negative-Only Weighting records the modest upward movement from the Cultural Revolution floor while the core architecture of media, political, religious and movement controls remained intact.

**Key Shift:** Shift from chaotic mass-mobilisation controls to a more regularised administrative and technological system of restriction, accompanied by limited non-political liberalisation.

### Why this produces Endos 22 / Xenos 12

Modest upward movement from the Cultural Revolution floor under Negative-Only Weighting, reflecting the end of continuous campaign terror. The core architecture remains intact, keeping scores low. The Xenos differential persists.

## **2013–Present: Xi-Era Intensification – Endos 12 / Xenos 8**

The period since 2013 has been marked by re-emphasis on ideological conformity, expansion of digital surveillance and content control, strengthened Party leadership over all domains of society, and heightened pressure on designated groups, particularly in Xinjiang and Tibet. The zero-COVID episode constituted an additional temporary layer of extreme movement and association restriction.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Strengthened Party ideological controls over speech, education, culture and online content; tightened censorship and propaganda discipline.
* Advanced digital surveillance systems, social-credit-related instruments, and comprehensive real-name and content-control regimes.
* Intensive controls in Xinjiang (re-education camps, movement restrictions, cultural and religious suppression) and continued tight control in Tibet, creating extreme differential intensity.
* Zero-COVID policies (2020–2022) that imposed severe temporary restrictions on movement, association and daily life across large parts of the country, including prolonged city-wide lockdowns.
* Continued suppression of independent civil society, human-rights lawyers, and unapproved religious practice.
* The cumulative effect is a return toward the lower end of the post-1978 range through ideological and technological intensification.

The restrictive profile is defined by the combination of renewed ideological mobilisation, sophisticated digital control tools, and episodic extreme measures such as zero-COVID. Negative-Only Weighting records the renewed intensity as a downward movement from the earlier reform-era level.

**Key Shift:** Re-centralisation and technological deepening of the restrictive system under Xi, reversing some of the limited non-political openings of the earlier reform era.

### Why this produces Endos 12 / Xenos 8

Renewed ideological and digital intensity, plus the zero-COVID overlay, pulls the scores back toward the lower end of the post-1978 range. Negative-Only Weighting applies. The Xenos differential remains pronounced because of the additional coercive apparatus directed at designated ethnic, religious and political groups.
