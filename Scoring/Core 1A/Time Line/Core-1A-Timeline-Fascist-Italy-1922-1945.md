# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in Fascist Italy (1922–1945)

## Introductory Overview

Fascist Italy constructed a progressive system of speech, association and political controls after the March on Rome, reaching high intensity by the late 1930s and extreme levels during the war and the Italian Social Republic. The trajectory is one of cumulative densification rather than an instantaneous totalitarian break of the Nazi type. Differential targeting of political opponents, later of Jews, and of other designated groups is present. Scores fall steadily across the three eras as the regime’s restrictive architecture expanded and radicalised.

## **1922–1925: Early Consolidation – Endos 45 / Xenos 35**

The transition from liberal Italy to the Fascist regime proceeded through a combination of legal manoeuvre, intimidation and selective violence. Opposition press and parties came under increasing pressure, but the full institutional apparatus of the later regime was not yet in place.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Pressure on opposition newspapers and political parties through violence, intimidation and administrative measures.
* Acerbo Law and electoral manipulation that undermined competitive politics.
* Early use of squadrist violence and administrative sanctions against opponents.
* Residual liberal legal forms continued to exist but were progressively hollowed out.
* Differential intensity against socialist, communist and other political opponents was already clear.
* Ordinary criminal law continued to operate for non-political matters.

The restrictive architecture is defined by the beginning of the destruction of the liberal competitive system rather than its completed replacement.

**Key Shift:** Beginning of the destruction of the liberal multi-party and independent-press environment through a combination of legal and extralegal means.

### Why this produces Endos 45 / Xenos 35

Partial but already material reduction in political expression and association rights produces mid-range scores for an interwar European dictatorship. The Xenos differential reflects higher intensity against political opponents.

## **1926–1938: Full Fascist System – Endos 28 / Xenos 18**

The exceptional decrees of 1926, the establishment of the one-party state, the corporative order, and the 1938 racial legislation completed the regime’s restrictive architecture. Independent political life was eliminated and differential treatment of Jews was introduced.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Exceptional decrees that banned opposition parties, suppressed independent trade unions and established special tribunals.
* Comprehensive press controls and the elimination of independent political journalism.
* One-party monopoly and the corporative restructuring of association.
* 1938 racial laws introducing formal discrimination against Jews and creating a clear dual status.
* Continued ordinary criminal law for non-political matters alongside political jurisdiction.
* The cumulative effect was a dense restrictive system, though less totalising in daily life than the contemporaneous Soviet or later Nazi orders.

The restrictive profile is defined by the completion of the legal and institutional apparatus of the Fascist regime and the introduction of racial legislation.

**Key Shift:** Completion of the legal and institutional apparatus of the regime and the introduction of formal racial discrimination.

### Why this produces Endos 28 / Xenos 18

Dense restrictive architecture with emerging racial differential produces lower scores than the early consolidation phase. Negative-Only Weighting applies.

## **1939–1945: War and Radicalisation – Endos 15 / Xenos 8**

Wartime mobilisation and the Italian Social Republic’s more extreme measures after 1943 produced the highest intensity of restriction in the Fascist period. Collaboration in deportations and intensified political repression marked the final phase.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Total war controls on speech, movement and labour.
* Intensified political repression under the Italian Social Republic.
* Collaboration in the deportation of Jews and other measures of racial persecution.
* Residual space for ordinary private life narrowed further under wartime and RSI conditions.
* Ordinary criminal law continued in limited domains but was subordinate to political and military priorities.

The restrictive profile is defined by wartime mobilisation combined with the radicalisation of the RSI period.

**Key Shift:** Descent into wartime mobilisation and the more extreme repressive practices of the Italian Social Republic.

### Why this produces Endos 15 / Xenos 8

Maximum intensity of the Fascist period produces the lowest scores in the series. The Xenos differential reflects the additional measures directed at Jews and political opponents under wartime and RSI conditions.
