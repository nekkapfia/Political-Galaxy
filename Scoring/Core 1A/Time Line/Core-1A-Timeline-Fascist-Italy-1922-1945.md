# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in Fascist Italy (1922–1945)

## Introductory Overview

Fascist Italy constructed a progressive system of speech, association and political controls after the March on Rome, reaching high intensity by the late 1930s and extreme levels during the war and the Italian Social Republic. The trajectory is one of cumulative densification rather than an instantaneous totalitarian break of the Nazi type. Differential targeting of political opponents, later of Jews, and of other designated groups is present. Scores fall steadily across the three eras as the regime’s restrictive architecture expanded and radicalised.

## **1922–1925: Early Consolidation – Endos 45 / Xenos 35**

The transition from liberal Italy to the Fascist regime after the March on Rome proceeded through a combination of legal manoeuvre, intimidation by Fascist squads, and selective administrative pressure. Opposition newspapers and parties came under increasing attack, but the full institutional apparatus of the later one-party state was not yet complete. Residual liberal legal forms, including parliamentary procedure and elements of judicial independence, continued to exist while being progressively hollowed out.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Systematic pressure on opposition newspapers through squadrist violence, office occupations, seizures of issues, and administrative harassment of editors and printers.
* Acerbo Law of 1923, which altered the electoral system to award a large majority premium to the leading list and thereby undermined genuine competitive multi-party politics.
* Widespread use of Fascist squad violence against socialist, communist, Catholic and other political opponents, often with limited or no restraint by state authorities.
* Progressive restriction of association rights for non-Fascist political and labour organisations through a mixture of intimidation and administrative measures.
* Residual liberal legal forms (parliament, courts, ordinary criminal law) continued to operate for non-political matters but were increasingly subordinated to the regime’s political priorities.
* Clear differential intensity against political opponents of the regime, while ordinary citizens outside political organisations retained more of the pre-Fascist legal baseline.

The restrictive architecture of this opening phase is defined by the beginning of the destruction of the liberal competitive system rather than by its completed replacement. Independent political expression and association remained formally possible but were already subject to systematic private violence and administrative pressure that the state failed to restrain and in many cases tolerated or encouraged. The Acerbo Law and the growing atmosphere of intimidation reduced the practical value of residual electoral and press freedoms. Ordinary criminal prohibitions on violence continued to apply in principle, yet their selective non-enforcement against Fascist squads created a de-facto dual standard. The period therefore sits at a mid-range level for an interwar European dictatorship: the multi-party and independent-press environment had been severely damaged but not yet extinguished by comprehensive statutory prohibition.

**Key Shift:** Beginning of the destruction of the liberal multi-party and independent-press environment through a combination of legal manipulation (Acerbo Law), systematic squadrist intimidation, and selective administrative pressure.

### Why this produces Endos 45 / Xenos 35

Partial but already material reduction in political expression and association rights produces mid-range scores for an interwar European dictatorship. The residual liberal legal forms and the incomplete character of the one-party system prevent a steeper drop. The Xenos differential of 35 reflects the higher intensity of violence and administrative targeting already directed at socialist, communist and other political opponents. Negative-Only Weighting applies; the score records the cumulative weight of the restrictions that existed rather than any positive credit for incomplete totalisation.

## **1926–1938: Full Fascist System – Endos 28 / Xenos 18**

The exceptional decrees of 1925–1926 (leggi fascistissime) completed the legal destruction of the liberal order. Opposition parties were banned, independent trade unions suppressed, and a Special Tribunal for the Defense of the State created to try political offences. Press controls required government approval of newspaper directors and barred anti-regime journalists from the profession. The one-party state and the corporative restructuring of association were established. In 1938 the Racial Laws introduced formal discrimination against Jews, excluding them from public employment, education, military service and imposing limits on property ownership.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Leggi fascistissime (1925–1926) that banned opposition parties, suppressed independent trade unions, expanded police powers and imposed strict press controls.
* Requirement that newspaper directors receive government recognition; anti-regime journalists excluded from the profession.
* Creation of the Special Tribunal for the Defense of the State and the use of confino (internal exile) against political dissidents.
* One-party monopoly and the corporative restructuring of association that eliminated independent collective organisation.
* 1938 Racial Laws introducing formal discrimination against Jews (exclusion from public employment, education, military service, property limits).
* Ordinary criminal law continued to operate for non-political matters alongside the new political jurisdiction.

The restrictive architecture of this period is defined by the completion of the legal and institutional apparatus of the Fascist regime and the introduction of formal racial legislation. Independent political life was extinguished by statute rather than merely by violence. Press and association were brought under comprehensive administrative control. The Racial Laws of 1938 added a stable, discretely targetable out-group differential. Ordinary protective criminal law remained in force for private violence, but the dominant reality for political and public expression was the new one-party and corporative order. The cumulative weight of these measures produces a clear downward movement from the incomplete consolidation of 1922–1925.

**Key Shift:** Completion of the legal and institutional apparatus of the regime through the exceptional decrees and the introduction of formal racial discrimination in 1938.

### Why this produces Endos 28 / Xenos 18

Dense restrictive architecture with an emerging racial differential produces lower scores than the early consolidation phase. Negative-Only Weighting applies. The Xenos differential of 18 reflects the additional formal disabilities imposed on Jews after 1938 together with the continued higher intensity against residual political opponents.

## **1939–1945: War and Radicalisation – Endos 15 / Xenos 8**

Wartime mobilisation tightened controls on speech, movement and labour across the population. After the fall of Mussolini in July 1943 and the establishment of the Italian Social Republic in the north, political repression intensified. Collaboration with German forces in the deportation of Jews and the persecution of political opponents marked the final phase. Residual space for ordinary private life narrowed further under wartime and RSI conditions.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Total war controls on speech, movement, labour and association imposed on the entire population.
* Intensified political repression under the Italian Social Republic after September 1943, including expanded use of special courts and summary measures.
* Collaboration in the deportation of Jews and other measures of racial persecution that produced extreme differential intensity.
* Further narrowing of residual private autonomy under the combined pressures of war economy and RSI radicalisation.
* Ordinary criminal law continued in limited non-political domains but was subordinated to political and military priorities.
* The highest intensity of restriction in the Fascist period was reached in these years.

The restrictive architecture of the final phase is defined by wartime mobilisation combined with the radicalisation of the Italian Social Republic. The residual liberal forms that had survived into the 1930s were extinguished under the dual pressure of total war and the more extreme RSI regime. Differential intensity against Jews and political opponents reached its peak through deportation and open collaboration with German extermination policy. Ordinary protective criminal law retained only marginal relevance. The cumulative multi-domain and group-targeted coercion produces the lowest scores in the Fascist series.

**Key Shift:** Descent into wartime mobilisation and the more extreme repressive practices of the Italian Social Republic, including collaboration in racial deportation.

### Why this produces Endos 15 / Xenos 8

Maximum intensity of the Fascist period produces the lowest scores in the series. The Xenos differential reflects the additional measures directed at Jews and political opponents under wartime and RSI conditions. Negative-Only Weighting applies throughout.
