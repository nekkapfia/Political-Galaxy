# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in Fascist Italy (1922–1945)

## Introductory Overview

Fascist Italy constructed a progressive system of speech, association and political controls after the March on Rome, reaching high intensity by the late 1930s and extreme levels during the war and the Italian Social Republic. The trajectory is one of cumulative densification rather than an instantaneous totalitarian break of the Nazi type. Differential targeting of political opponents, later of Jews, and of other designated groups is present.

## **1922–1925: Early Consolidation – Endos 45 / Xenos 35**

Transition from liberal Italy to the Fascist regime through legal and extralegal means.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Pressure on opposition press and parties.
* Acerbo Law and electoral manipulation.
* Early use of violence and administrative measures against opponents.

**Key Shift:** Beginning of the destruction of the liberal competitive system.

### Why this produces Endos 45 / Xenos 35
Partial but already material reduction in political expression and association rights.

## **1926–1938: Full Fascist System – Endos 28 / Xenos 18**

Exceptional decrees, ban on opposition, corporative state, and 1938 racial legislation.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Comprehensive press and association controls.
* One-party monopoly.
* Anti-Jewish racial laws introducing clear differential treatment.

**Key Shift:** Completion of the legal and institutional apparatus of the regime.

### Why this produces Endos 28 / Xenos 18
Dense restrictive architecture with emerging racial differential.

## **1939–1945: War and Radicalisation – Endos 15 / Xenos 8**

Wartime mobilisation and the Italian Social Republic’s more extreme measures.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Collaboration in deportations.

**Key Shift:** Descent into wartime and quasi-genocidal radicalisation.

### Why this produces Endos 15 / Xenos 8
