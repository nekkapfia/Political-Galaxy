# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in Germany (1998–2026)

## Introductory Overview

Contemporary Germany operates under a dense constitutional and statutory framework of hate-speech (Volksverhetzung), online content regulation (NetzDG and successors), and public-order powers that keeps Core 1A scores in the mid-to-low range by comparative democratic standards. The period from 1998 is marked by continuity of the post-reunification restrictive architecture with incremental expansions in online and identity-based domains. COVID produced a temporary further drop. No fundamental liberalisation of the core speech offences has occurred.

## **1998–2015: Post-Reunification Framework – Base Score: 58**

Stable operation of the Basic Law dignity and hate-speech regime together with public-order powers.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Volksverhetzung and related criminal speech offences.
* Public-order and assembly restrictions.
* Early internet content pressures.

**Key Shift:** Continuity of the established restrictive baseline into the digital era.

### Why this produces a final score of 58
Cumulative weight of the long-standing speech and dignity architecture.

---

## **2016–2019: NetzDG Expansion – Base Score: 55**

Network Enforcement Act and related measures increased platform liability for illegal content.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* NetzDG duties on platforms to remove illegal content rapidly.
* Continued enforcement of criminal hate-speech provisions.
* Rising institutional speech norms.

**Key Shift:** Addition of a comprehensive online intermediary liability layer.

### Why this produces a final score of 55
Modest further downward movement from the online layer.

---

## **2020–early 2022: COVID Block – Base Score: 40**

Nationwide and Land-level movement, gathering and medical restrictions.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Lockdowns, contact restrictions, gathering bans.
* Vaccine and access mandates.

**Key Shift:** Temporary multi-domain emergency regime.

### Why this produces a final score of 40
Protocol-consistent block scoring.

---

## **Mid-2022 onward: Post-COVID Continuity – Base Score: 56**

Lifting of COVID measures with retention of the pre-existing speech and online framework.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis
* Termination of the most sweeping COVID restrictions.
* Continued NetzDG/successor and Volksverhetzung architecture.

**Key Shift:** Removal of the temporary COVID layer.

### Why this produces a final score of 56
Recovery limited by the persistent restrictive baseline.
