# Core 1A: Eras of Personal Autonomy in the United States (1945–Present)

## Introductory Overview

The United States maintains a structurally higher Core 1A baseline than most European systems because of the First Amendment. From 1945 the trajectory is one of gradual expansion of administrative, institutional, and platform-mediated controls rather than the dense statutory hate-speech codes characteristic of the UK or France. The Warren and Burger Court eras reinforced constitutional speech protections. Later decades saw the rise of campus speech codes, workplace harassment doctrines, and, after 2010, intense pressure for platform content moderation. COVID produced a temporary multi-domain restriction. The post-2020 period features polarisation over the scope of private and administrative speech controls, but the constitutional floor keeps scores higher than in comparator democracies. No system-wide Endos/Xenos split of the French type is scored; differentials appear mainly in institutional enforcement intensity.

## **1945–1969: High Constitutional Baseline – Base Score: 85**

Post-war America operated under a robust First Amendment regime with relatively few content-based statutory restrictions on adult speech.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Narrow categories of unprotected speech (true threats, incitement, obscenity under then-prevailing tests).
* Core criminal prohibitions on violence.
* Limited federal administrative speech controls.
* McCarthy-era loyalty and association pressures existed but were partially constrained by later judicial decisions within the period.

**Key Shift:** Continuity of the high First Amendment floor after 1945.

### Why this produces a final score of 85

Strong constitutional protection plus limited statutory content controls yields a high opening score.

---

## **1970–1990: Institutional and Workplace Expansion – Base Score: 80**

Growth of campus speech codes, workplace harassment doctrine, and related institutional controls began to add non-statutory but effective restrictions on expression in key settings.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Expansion of hostile-environment and harassment doctrines affecting workplace and educational speech.
* Proliferation of campus speech codes.
* Retention of the core First Amendment baseline for pure government restriction.

**Key Shift:** Rise of institutional (non-criminal) speech regulation in universities and employment.

### Why this produces a final score of 80

Modest downward adjustment from the new institutional layer.

---

## **1991–2010: Continued Institutional Density – Base Score: 76**

Further entrenchment of institutional codes and early debates over internet regulation.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Persistent campus and workplace speech regimes.
* Early attempts at online content regulation (many struck down or narrowed).
* Retention of constitutional floor.

**Key Shift:** Consolidation of the institutional layer without major new federal criminal speech offences.

### Why this produces a final score of 76

Continued cumulative weight of institutional controls.

---

## **2011–2019: Platform Moderation Era – Base Score: 70**

Intense social and political pressure on major platforms to moderate content, combined with continued institutional codes.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Platform content-moderation practices driven by public and advertiser pressure (not direct state prohibition, but relevant to the lived restrictive environment when state actors amplify them).
* Ongoing campus and workplace regimes.
* Limited new federal criminal speech legislation.

**Key Shift:** Platform-level content controls become a central feature of the expression environment.

### Why this produces a final score of 70

Additional downward pressure from the platform layer.

---

## **2020–early 2022: COVID Restrictions Block – Base Score: 45**

Federal, state and local movement, gathering and medical mandates.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Stay-at-home orders, business closures, gathering limits.
* Vaccine and masking mandates conditioning access to activities.
* Variation by state produced some internal differentiation, but the national cumulative weight was substantial.

**Key Shift:** Temporary multi-domain emergency restrictions.

### Why this produces a final score of 45

Protocol-consistent block scoring.

---

## **Mid-2022 onward: Contested Recovery – Base Score: 72**

Lifting of most COVID measures and renewed political contestation over platform and institutional speech controls.

### Core 1A Relevant Restrictions / Elements – Expanded Analysis

* Termination of the most sweeping COVID mandates.
* Ongoing litigation and legislation over platform moderation, campus speech, and government jawboning.
* Retention of the long-standing institutional layer.

**Key Shift:** End of COVID superstructure amid heightened polarisation over speech governance.

### Why this produces a final score of 72

Recovery from COVID plus the persistent but non-criminal institutional and platform environment produces 72 – still below the mid-century high but well above European comparators.
