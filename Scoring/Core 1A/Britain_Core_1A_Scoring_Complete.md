## 1. Rule & Methodology Changes (Authoritative & Locked)

### Current-Policy Basis (Locked Rule)
All scoring for UK parties, leaders, and ideologies is performed on **current observable policies** (what they are actively maintaining or promoting in the real world). Aspirational or "unconstrained total control" goals are **not** used unless explicitly instructed by the user for a specific entity. This ensures full methodological equivalence with the USA and Germany documents.

### Strict Negative-Only Weighting (Locked Rule)
Scores reflect only the presence and weight of invasive/restrictive policies. Removal, opposition, or repeal of restrictions grants **no positive credit**. "We will repeal X" or "we oppose Y" is rhetoric only and does not raise the score.

### Johnson COVID vs Non-COVID Split (Locked)
Boris Johnson’s tenure is split into two distinct eras for scoring:
- **COVID Era (primarily 2020–early 2022)**: Dominated by Infection Protection Act measures (contact bans, curfews, business/school closures, movement restrictions) scored at the extreme low end.
- **Excluding COVID Era (2019 & late 2022 tail)**: Pre- and post-peak policies scored separately without the COVID drag.

This split is required because the COVID policies represent an unprecedented, uniform, and temporary loss of daily autonomy that would otherwise distort the overall assessment of the era.

### Future Roundup Rule (Locked)
When a politician, party, era, or ideology is discussed in the thread but not explicitly scored with an independent entry, it will be noted as "discussed and scored" with the specific policies and rationale from the thread. All scores remain strictly negative-only.

### No Positive Buffs for Repeal/Opposition (Locked)
Repealing or opposing previous restrictions is never scored as a positive factor. Only active, maintained invasive policies lower the score.

### No Endos/Xenos Split for Standard UK Parties (Locked)
For mainstream UK parties and leaders, no formal Endos/Xenos split is applied in holistic scores unless a policy explicitly creates permanent legal divergence between in-group and out-group treatment (e.g., certain immigration or citizenship rules). Differential treatment is noted in rationales but does not usually trigger separate holistic scores.

## 3. All Scored Policies — Full Entries with Expanded Justifications

### Pre-1998 / Foundational Policies (retained and expanded)

#### General Police and Criminal Investigation Powers (StPO equivalent — Police and Criminal Evidence Act elements)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Standard procedural powers of search, seizure, interrogation, arrest, detention and limited surveillance in criminal investigations, subject to reasonable suspicion, judicial warrants and proportionality requirements.  
**Core 1A Score:** 85  
**Rationale:** Routine criminal justice tools for prevention of harm to others. Extreme procedural limitations (judicial oversight, rights protections, proportionality) place it firmly in the protection-of-vulnerable / prevention-of-harm band.

#### Public-Nuisance Regulations (Public Order Act elements, local ordinances)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Prohibition of behaviours such as excessive noise, littering, obstruction of public ways and disorderly conduct that disturb public order, enforced by administrative fines or criminal sanctions in repeated cases.  
**Core 1A Score:** 64  
**Rationale:** Anti-social behaviour and public-order restrictions aimed at betterment of society and prevention of nuisance rather than direct ideological enforcement.

#### Restrictive Abortion Rules (pre-2000 framework, with later reforms)
**Neutered Base Policy:** General criminal prohibition on abortion with exemptions only for medical necessity, rape, or after mandatory counselling and waiting period in first trimester (with subsequent reforms).  
**Core 1A Score:** 82  
**Rationale:** Ban on abortion with explicit carve-outs for life/health of mother and rape cases. Fits “safe, legal and rare” style framework protecting the fetus as vulnerable while allowing narrow exceptions. Places it in the 82–89 protecting vulnerable individuals band.

#### Criminalisation of Most Recreational Drugs (Misuse of Drugs Act framework)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Comprehensive criminal prohibition on possession, consumption, manufacture and trade of listed narcotic substances.  
**Core 1A Score:** 72  
**Rationale:** Primary restriction targets self-regarding acts of possession and consumption (prevention of rendering self pseudo-vulnerable) combined with trade prohibitions.

### Key Modern Policies (from thread scoring)

#### Mandatory EDI / Sensitivity / Unconscious Bias Training (across institutions)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Requirement for institutions, corporations, education, and government bodies to conduct mandatory training sessions that instruct participants their existing beliefs on identity are biased/harmful and must be corrected.  
**Core 1A Score:** 22  
**Rationale:** Direct ideological retraining / thought control. The single heaviest and most consistent downward driver across all progressive packages. Matches the 19–27 ideological reprogramming / thought control band.

#### Self-ID / Self-Declaration Gender Recognition (progressive model)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Legal recognition of acquired gender based on self-declaration with minimal or zero gatekeeping, medical evidence, or waiting periods; often linked to compulsory institutional recognition.  
**Core 1A Score:** 44  
**Rationale:** Early ideological/identity-based control. Creates institutional rules requiring conformity in speech and self-presentation. Fits the 44–50 band.

#### Compulsory Institutional Recognition of Self-Declared Gender
**Neutered Base Policy:** Requirement for services, sports, prisons, workplaces, and official documents to treat self-declared gender as legal sex, often with limited or no exceptions.  
**Core 1A Score:** 42  
**Rationale:** Institutional compulsion and identity-based control. Strong downward driver when combined with EDI and self-ID.

#### Non-Crime Hate Incident Recording and Institutional Monitoring
**Neutered Base Policy:** Recording and institutional monitoring of incidents that are not criminal but are perceived as motivated by hostility toward protected characteristics, often leading to institutional alignment requirements.  
**Core 1A Score:** 26  
**Rationale:** Police-state monitoring of deviant behaviour and ideological enforcement. Fits the 19–27 / 28–36 range depending on enforcement intensity.

#### Racial and Religious Hatred Act (in practice / Blair-era usage)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Criminal offences for stirring up racial or religious hatred, with documented usage patterns that extended to suppressing legitimate discussion of certain issues.  
**Core 1A Score:** 24 (in documented usage)  
**Rationale:** In practice functioned as ideological suppression rather than pure betterment of society. Lowered from pure betterment band due to enforcement patterns.

#### ASBOs / Anti-Social Behaviour Orders (and successors)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Civil orders restricting behaviour deemed anti-social, with criminal penalties for breach; broad and discretionary application.  
**Core 1A Score:** 62  
**Rationale:** Betterment of society / nuisance control. Fits the 60–69 band as a targeted restriction on behaviour considered damaging to social order.

#### RIPA (Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act) and successors (DRIPA, etc.)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Mass communications data retention and investigatory powers allowing bulk collection of metadata and communications data without individualised suspicion.  
**Core 1A Score:** 30  
**Rationale:** Police-state / surveillance element. Systematic, suspicionless collection of personal data. Fits the 28–36 ideological enforcement / police-state band.

#### Prevent Strategy / Counter-Extremism Institutional Monitoring
**Neutered Base Policy:** Requirement for public institutions (schools, universities, NHS, etc.) to monitor and report on extremism/radicalisation, with institutional alignment requirements.  
**Core 1A Score:** 25  
**Rationale:** Institutional ideological alignment / thought-control adjacent monitoring. Fits the lower end of the 19–27 / 28–36 range.

#### Online Safety Act 2023 (and related content moderation regimes)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Platform duties for illegal/harmful content, age verification, safety-by-design, and expanded content moderation regimes with significant penalties.  
**Core 1A Score:** 24  
**Rationale:** Expanded expression sanctioning / new content moderation regime. State-mandated proactive censorship through corporate proxy. Fits the 19–27 ideological enforcement band.

#### COVID-19 Restrictions / Lockdowns and related compulsion (Johnson era)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Unprecedented temporary restrictions on movement, association, education, work, and bodily/medical autonomy under the Infection Protection Act (contact bans, curfews, business/school closures, vaccine mandates in some contexts).  
**Core 1A Score:** 18 (COVID Era dominant driver)  
**Rationale:** Extreme, uniform, and prolonged loss of daily personal autonomy for the general population. One of the lowest mechanical effects recorded in post-war UK history. Temporally isolated but correctly pulls the COVID-era holistic score into the low 40s (or lower when dominant).

#### Race Relations Act 1976 (and related betterment measures)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Made racial discrimination unlawful in employment, housing, goods/services and education; created positive duties on public bodies in some contexts.  
**Core 1A Score:** 65  
**Rationale:** Betterment of society / protection against exploitation. Does not involve mandatory ideological retraining. Top-end of the 60–69 band.

#### Official Secrets Act (various amendments, including 1989)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Extremely broad criminal offences for unauthorised disclosure of official information, with significant penalties.  
**Core 1A Score:** 97  
**Rationale:** System self-defence law. Explicitly in the 97–98 sub-band for laws whose purpose is to allow the system to protect itself from destruction. Not a general speech restriction.
