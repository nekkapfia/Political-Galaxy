### Broad Summary

The People’s Republic of China operates under a single-party system in which the Chinese Communist Party exercises comprehensive, multi-domain control over personal autonomy. There is no competitive multi-party system of the kind analysed in democratic countries. The CCP’s institutional and legal framework imposes pervasive direct restrictions on speech, association, religious practice, movement, education content, and online communication. Endos (core Han population in good political standing) experience a dense baseline of controls; Xenos groups (designated ethnic, religious, and political out-groups) face markedly more intense and coercive restrictions. The score range is therefore extremely low by comparative Core 1A standards, reflecting the near-total absence of protected voluntary individual action in politically sensitive domains.

# Chinese Communist Party (CCP)

The CCP maintains a system of comprehensive ideological, technological, and administrative control over expression, association, religion, and movement.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Extensive criminal and administrative prohibitions on speech critical of the Party, leadership, or official ideology (including online).
* Comprehensive real-name registration, surveillance, and content-control systems for all major online platforms and communications.
* Severe restrictions on unapproved religious practice, house churches, and non-state-sanctioned belief systems.
* Targeted, high-intensity controls on Uyghur, Tibetan, and other designated populations (re-education, movement, cultural/religious expression).
* Strict limits on independent association, civil-society organisation, and public assembly.
* Ideological education and content controls throughout the education system and media.
* Exit and internal movement controls that can be activated on political or stability grounds.

The cumulative weight of these direct restrictions places the CCP at the extreme low end of the Core 1A scale. The Endos/Xenos differential is required because designated out-groups receive systematically more intensive coercive treatment.

### Why this produces Endos 12 / Xenos 8

Negative-Only Weighting applied to the multi-domain system of speech, association, religious, movement, and ideological controls produces scores in the single digits to low teens. The Xenos score is lower still because of the additional intensity directed at stable, discretely targetable out-groups. No positive credit is available under the rules.
