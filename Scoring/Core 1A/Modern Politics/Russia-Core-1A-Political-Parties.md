### Broad Summary

The Russian party system operates inside a managed political space in which the dominant United Russia party and the systemic opposition accept or actively support a dense framework of speech, association, “foreign agent”, extremism, and public-morality restrictions. Genuine opposition forces capable of challenging the core restrictive architecture have been marginalised or eliminated. Scores are therefore clustered at the low end of the Core 1A scale. Endos/Xenos differentials appear where additional intensity is directed at designated categories (LGBTQ expression, certain ethnic or political dissidents, “foreign agents”). No major party proposes meaningful repeal of the key restrictive statutes.

---

# United Russia

United Russia is the dominant party of power and the primary vehicle for the current restrictive legal and administrative regime.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Support for and expansion of laws on “discrediting the armed forces”, “fake news” about the military, and related wartime speech restrictions.
* “Foreign agent” and “undesirable organisation” regimes that restrict association and expression.
* Extremism and public-morality legislation used against political and LGBTQ expression.
* Comprehensive online content controls and platform liability rules.
* Retention of the full post-2012 and post-2022 restrictive architecture.

### Why this produces Endos 28 / Xenos 22

Heavy cumulative weight of speech, association, and targeted morality/extremism controls under Negative-Only Weighting. Differential intensity on designated categories produces the Xenos adjustment.

---

# Communist Party of the Russian Federation (CPRF)

CPRF operates as systemic opposition while accepting the core restrictive framework.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Retention of the existing speech, foreign-agent, and extremism rules.
* Limited additional emphasis on certain ideological controls.

### Why this produces Endos 32 / Xenos 28

Slightly higher than United Russia only because of less active expansion; still deeply restrictive.

---

# Liberal Democratic Party of Russia (LDPR) and A Just Russia – For Truth

Both are systemic parties that do not challenge the fundamental speech and association restrictions.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Acceptance of the wartime and foreign-agent speech regime.
* Nationalist or social-conservative emphases that do not liberalise Core 1A domains.

### Why this produces scores in the high 20s to low 30s

Baseline restrictive weight dominates.

---

# New People

New People offers mildly less restrictive rhetoric on some economic and administrative issues but does not propose repeal of the core speech and association controls.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Limited critique of over-broad enforcement within the existing legal framework.
* No programme for dismantling foreign-agent, extremism, or wartime speech laws.

### Why this produces Endos 35 / Xenos N/A

Marginally higher autonomy language produces a small upward difference under Negative-Only Weighting; no material split.
