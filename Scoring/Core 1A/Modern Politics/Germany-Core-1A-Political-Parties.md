### Broad Summary

The German party system on Core 1A is shaped by the dense post-war and post-reunification hate-speech, Volksverhetzung, and online-content framework (NetzDG legacy and successors). AfD and FDP occupy the higher-autonomy positions by opposing further expansion of speech offences and emphasising classical free-expression protections, while still operating inside the existing constitutional and statutory limits. CDU/CSU maintains a centrist retention profile. SPD, Greens, and Die Linke apply greater downward pressure through defence or expansion of identity-based speech controls, online platform duties, and institutional anti-discrimination measures. Endos/Xenos differentials appear primarily where parties intensify scrutiny of speech critical of Islam, migration, or certain identity categories. The overall system remains constrained by constitutional dignity and hate-speech provisions that no major party proposes to dismantle.

# Alternative für Deutschland (AfD)

AfD prioritises reduction of speech restrictions, opposition to further NetzDG-style platform duties, and resistance to identity-based expansion of hate-speech offences.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Opposition to expansion of Volksverhetzung and related hate-speech offences.
* Calls for narrowing or reform of online platform liability and content-removal duties.
* Resistance to institutional speech codes and DEI-style controls on expression.
* Retention of core constitutional limits on speech that incites violence or denies recognised historical crimes (baseline).
* Differential emphasis on speech critical of Islam or migration policy as protected political expression.

### Why this produces Endos 72 / Xenos 55

Negative-Only Weighting on retained constitutional baseline plus opposition to further expansion yields Endos 72. Limited Xenos differential reflects selective intensity around certain identity-critical speech.

# Bündnis 90/Die Grünen

The Greens support robust hate-speech enforcement, platform duties, and institutional measures against discrimination and “hate”.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Defence and potential expansion of hate-speech and online-content rules.
* Strong institutional and educational anti-discrimination speech controls.
* Support for platform accountability for harmful and identity-hostile content.

### Why this produces Endos 48 / Xenos 42

Cumulative identity-based and online restrictions produce the lower scores with a modest differential.

# Christlich Demokratische Union (CDU/CSU)

CDU/CSU retains the existing framework while offering moderate free-speech language and resistance to the most expansive Green/Left proposals.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Retention of Volksverhetzung, online-safety, and public-order speech rules.
* Moderate emphasis on political expression protections within constitutional limits.

### Why this produces Endos 65 / Xenos N/A

Baseline retention under Negative-Only Weighting.

---

# Freie Demokratische Partei (FDP)

FDP maintains a classical-liberal profile on expression, opposing over-broad platform and hate-speech expansions while accepting core constitutional limits.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Opposition to further tightening of online content duties.
* Defence of political and scientific speech within existing limits.
* Retention of core hate-speech and dignity-based restrictions.

### Why this produces Endos 70 / Xenos N/A

Higher autonomy relative to left parties through resistance to expansion.

---

# Sozialdemokratische Partei Deutschlands (SPD)

SPD defends the existing hate-speech and online-safety architecture and supports institutional anti-discrimination measures.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Defence of current Volksverhetzung and platform rules.
* Support for identity-based institutional speech controls.

### Why this produces Endos 55 / Xenos 50

Retained plus moderately expanded identity focus.

---

# Die Linke

Die Linke supports the strongest identity-based and anti-discrimination speech controls among major parties.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Expansionary stance on hate-speech and institutional controls.
* Framing of certain critical speech as illegitimate or harmful.

### Why this produces Endos 42 / Xenos 38

Heaviest downward pressure from identity and content controls.
