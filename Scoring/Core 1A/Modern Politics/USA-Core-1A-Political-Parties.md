### Broad Summary

The American party system on Core 1A is anchored by the First Amendment baseline, which produces structurally higher scores than most European systems. The Republican Party and especially the Libertarian Party defend broad protections for speech, association, and against new federal content or platform mandates. The Democratic Party supports a denser set of norms and potential regulatory tools around online content, “hate speech,” and institutional speech codes, generating lower scores and a limited Endos/Xenos differential where identity-based enforcement intensity differs. The Green Party aligns more closely with expansive content and identity controls. Because constitutional protections limit the range of direct statutory restrictions that parties can credibly propose, scores remain higher overall than in the UK, France, or Germany; the principal contest is over the expansion or contraction of administrative, platform, and institutional controls rather than the core criminal speech offences.

# Democratic Party

The Democratic Party supports stronger platform accountability for harmful content, institutional anti-discrimination speech norms, and resistance to roll-back of existing content-moderation practices.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Support for federal and administrative pressure on platforms to moderate hate speech, misinformation, and harmful content.
* Defence of institutional speech codes and DEI-related expression constraints in universities, corporations, and government.
* Opposition to broad statutory protections that would limit private or platform moderation.
* Retention of existing federal civil-rights and hate-crime frameworks that can affect expression in certain contexts.
* Limited Endos/Xenos differential arising from heightened institutional scrutiny of speech critical of certain protected categories.

### Why this produces Endos 58 / Xenos 52

Negative-Only Weighting on the cumulative administrative, platform, and institutional restrictions produces the mid-range scores. The modest differential reflects identity-based intensity differences.

# Republican Party

The Republican Party emphasises robust First Amendment protection and opposes most new federal speech, platform, and institutional content mandates.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Opposition to federal platform content-moderation mandates and “hate speech” regulatory expansions.
* Support for legislation protecting political and religious speech from institutional deplatforming or discipline.
* Defence of broad constitutional free-speech protections against administrative expansion.
* Acceptance of traditional narrow limits (true threats, incitement, etc.) as the baseline.

### Why this produces Endos 75 / Xenos N/A

Strong resistance to new restrictions under Negative-Only Weighting, combined with the high constitutional baseline, produces the higher score. No material Endos/Xenos split.

# Libertarian Party

The Libertarian Party holds the most expansive free-speech and anti-restriction position among organised parties.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Maximalist defence of free expression and association against both state and platform compulsion.
* Opposition to virtually all content-based speech restrictions beyond the narrowest constitutional categories.
* Rejection of institutional speech codes and compelled speech.

### Why this produces Endos 88 / Xenos N/A

Closest approach to the high end of the Core 1A scale consistent with residual narrow limits on true threats and incitement.

# Green Party of the United States

The Green Party prioritises identity-based protections and content controls similar to European left parties.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Support for stronger hate-speech norms and platform accountability.
* Institutional anti-discrimination speech measures.
* Framing of certain critical speech as harmful or illegitimate.

### Why this produces Endos 50 / Xenos 45

Identity and content priorities produce the lower scores with a limited differential.
