### Broad Summary

The French party system on Core 1A is structured around the intensity and targeting of restrictions on expression, religious practice, association, and online content. Rassemblement National and Les Républicains produce clear Endos/Xenos splits driven by aggressive expansion of laïcité rules (religious symbols, abayas, fasting in certain contexts) and anti-separatism measures framed as system protection. These create markedly higher restrictions for the Xenos group (primarily Muslim and religious-minority communities associated with separatism) while leaving the Endos (core secular republican population) closer to the post-COVID baseline. Renaissance maintains a more uniform but still restrictive profile centred on the existing hate-speech, glorification-of-terrorism, and security framework with moderate laïcité reinforcement. Nouveau Front Populaire sits at the low end through support for expanded hate-speech enforcement, online content controls, institutional anti-racism measures, and framing of certain identity-critical speech as illegitimate. The cumulative effect is a system in which baseline speech and association restrictions are high across the board, with the largest downward pressure coming from parties that either expand identity-based controls broadly or apply them differentially.

# Les Républicains (LR)

Les Républicains present a hardline republican secularism combined with public-order and anti-separatism priorities. The party supports aggressive expansion of existing laïcité measures and enhanced powers against organisations and practices deemed incompatible with republican values.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Ban on headscarves for minors in public spaces and broader religious-symbol restrictions (including school trips and sports).
* Ban on abayas and similar clothing in schools with further extensions.
* Restrictions on religious fasting (e.g., Ramadan) for minors under certain ages in institutional contexts.
* Monitoring, oversight, and potential closure of mosques or organisations suspected of separatism (system-protection framing).
* Enhanced powers to intervene in religious education and practices deemed incompatible with republican values.
* Quicker administrative intervention and bans on demonstrations linked to separatism or extremism.
* Strengthened mandatory civic education emphasising laïcité and republican values.
* Retention of the existing hate-speech, defamation, and glorification-of-terrorism framework.

The party-as-totality profile combines the retained post-COVID baseline with multiple new or expanded restrictions that fall in the 44–50 (early ideological/identity-based controls) and 95–97 (system protecting itself) bands when applied to the Xenos group. Endos experience primarily the baseline plus milder civic-education pressure.

### Why this produces Endos 66 / Xenos 48

Negative-Only Weighting applied to the retained baseline plus the targeted religious-expression, association, and system-protection measures produces Endos 66 (baseline-dominant) and Xenos 48 (heavy additional weight from 44–50 and 95–97 elements). The split is required because the outgroup (Muslim/religious-minority communities associated with separatism) receives markedly worse treatment under a stable, discretely targetable set of policies.

# Nouveau Front Populaire (NFP)

The Nouveau Front Populaire (left alliance, LFI-dominant) supports expansion of existing hate-speech and anti-discrimination frameworks, stronger online content controls, and institutional measures against what it characterises as systemic racism and reactionary ideologies.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Expansion of existing hate-speech laws or their enforcement to cover more forms of speech deemed racist or discriminatory.
* Support for measures targeting online content and platforms regarding hate speech.
* Framing of criticism of certain identity-based or immigration-related policies as potentially falling under hate speech or racism.
* Institutional and educational measures to combat systemic racism (DEI-style programmes).
* Retention and defence of the full existing hate-speech, glorification-of-terrorism, and secularism framework without repeal of restrictive elements.
* Support for easier association and protest rights in some domains, but these are treated under Negative-Only Weighting as non-scoring removals only.

The profile is dominated by downward pressure from expanded content-based and ideological controls (roughly 28–38 band elements) added to the already restrictive baseline. No clear Endos/Xenos split meets the durability and discrete-targetability test at the level of the alliance as a whole.

### Why this produces Endos 30 / Xenos N/A (holistic)

Cumulative weight of the retained baseline plus multiple expanded hate-speech, online-content, and institutional identity measures produces a holistic score of approximately 30. Negative-Only Weighting applies; proposed expansions of protest or association rights grant no upward credit. No stable Endos/Xenos differential is scored for the alliance as a unitary actor.

# Rassemblement National (RN)

Rassemblement National under Jordan Bardella prioritises national cohesion through reinforced secularism and targeted measures against Islamist separatism and related expression/association.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Extension of bans on religious clothing (abayas, headscarves, etc.) in broader public and institutional settings.
* Monitoring and potential closure of mosques or organisations linked to Islamism/separatism.
* Framing of visible Islamic practices as incompatible with secular republican identity in certain contexts.
* Retention of the existing hate-speech and security framework with selective emphasis on Islamist-related speech.
* Opposition to certain ideological teaching in schools (removal of restrictions is non-scoring under Negative-Only Weighting).

The party produces a clear Endos/Xenos split: Endos remain close to the post-COVID baseline with limited new burdens, while Xenos face multiple targeted restrictions in the early-ideological and system-protection bands.

### Why this produces Endos 71 / Xenos 50

Negative-Only Weighting on the retained baseline plus targeted religious-expression and association controls yields Endos 71 and Xenos 50. The split is required by the stable, discretely targetable outgroup differential.

# Renaissance (and allies)

Renaissance maintains the existing post-COVID Core 1A baseline (hate speech, glorification of terrorism, security measures) while supporting continued laïcité enforcement and targeted anti-separatism tools.

### Core 1A Relevant Policies / Elements

* Retention of the full existing hate-speech, defamation, and glorification-of-terrorism framework.
* Continued application of religious-symbol and laïcité rules in schools and public institutions.
* Support for administrative and security measures against separatism (system-protection elements).
* Civic-education and institutional promotion of republican values.
* No major new expansion of content-based speech offences beyond the baseline, but no repeal either.

The profile is a moderate downward adjustment from pure baseline due to active laïcité and anti-separatism enforcement that carries some differential weight.

### Why this produces Endos 64 / Xenos 52

Retained baseline plus moderate laïcité and system-protection measures produce Endos 64 and a limited Xenos differential of 52. Negative-Only Weighting applies throughout.
