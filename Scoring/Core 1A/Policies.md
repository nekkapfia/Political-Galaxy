# Core 1A Scored Policies Reference

**Version:** 1.1 (4 June 2026) — Merged version. Contains all content from previous versions plus missing elements from the older scored policies document, fully preserved in their complete form.

**Purpose:** Pure reference document for the mechanical policy *types* scored under Core 1A (Personal Autonomy). Contains only the neutered base policy descriptions and the rationale for each score. No ideologies, historical regimes/countries, politicians, entities, overview philosophy, discarded policies, or mapping notes. This is the single source of truth for consistent policy scoring.

Policies are organised by **type of restriction** rather than score band. Within each category, policies are listed in **descending score order**.

**Element Rule**: Policies are broken into elements. Each element is placed in the most appropriate category. If a policy contains elements belonging to different categories, it appears in each relevant category showing only the elements that belong there.

## 1. Information Control

### National Security Law (Hong Kong, 2020)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Broad legislation creating offences of secession, subversion, terrorism, and collusion with foreign forces, with vague definitions applicable to political speech, publications, slogans, and organisational activities.

**Elements**:
- Targeting of specific slogans and publications deemed to promote independence or subversion → **97–98**
- Restrictions on publishing or possessing materials advocating secession, subversion, or collusion with foreign forces → **97–98**

**Rationale**: Core elements function as system self-defence. The targeting of slogans, publications, and materials promoting independence or challenging state authority places these elements in the 97–98 band.

### Official Secrets Act 1989
**Neutered Base Policy**: Extremely broad criminal offences for unauthorised disclosure of official SECRET information relevant to national security by crown servants and journalists.

**Core 1A Score: 97**

**Rationale**: Pure system-protection mechanism with no general speech-restriction intent. It sits in the explicit 97–98 sub-range for laws whose purpose is to allow the system to protect itself from destruction.

### Ideological control in media and online platforms
**Neutered Base Policy**: Requirements for platforms and media to proactively moderate content, implement self-censorship systems, and face liability for "harmful" material.

**Elements**:
- Requirements for platforms and media to proactively moderate content → **28–36**
- Implementation of self-censorship systems and platform liability for "harmful" material → **28–36**

**Rationale**: State-mandated proactive censorship through corporate proxy. Creates institutionalised self-censorship across public discourse.

### Systematic expansion of surveillance infrastructure (information aspect)
**Neutered Base Policy**: State-driven rollout of facial recognition cameras, mass data collection systems, and integration of surveillance data across government and commercial platforms.

**Elements**:
- Mass data collection systems and integration of surveillance data → **28–36**

**Rationale**: Creates a pervasive monitoring environment that restricts expression and association through the knowledge of constant observation.

### Online Safety Act 2023
**Neutered Base Policy**: Imposes duties on online platforms to mitigate illegal and "legal but harmful" content, including age verification, safety-by-design requirements, and Ofcom enforcement powers.

**Core 1A Score: 24**

**Rationale**: State-mandated restriction/sanctioning of expression deemed harmful. Broader scope ("legal but harmful") and proactive platform duties.

## 2. Speech & Expression Restrictions

### Racial Hatred Offences (Public Order Act 1986 Part 3)
**Neutered Base Policy**: It is an offence to use threatening, abusive or insulting words or behaviour, or display written material which is threatening, abusive or insulting, if the person intends to stir up racial hatred or racial hatred is likely to be stirred up thereby.

**Core 1A Score: 65** (top end of 60–69 band)

**Rationale**: Betterment of society. Targets speech and expression that risks stirring up racial hatred.

### Religious Hatred Offences (Racial and Religious Hatred Act 2006)
**Neutered Base Policy**: It is an offence to use threatening words or behaviour, or display any written material which is threatening, if the person intends thereby to stir up religious hatred.

**Core 1A Score: 32** (28–36 band)

**Rationale**: Ideological enforcement and police state. Religion treated as proto-ideology → ideological suppression.

### Blair-era usage of Racial and Religious Hatred Act 2006
**Core 1A Score: 24** (mid/top 19–27 band)

**Rationale**: Due to how it was used in practice (suppression of legitimate public discussion), rather than its literal statutory content.

### Obscene Publications Act 1959
**Neutered Base Policy**: Criminalises publication of material likely to deprave and corrupt.

**Core 1A Score: 63** (Betterment of society)

**Rationale**: Obscene publications are still limited even under progressive ideology, explicitly targeting the showing of pornography to very young children. The crossing into banning for adults brings it into betterment of society.

## 3. Movement Restrictions (including Assembly & Association)

**Neutered Base Policy**: Allowed detention of terrorism suspects for up to 7 days without charge.

**Core 1A Score: 88**

### Control Orders (2005)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Imposed severe restrictions on individuals suspected of involvement in terrorism, including house arrest, electronic tagging, and limits on association and movement, without criminal conviction.

**Core 1A Score: 88**

### Public Order Ordinance (Hong Kong) – Prohibition on national security grounds
**Neutered Base Policy**: Police powers to prohibit public assemblies on grounds of national security.

**Core 1A Score: 97–98**

**Rationale**: Allows prohibition based on protecting the system from perceived threats to its existence or authority.

### Public Order Ordinance (Hong Kong) – Prohibition on public safety grounds
**Neutered Base Policy**: Police powers to prohibit public assemblies on grounds of public safety.

**Core 1A Score: 90–95**

**Rationale**: Focused on preventing direct, observable harm (violence, serious disorder).

### SOCPA Protest Zones (2005)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Restrictions on protests within a 1 km zone around Parliament.

**Core 1A Score: 85**

**Rationale**: Geographically narrow and high-security focused. The targeted, limited-scope version is treated as significantly less invasive than nationwide Public Order Acts.

### Definition of "unlawful assembly" (Hong Kong)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Three or more people assembled who behave in a disorderly, intimidating, insulting or provocative manner likely to cause fear of a breach of the peace.

**Core 1A Score: High 80s–90s**

**Rationale**: Classic prevention of direct harm to others through fear of violence.

### Public Order Ordinance (Hong Kong) – Prohibition on public order grounds
**Neutered Base Policy**: Police powers to prohibit public assemblies on grounds of public order or protection of the rights of others.

**Core 1A Score: 60–69**

**Rationale**: Targets disruptive or annoying behaviour beyond direct harm. Moves into social order management.

**Neutered Base Policy**: Compulsory unpaid labour imposed on individuals, combined with restrictions on movement and detention.

**Core 1A Score: 10–18 band**

**Rationale**: Direct match to extreme restrictions on voluntary actions, movement, and association.

## 4. Physical Restrictions

### Gender Recognition Act 2004 – Panel application for legal gender change
**Neutered Base Policy**: Legal gender change requires application to a Gender Recognition Panel.

**Core 1A Score: 72**

**Rationale**: Explicitly limited to official documents only. Does not prevent personal presentation or speech, and does not compel others to affirm.

### Gender Recognition Act 2004 – Medical evidence of gender dysphoria requirement
**Neutered Base Policy**: Application requires medical evidence of gender dysphoria.

**Core 1A Score: 72**

**Rationale**: Explicitly limited to official documents only. Does not prevent personal presentation or speech, and does not compel others to affirm.

### Gender Recognition Act 2004 – Two-year real-life experience requirement
**Neutered Base Policy**: Applicant must live in the acquired gender for two years prior to application.

**Core 1A Score: 72**

**Rationale**: Explicitly limited to official documents only. Does not prevent personal presentation or speech. The two-year requirement explicitly requires active self-expression. Does not compel others to affirm.

### Gender Recognition Act 2004 – Legal sex change via certificate on official documents
**Neutered Base Policy**: Successful applicants receive a Gender Recognition Certificate enabling change of legal sex on official documents (birth certificate, etc.).

**Core 1A Score: 72**

**Rationale**: Explicitly limited to official documents only. Does not prevent personal presentation or speech, and does not compel others to affirm.

**Weighting Note (all four GRA 2004 parts)**: These policies are strictly limited to official documents and do not prevent the individual's own self-expression, presentation, or speech. The two-year requirement explicitly requires active lived expression. No compulsion is placed on third parties to affirm. This distinguishes them from modern self-ID + institutional compulsion packages.

### Compulsory Institutional Recognition of Self-Declared Gender
**Neutered Base Policy**: Requires institutions to treat self-declared gender as legal sex in all relevant contexts (services, sports, prisons, workplaces, etc.).

**Core 1A Score: 42**

### Self-ID / Self-Declaration Gender Recognition
**Neutered Base Policy**: An individual can legally change their recorded sex/gender by self-declaration alone, with no medical gatekeeping or real-life test required.

**Core 1A Score: 44**

### Deng-Era One-Child Policy – Core numerical restriction
**Neutered Base Policy**: Legal limit on the number of children a couple may have without official permission.

**Core 1A Score: 10–18 band**

**Rationale**: Direct restriction on reproductive decision-making and bodily autonomy.

### Deng-Era One-Child Policy – Physical coercion (forced abortions, sterilisations, IUD insertions)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Women are subjected to forced abortions, sterilisations, or IUD insertions to meet birth quotas or as punishment for unauthorised pregnancies.

**Placement**: Punishment not Restriction

**Reason**: With widespread state-provided access to birth control, these measures primarily function as enforcement/punishment rather than the core restriction.

### Deng-Era One-Child Policy – Denial of legal status and rights to children born outside the plan
**Neutered Base Policy**: Children born without official permission can be denied hukou registration and access to education, healthcare, and other state benefits.

**Placement**: Cultural 4A – Hierarchy + Punishment not Restriction

**Reason**: Creates long-term structural disadvantage and inherited status. Also functions as punishment for violating the birth limit.
