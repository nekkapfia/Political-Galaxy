# Core 1A Scored Policies Reference

**Version:** 1.0 (4 June 2026)  
**Status:** Clean Baseline

---

## Introduction and Purpose

This document is the **single source of truth** for all mechanical policy types scored under **Core 1A (Personal Autonomy)**. It contains only neutral, reusable policy descriptions and their associated scores. It is designed to enable consistent scoring across countries, eras, leaders, and ideologies without repeating analysis.

The document deliberately excludes:
- Historical or ideological narrative
- Country-specific context
- Political parties, leaders, or regimes
- Discarded elements and mapping notes

Its sole purpose is to provide clean, modular, and reusable scored policy components.

---

## Structure and Rules

### 1. Organisation by Type of Restriction
Policies are organised into **five broad categories** based on the fundamental type of restriction, not by score band:

1. **Information Control**
2. **Speech & Expression Restrictions**
3. **Movement Restrictions** (including Assembly & Association)
4. **Physical Restrictions**
5. **Surveillance & Monitoring**

### 2. Element-Level Breakdown
Each policy is broken down into its individual **elements**. Only the elements relevant to a specific category are shown in that category.

**Example**:  
If a single policy contains both an *information control* element and a *surveillance* element, the policy will appear in both the **Information Control** category and the **Surveillance & Monitoring** category — but only the relevant element(s) will be shown in each.

### 3. Scoring Within Categories
Within each category, policies (and their elements) are listed in **descending score order** (highest score first). This allows quick reference when looking for comparable policies.

### 4. How to Use This Document
When scoring a new policy:

1. Break the policy down into its individual elements.
2. For each element, identify the most appropriate category.
3. Check existing entries in that category for close equivalents.
4. If a close equivalent exists, **inherit the score** (with possible small modifier of ±5 if justified).
5. If no close equivalent exists, create a new entry using the same neutral mechanical format.

### 5. Important Principles
- **Negative-only weighting** applies throughout.
- **Chilling effects**, self-censorship, and behavioural consequences are **never scored**.
- **Punishment** and **process** elements are generally discarded.
- **Economic and property** restrictions belong in Core 1B, not Core 1A.
- All entries use **neutral mechanical language** only.

## 1. Information Control

Restrictions on accessing, creating, or distributing information.

### Official Secrets Act 1989 (UK)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Extremely broad criminal offences for unauthorised disclosure of official secret information relevant to national security by crown servants and journalists.

**Core 1A Score**: **97**

**Rationale**: Pure system-protection mechanism. Sits in the explicit 97–98 band for laws whose purpose is to allow the system to protect itself from destruction.

### National Security Law (Hong Kong, 2020) – Core Elements
**Neutered Base Policy**: Broad offences of secession, subversion, terrorism, and collusion with foreign forces, with vague definitions applicable to political slogans, publications, and materials.

**Core 1A Score**: **97–98**

**Rationale**: Core elements targeting specific slogans, publications, and materials function primarily as system self-defence.

### Ideological Control in Media and Online Platforms (PRC / Xi Era)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Requirements for media outlets and online platforms to proactively moderate content, implement self-censorship systems, and face liability for politically sensitive or "harmful" material.

**Core 1A Score**: **28–36**

**Rationale**: State-mandated proactive censorship through corporate proxy. Creates institutionalised control over information flow.

### Systematic Expansion of Surveillance Infrastructure (PRC)
**Neutered Base Policy**: State-driven rollout and integration of mass data collection systems, facial recognition, and surveillance data across government and commercial platforms.

**Core 1A Score**: **28–36**

**Rationale**: Creates pervasive information collection that restricts expression through the knowledge of constant monitoring (information control aspect).

### Online Safety Act 2023 (UK)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Imposes duties on online platforms to mitigate illegal and "legal but harmful" content, including proactive moderation and Ofcom enforcement powers.

**Core 1A Score**: **24**

**Rationale**: State-mandated restriction and sanctioning of expression through corporate platforms. Expands control over accessible information.

## 2. Speech & Expression Restrictions

Direct limits on what individuals are allowed to say, write, publish, or communicate.

### Racial Hatred Offences (Public Order Act 1986 Part 3)
**Neutered Base Policy**: It is an offence to use threatening, abusive or insulting words or behaviour, or display written material which is threatening, abusive or insulting, if the person intends to stir up racial hatred or such hatred is likely to be stirred up.

**Core 1A Score**: **65**

**Rationale**: Betterment of society – protection against incitement of racial hatred. Top end of the 60–69 band.

### Obscene Publications Act 1959
**Neutered Base Policy**: Criminalises the publication of material likely to deprave and corrupt.

**Core 1A Score**: **63**

**Rationale**: Betterment of society restriction on extreme content. Sits in the upper 60–69 band.

### Religious Hatred Offences (Racial and Religious Hatred Act 2006)
**Neutered Base Policy**: It is an offence to use threatening words or behaviour, or display any written material which is threatening, if the person intends thereby to stir up religious hatred.

**Core 1A Score**: **32**

**Rationale**: Functions as ideological enforcement / police state when applied to religious belief (treated as proto-ideology). Sits in the 28–36 band.

### Blair-era usage of Racial and Religious Hatred Act 2006
**Neutered Base Policy**: Application of hatred laws in practice, including suppression of legitimate public discussion on sensitive topics (e.g. grooming gangs, immigration).

**Core 1A Score**: **24**

**Rationale**: In documented usage, functioned more as ideological suppression than pure betterment of society. Mid 19–27 band.

### Online Safety Act 2023
**Neutered Base Policy**: Imposes duties on platforms to proactively address "legal but harmful" content, with significant enforcement powers and penalties.

**Core 1A Score**: **24**

**Rationale**: State-mandated proactive restriction and sanctioning of expression through corporate intermediaries. Expands control over public discourse.

## 3. Movement Restrictions (including Assembly & Association)

Limits on physical movement, gathering with others, and collective action. Includes imprisonment as the extreme form of movement restriction.

### Hong Kong National Security Law – Organisational and Assembly Elements
**Neutered Base Policy**: Restrictions on organising or participating in groups or activities seen as promoting Hong Kong independence or challenging state authority.

**Core 1A Score**: **97–98**

**Rationale**: Core elements function primarily as system self-defence when targeting activities that challenge the authority of the state.

### Hong Kong Public Order Ordinance – Prohibition on National Security Grounds
**Neutered Base Policy**: Police power to prohibit public meetings or processions if believed necessary in the interests of national security.

**Core 1A Score**: **97–98**

**Rationale**: When justified on national security grounds, functions as system self-defence.

### Hong Kong Public Order Ordinance – Prohibition on Public Safety Grounds
**Neutered Base Policy**: Police power to prohibit or impose conditions on assemblies if believed necessary for public safety.

**Core 1A Score**: **90–95**

**Rationale**: Direct protection against observable harm to others.

### Hong Kong Public Order Ordinance – Prohibition on Public Order Grounds
**Neutered Base Policy**: Police power to prohibit or impose conditions on assemblies on grounds of public order or protection of the rights and freedoms of others.

**Core 1A Score**: **60–69**

**Rationale**: Betterment of society – targeting disruptive or annoying behaviour.

### SOCPA Protest Zones (UK, 2005)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Restrictions on protests and demonstrations within a 1 km zone around Parliament.

**Core 1A Score**: **85**

**Rationale**: Geographically narrow, high-security focused restriction on assembly and protest.

### Definition of “Unlawful Assembly” (Hong Kong)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Three or more people assembled who behave in a disorderly, intimidating, insulting or provocative manner that is intended or likely to cause any person reasonably to fear a breach of the peace.

**Core 1A Score**: **High 80s–90s**

**Rationale**: Classic prevention of direct harm to others through fear of violence.

### COVID-19 Movement and Gathering Restrictions
**Neutered Base Policy**: Legal requirements or strong compulsion limiting leaving home, internal movement, and the number/size of gatherings (indoors and outdoors).

**Core 1A Score**: **18–25**

**Rationale**: Direct and widespread restriction on freedom of movement and association. Temporary emergency framing provides modest upward weighting.

### Famine-Era / Crisis Movement Controls
**Neutered Base Policy**: Strict movement bans, travel restrictions, or checkpoint systems preventing individuals from leaving affected areas.

**Core 1A Score**: **10–18**

**Rationale**: Severe restriction on movement, especially damaging when combined with other crises.

## 4. Physical Restrictions

Interference with the body, medical decisions, personal physical acts, behavioural regulation, and direct physical actions between people.

### Race Relations Act 1976 (UK)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Made racial discrimination unlawful in employment, housing, goods/services and education; created positive duties on public bodies in some contexts.

**Core 1A Score**: **65**

**Rationale**: Betterment of society – protection against exploitation and discrimination. Top end of the 60–69 band.

### ASBOs / Anti-Social Behaviour Orders (and successors)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Civil orders restricting specific behaviours deemed anti-social, with criminal penalties for breach; broad and discretionary application.

**Core 1A Score**: **62**

**Rationale**: Behavioural regulation aimed at improving social order and reducing nuisance. Sits in the 60–69 betterment of society band.

### Obscene Publications Act 1959 (applied to adult content)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Criminalises the publication of material likely to deprave and corrupt (applied to adult pornography and extreme content).

**Core 1A Score**: **63**

**Rationale**: Regulation of physical/moral content and behaviour. Upper end of the 60–69 band.

### COVID-19 Medical Certification and Vaccine/Test Mandates
**Neutered Base Policy**: Requirements to provide proof of vaccination, testing, or recovery to access work, travel, hospitality, or other areas of public life.

**Core 1A Score**: **25–30**

**Rationale**: Direct restriction on medical choice and bodily autonomy through certification requirements. Temporary emergency framing provides modest upward weighting.

### COVID-19 School and Workplace Activity Restrictions
**Neutered Base Policy**: Mandatory closure of schools or severe limits on in-person education and work activity, with movement to online or heavily restricted formats.

**Core 1A Score**: **22–28**

**Rationale**: Significant interference with daily physical routines, education, and work as forms of personal autonomy.

### One-Child Policy – Numerical Birth Limits (PRC)
**Neutered Base Policy**: State-imposed numerical limits on the number of children per couple/family, enforced through administrative quotas and incentives/penalties.

**Core 1A Score**: **10–18**

**Rationale**: Direct restriction on reproductive autonomy and family formation decisions. Core numerical limit element.

**End of Category 4 – Physical Restrictions**

## 5. Surveillance & Monitoring

Systematic data collection, watching, and monitoring of the population.

### Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 (PACE)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Standard procedural powers of search, seizure, interrogation, arrest, detention and limited surveillance in criminal investigations, subject to reasonable suspicion, judicial warrants and proportionality requirements.

**Core 1A Score**: **38**

**Rationale**: Routine criminal justice tools with significant procedural limitations and judicial oversight. Prevention of harm to others.

### National DNA Database Expansion
**Neutered Base Policy**: Large-scale retention of DNA profiles from individuals arrested or charged, even if not convicted.

**Core 1A Score**: **33**

**Rationale**: Systematic collection and retention of highly personal biometric data.

### Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act 2000 (RIPA)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Authorises the interception of communications, acquisition of communications data, and other investigatory powers by public authorities.

**Core 1A Score**: **30**

**Rationale**: Systematic access to personal communications data. Creates police-state style monitoring capabilities.

### Data Retention and Investigatory Powers Act 2014 (DRIPA)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Requires retention of communications data by telecommunications operators and provides access for public authorities.

**Core 1A Score**: **29**

**Rationale**: Mass retention and access to communications data. Functionally similar to RIPA.

### Prevent Strategy / Counter-Extremism Institutional Monitoring
**Neutered Base Policy**: Requires public institutions (schools, universities, NHS, etc.) to monitor, report on, and refer individuals deemed at risk of radicalisation or extremism based on beliefs and behaviour.

**Core 1A Score**: **28–35**

**Rationale**: Creates widespread institutional monitoring of personal beliefs, associations, and behaviour. Functions as ideological enforcement through surveillance.

### Systematic Expansion of Surveillance Infrastructure (PRC / Xi Era)
**Neutered Base Policy**: State-driven rollout of widespread facial recognition cameras, mass data collection systems, and integration of surveillance data across government and commercial platforms.

**Core 1A Score**: **28–36**

**Rationale**: Pervasive, normalised surveillance that enables broad monitoring of population behaviour, movement, and communications.
