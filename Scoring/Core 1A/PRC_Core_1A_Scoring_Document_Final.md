# PRC Core 1A Scoring Document

**Version:** Final Locked (4 June 2026)  
**Status:** Complete

This document provides a detailed Core 1A (Personal Autonomy) analysis of the People’s Republic of China from 1949 to the present. It follows the same standards of structure, rigour, and presentation as the UK and USA scoring documents in this repository.

Only direct restrictions on personal autonomy are scored. Processes, enforcement actions, consequences (such as chilling effects), Core 1B elements, and political structure changes are excluded.

## 3. Key Locked Policies and Scored Elements

### National Security Law (Hong Kong, 2020)
- Core elements (specific slogans, publications, and organisational activities promoting independence or challenging state authority) → **97–98**

### National Security Education (Hong Kong)
- Compulsory teaching of specific ideological content → **19–27**
- Restrictions on what can be taught or discussed in education → **28–36**

### Public Order Ordinance (Hong Kong)
- Prohibition on grounds of national security → **97–98**
- Prohibition on grounds of public safety → **90–95**
- Prohibition on grounds of public order / protection of the rights and freedoms of others → **60–69**
- Definition of “unlawful assembly” → High 80s–90s

### Mainland PRC – Major Scored Policy Types
- Class-based execution and persecution of designated groups → **5**
- Policy-induced mass famine through grain requisition and movement controls → **5**
- Struggle sessions, public humiliation, and ideological persecution → **7–9**
- Long-term compulsory labour in camps with severe restrictions → **7–9**
- Ideological enforcement and police state measures → **28–36**
- Surveillance state expansion (policy-level) → **Police State**

## 4. Methodology

- Scores reflect only **direct restrictions** on personal autonomy.
- Endos/Xenos splits are applied only where a discretely targetable outgroup receives markedly different treatment.
- The Zero-COVID period is treated as a **temporary global event** and does not define the long-term Xi baseline.
- Hong Kong is maintained as a **separate table** until it reaches legal unity with mainland China.
- All scores are **locked**.
