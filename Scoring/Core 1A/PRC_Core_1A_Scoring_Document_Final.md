# PRC Core 1A Scoring Document — Complete Authoritative Reference

**Version:** Final Locked (4 June 2026)  
**Status:** Complete and Locked

This document provides the full Core 1A (Personal Autonomy) analysis of the People’s Republic of China from 1949 to the present, including Hong Kong as a separate section. It is written to the same standard of detail, rigour, and presentation as the UK and USA scoring documents in this repository.

Only direct restrictions on personal autonomy are scored. Processes, enforcement actions, consequences (such as chilling effects), Core 1B elements, and political structure changes are excluded.

---

## 1. Rule & Methodology Changes (Locked)

### Negative-Only Weighting (Locked)
Scores reflect only the presence and weight of invasive or restrictive policies. Removal or opposition to restrictions grants no positive credit. Statements of future intent to reduce restrictions are treated as rhetoric only and do not improve the score.

### Endos/Xenos Split (Locked)
Applied only where a discretely targetable outgroup receives markedly different treatment from the general population. Examples include:
- Falun Gong practitioners under Jiang Zemin (targeted persecution, organ harvesting allegations)
- Uyghurs and other Turkic Muslims under Xi Jinping (mass detention, re-education, cultural destruction)
- Class enemies and designated political/ideological groups during specific Mao periods

The split is **not** applied where differential treatment is primarily behavioural or political rather than based on a stable, pre-existing group identity.

### Temporary Events (Locked)
The Zero-COVID period (2020–early 2023) is treated as a temporary global event and is segregated from baseline era scoring. This prevents an unprecedented but time-limited loss of autonomy from distorting the long-term assessment of the Xi era.

### Hong Kong Treatment (Locked)
Hong Kong is maintained as a **separate table** until legal unity with mainland China is achieved. No Endos/Xenos split is applied to Hong Kong as a whole because the major post-2020 restrictions primarily targeted political expression and participation based on views and behaviour rather than a stable outgroup identity in the same structured way as certain mainland cases.

### Current-Policy Basis (Locked)
All scoring is performed on observable, real-world policies that are actively maintained or promoted at the time of assessment. Aspirational or "unconstrained total control" goals are not used unless explicitly instructed.

### Punishment vs Restriction (Locked)
Arrests, prosecutions, fines, asset freezes, and other enforcement actions are **not scored**. Only the underlying rules that restrict what people are permitted to do, say, or organise are scored. Enforcement is a consequence of the restriction, not the restriction itself.

### Process vs Restriction (Locked)
Notification requirements, bureaucratic procedures, administrative mechanisms, and organisational structures are **not scored**. Only the substantive restrictions on individual autonomy are scored.

### Chilling Effects (Locked — Permanent Rule)
Chilling effects, self-censorship, and behavioural changes resulting from fear of consequences are **not scored**. These are consequences of restrictions, not restrictions themselves. Any element whose primary described effect is a chilling effect or self-censorship is immediately marked as not scored and discarded.

### Floor for Real-World Scoring (Locked)
Score **4** is treated as the effective floor for real-world (non-sci-fi) policies. Scores below 4 require meaningful control over involuntary physiological processes, which is not currently technologically feasible at scale for mass populations.

## 3. All PRC China Scored Policies — Full Entries with Expanded Justifications

### Class-based Execution and Persecution of Designated Groups (Mao Era)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Systematic execution, imprisonment, and persecution of individuals identified as belonging to designated class, political, or ideological categories (landlords, counter-revolutionaries, "rightists", class enemies).

**Core 1A Score**: **5**

**Rationale**: Systematic extermination of designated groups. Targeted/limited scope reduces weighting in holistic scoring compared to total population extermination, but remains one of the lowest possible real-world scores.

**Elements**:
- Execution of individuals belonging to designated groups — **Scored (5)**. Systematic extermination of designated groups. Matches "Systematic extermination of designated groups" category.
- Imprisonment and long-term labour camps for designated groups — **Scored (7–9)**. Long-term control over voluntary actions through compulsory labour and restricted movement.
- Public struggle sessions, humiliation, and ideological persecution — **Scored (7–9)**. Extreme low autonomy through public degradation and ideological coercion.

### Policy-induced Mass Famine through Grain Requisition and Movement Controls (Great Leap Forward)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Deliberate requisition of grain from rural areas combined with severe restrictions on internal movement (household registration system enforcement), resulting in mass starvation.

**Core 1A Score**: **5**

**Rationale**: Systematic extermination through policy-induced famine. One of the largest man-made famines in history. Extremely low autonomy through control over food access and movement.

**Elements**:
- Deliberate grain requisition leading to starvation — **Scored (5)**. Systematic extermination through policy design.
- Severe restrictions on internal movement (hukou enforcement) — **Scored (10–18)**. Extreme movement restrictions preventing escape from famine areas.
- Denial of food aid to designated "rightist" or unproductive areas — **Scored (5)**. Targeted denial of survival resources.

### Struggle Sessions, Public Humiliation, and Ideological Persecution (Cultural Revolution)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Organised public events in which designated individuals (intellectuals, officials, "class enemies") were subjected to verbal abuse, physical humiliation, and forced ideological self-criticism, often accompanied by violence from Red Guards.

**Core 1A Score**: **7–9**

**Rationale**: Extreme low autonomy through public degradation and ideological coercion. Systematic use of public humiliation as a tool of social control and ideological enforcement.

**Elements**:
- Public struggle sessions and physical/verbal humiliation — **Scored (7–9)**. Extreme low autonomy through public degradation.
- Forced ideological self-criticism and denunciation — **Scored (19–27)**. Ideological reprogramming / thought control.
- Red Guard violence and social chaos targeting designated groups — **Scored (7–9)**. Extreme low autonomy through fear and violence.

### Long-term Compulsory Labour in Camps (Laogai / Re-education Through Labour System)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Assignment of individuals to long-term labour camps (laogai, re-education through labour) involving compulsory work, restricted movement, controlled daily routines, and ideological indoctrination.

**Core 1A Score**: **7–9**

**Rationale**: Extreme low autonomy through control over most voluntary actions. Long-term detention combined with forced labour and ideological control.

**Elements**:
- Compulsory labour and restricted movement in camps — **Scored (7–9)**. Extreme low autonomy through control over voluntary actions.
- Ideological indoctrination and self-criticism requirements in camps — **Scored (19–27)**. Ideological reprogramming / thought control.

### Ideological Enforcement and Police State Measures (Post-Mao, especially Xi Era)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Systematic expansion of surveillance infrastructure, ideological control in education and media, "national security" framing to justify restrictions on expression and association, and institutional monitoring requirements.

**Core 1A Score**: **28–36**

**Rationale**: Creates a pervasive environment of monitoring and ideological conformity. Expands the scope of state control over daily life and expression.

**Elements**:
- Expansion of surveillance state (facial recognition, data collection, social credit pilots) — **Scored (28–36)**. Police-state surveillance creating chilling environment.
- Ideological control in education (curriculum control, loyalty requirements, restrictions on foreign ideas) — **Scored (28–36)**. Ideological enforcement and curtailment of critical information.
- Ideological control in media and online platforms (content moderation, self-censorship requirements, platform liability) — **Scored (28–36)**. Ideological enforcement through corporate proxy.
- Use of "national security" framing to restrict expression, association, and business activity — **Scored (28–36)**. Expands scope of justified restrictions across society.

## 6. All PRC Hong Kong Scored Policies — Full Entries with Expanded Justifications

### National Security Law (Hong Kong, 2020)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Broad legislation imposed by Beijing creating offences of secession, subversion, terrorism, and collusion with foreign forces, with vague definitions that can be applied to political speech, publications, slogans, and organisational activities.

**Core 1A Score**: **97–98**

**Rationale**: The core elements function primarily as system self-defence measures. The targeting of slogans (e.g., "Liberate Hong Kong"), publications, and activities promoting Hong Kong independence or challenging Beijing’s authority places these elements in the 97–98 band for laws whose purpose is to allow the system to protect itself from destruction.

**Elements**:
- Targeting of specific slogans and publications deemed to promote independence or subversion — **Scored (97–98)**. System self-defence. Direct restriction on political expression challenging state authority.
- Restrictions on organising or participating in groups or activities seen as promoting independence or challenging Beijing’s authority — **Scored (97–98)**. System self-defence. Limits collective political action.
- Restrictions on organisations and foreign groups — **Removed**. Organisations are not individuals; foreigners fall outside the domestic system in the same way.
- Pre-NSL indirect pressure on media (ownership changes, advertising pressure) — **Removed**. Outside scope (economic/indirect pressure).

### National Security Education (Hong Kong)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Mandatory education programmes requiring schools to teach specific ideological and national security content aligned with Beijing’s perspective, while restricting discussion of certain historical events and political topics.

**Core 1A Score**: **26–30**

**Rationale**: Combines ideological reprogramming/thought control with restrictions on access to critical or undesirable information.

**Elements**:
- Compulsory teaching of specific ideological and national security content — **Scored (19–27)**. Ideological reprogramming / thought control. Education used to shape political beliefs.
- Restrictions on what can be taught or discussed in education (sensitive historical events, political topics) — **Scored (28–36)**. Ideological enforcement and police state. Access to critical or undesirable information is curtailed.

### Public Order Ordinance (Hong Kong)
**Neutered Base Policy**: Framework granting the police powers to regulate public assemblies, including notification requirements, conditions on time/place/route, and powers to prohibit assemblies on various grounds (national security, public safety, public order, protection of rights of others).

**Core 1A Scores** (vary by justification used):
- Prohibition on grounds of **national security** → **97–98** (system self-defence)
- Prohibition on grounds of **public safety** → **90–95** (strict direct harm protection)
- Prohibition on grounds of **public order** or **protection of the rights and freedoms of others** → **60–69** (betterment of society / targeting disruptive behaviour)
- Definition of "unlawful assembly" (3+ people behaving in disorderly, intimidating, insulting or provocative manner likely to cause fear of breach of the peace) → High 80s–90s (prevention of direct harm)

**Elements**:
- Power to prohibit on national security grounds — **Scored (97–98)**. System self-defence.
- Power to prohibit on public safety grounds — **Scored (90–95)**. Strict direct harm protection.
- Power to prohibit on public order / rights of others grounds — **Scored (60–69)**. Betterment of society restrictions.
- Definition of unlawful assembly — **Scored (High 80s–90s)**. Prevention of direct harm.
- Notification requirements and power to impose conditions — **Removed**. Procedural, outside scope.
