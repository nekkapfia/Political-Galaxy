## 1. Rule & Methodology Changes (Authoritative & Locked)

### Nazi-Symbol Bans (§86, §86a StGB) — Downweighting Rule
**Individual Policy Score:** 32 (28–36 ideological enforcement / police-state band) — unchanged.  
**Holistic Treatment:** Explicitly **downweighted** as a “limited, targeted, historical exception”.  

**Full Justification & Rationale:**  
The ban is extremely narrow in scope — it prohibits only specific Nazi symbols, insignia, and propaganda from one defeated historical regime. It is not a generic or broad ideological restriction on political speech, dissent, or opposing worldviews. It has existed continuously since the 1950s/1960s as a foundational post-war constitutional settlement rather than an active tool of ongoing political control.  

Per direct user instruction (“keep it as it is, but downweight it as it is very specific and is not a general ideological restriction”), the “one limited bad policy doesn’t cripple everything” rule is now active for all German scoring. This prevents a single narrow historical policy from artificially capping every era at 32 and makes the slider usable for meaningful differentiation between governments and time periods. The ban still contributes to the overall texture of the system but no longer dominates the holistic floor.

**Alignment with Lived Experience:** Germans generally treat the Nazi-symbol ban as a settled, background taboo rather than a daily threat to normal political expression. Downweighting accurately reflects this lived reality.

## 3. All Scored Policies — Full Entries with Expanded Justifications

### Pre-Oct 1998 Baseline (already in prior document — retained for completeness)

#### General Police and Criminal Investigation Powers (StPO — net invasive elements)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Standard procedural powers of search, seizure, interrogation, arrest, detention and limited surveillance in criminal investigations, subject to reasonable suspicion, judicial warrants and proportionality requirements.  
**Core 1A Score:** 85  
**Rationale:** Routine criminal justice tools for prevention of harm to others. Extreme procedural limitations (judicial oversight, rights protections, proportionality) place it firmly in the protection-of-vulnerable / prevention-of-harm band rather than police-state monitoring.

#### Public-Nuisance Regulations (OWiG and local ordinances)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Prohibition of behaviours such as excessive noise, littering, obstruction of public ways and disorderly conduct that disturb public order, enforced by administrative fines or criminal sanctions in repeated cases.  
**Core 1A Score:** 64  
**Rationale:** Anti-social behaviour and public-order restrictions aimed at betterment of society and prevention of nuisance rather than direct ideological enforcement or severe personal autonomy restrictions.

#### Restrictive Abortion Rules (§218 StGB pre-2000)
**Neutered Base Policy:** General criminal prohibition on abortion with exemptions only for medical necessity, rape, or after mandatory state counselling and waiting period in first trimester.  
**Core 1A Score:** 82  
**Rationale:** Ban on abortion with explicit carve-outs for life/health of mother and rape cases. Fits “safe, legal and rare” style framework protecting the fetus as vulnerable while allowing narrow exceptions. Places it in the 82–89 protecting vulnerable individuals band.

#### Criminalisation of Most Recreational Drugs (Betäubungsmittelgesetz)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Comprehensive criminal prohibition on possession, consumption, manufacture and trade of listed narcotic substances.  
**Core 1A Score:** 72  
**Rationale:** Primary restriction targets self-regarding acts of possession and consumption (prevention of rendering self pseudo-vulnerable) combined with trade prohibitions. Overall score reflects the self-harm / pseudo-vulnerability focus.

### Schröder Era (1998–2005) — New/Changed Policies

#### Prostitution treated as “immoral” contract — repealed (2002 Prostitution Act)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Contracts for sexual services were previously treated as immoral and unenforceable; the 2002 Act removed this legal barrier and made such contracts fully enforceable.  
**Core 1A Score:** 100 (Discarded / Not Scored)  
**Rationale:** Pure expansion of autonomy for consenting adults. The self-regarding act of entering into a voluntary contract for sexual services is now fully legal and enforceable. Per Core 1A rules, expansions of rights that add autonomy rather than remove it are discarded from negative scoring.

#### Registered Life Partnerships (2001)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Legal recognition of same-sex registered partnerships with many (but not all) of the rights and obligations of marriage.  
**Core 1A Score:** 100 (Discarded / Not Scored)  
**Rationale:** Pure grant of additional legal rights to a previously excluded group. No new restriction is imposed on anyone. Positive rights expansions are discarded.

#### 2002 Weapons Act Revision (selected Core 1A elements)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Introduction of mandatory psychological evaluation for certain licence categories, higher minimum age for some weapons, and refined “need” justification requirements.  
**Core 1A Score:** 82  
**Rationale:** Narrow, targeted tightenings focused on prevention of harm and protection of vulnerable individuals (psychological screening, age gates). Fits the upper end of the 82–89 protecting vulnerable individuals band.

### Merkel Phase 1 (2005–2013) — New/Changed Policies

#### Mandatory Military Conscription (or Zivildienst) — suspended 2011
**Neutered Base Policy:** Requirement for young men to perform either military service or alternative civilian service (Zivildienst) as a condition of citizenship/residency.  
**Core 1A Score:** 100 (Discarded / Not Scored)  
**Rationale:** Forced unpaid labour / mandatory service in peacetime ended. Pure liberalisation of voluntary action, movement, and association. One of the most significant autonomy expansions in modern German history.

#### Telecommunications Data Retention (Vorratsdatenspeicherung) 2007–2010
**Neutered Base Policy:** Mandatory retention by telecom providers of traffic and location data (who called whom, when, how long, cell-tower location) for all users for six months without specific suspicion.  
**Core 1A Score:** 32  
**Rationale:** Systematic, suspicionless blanket collection of every citizen’s communications metadata. Even though later struck down, the act of mandatory mass data retention itself fits the 28–36 police-state / ideological enforcement band.

### Merkel Phase 2 (2013–2020) — New/Changed Policies

#### Telecommunications Data Retention II (2015)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Re-imposed mandatory retention of traffic and location data for 10 weeks (location data 4 weeks) after the previous law was invalidated.  
**Core 1A Score:** 32  
**Rationale:** Same police-state level blanket metadata collection re-established. The temporary nature and later legal challenges do not change the fundamental invasiveness of the retention mandate itself.

#### Network Enforcement Act (NetzDG 2017)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Social media platforms with >2 million German users must delete “obviously illegal” content (hate speech, incitement, defamation, Holocaust denial, etc.) within 24 hours of complaint or face fines up to €50 million.  
**Core 1A Score:** 32  
**Rationale:** State-mandated proactive censorship of speech on the primary modern public square, enforced with massive financial penalties on private companies. Classic ideological enforcement through corporate proxy. Matches the 28–36 band.

### Scholz Era (post-COVID, 2023–2025) — New/Changed Policies

#### Cannabis Act 2024 (CanG)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Partial legalisation of recreational cannabis for adults: possession up to 25 g in public / 50 g at home, cultivation of up to 3 plants for personal use, and non-commercial cannabis social clubs (from July 2024).  
**Core 1A Score:** 82 (replacing previous 72 for the personal-use portion)  
**Rationale:** Removes the previous blanket criminal prohibition on self-regarding acts of possession and home cultivation for consenting adults. Moves the policy from the 70–75 self-regarding pseudo-vulnerable restriction band into the 82–89 narrow harm-prevention / protection of vulnerable band (remaining rules on commercial sale, advertising, youth protection, and driving limits stay in place).

### Merz Era (2025–present) — New/Changed Policies

#### Tightened Migration Enforcement and Border Controls (2025–2026)
**Neutered Base Policy:** Strengthened border controls, accelerated deportations of rejected asylum seekers and irregular migrants, stricter entry and naturalisation criteria, and increased emphasis on “orderly migration” with more rejections.  
**Core 1A (Xenos) Score:** 45  
**Rationale:** Incremental tightening of existing enforcement mechanisms rather than a wholly new framework. Increases practical restrictiveness for non-citizens (residence, movement, expulsion) but remains within the same band as prior Xenos rules (limits on voluntary residence and movement for the out-group). No new blanket restrictions on the general (Endos) population.

## 4. Dissected Policies — Full Component Scores & Reasoning

### A. Strict Firearms Licensing and Control (Waffengesetz) — Dissected Core 1A Elements

After full negative-conditions list → filtered per rules (licensing = Core 1B, convictions = discarded, mental health = true vulnerable, substance abuse = pseudo-vulnerable, general unreliability = ideological, knowledge/training = prevention of harm top-end). Only remaining Core 1A elements scored.

| Component | Score | Band | Full Reasoning & Justification |
|-----------|-------|------|--------------------------------|
| **General unreliability / catch-all subjective denial** | **35** | 28–36 (ideological enforcement) | Broad, discretionary power to deny licences on the grounds that the applicant is “unreliable” without an objective, measurable trigger. User explicitly stated this is “so broad and subjective that it might as well be lumped in with the ideological restrictions” (“because we think you’re unreliable” = open-ended “because we say so”). Matches the same band as Nazi-symbol bans and data retention. |
| **Absence of recognised need (self-defence/general personal protection explicitly excluded)** | **48** | 44–50 (early ideological/identity-based controls) | Pure justification/need requirement with very narrow, difficult-to-obtain exceptions (sport club, hunting licence, museum collecting). Self-defence is never accepted as a legitimate need. Creates ongoing bureaucratic/identity-based control over a basic autonomy interest (armed self-defence). |
| **Strict carrying prohibition** | **55** | 51–59 (paternalism / self-betterment) | Additional layer of discretionary justification required for public carry (separate Waffenschein issued only in extremely narrow circumstances, never for self-defence). An extra control on daily behaviour and movement that sits in the paternalism band. |
| **Mandatory safe-storage rules** | **78** | 76–81 (protection against exploitation) | Narrow, targeted prevention of accidental harm/exploitation (firearms must be stored unloaded in approved safes with ammunition stored separately). Fits the protection-against-exploitation band as a specific third-party-risk safeguard rather than broad ideological control. |
| **Weapon-specific partial bans** (fully automatic, silencers, certain military-style arms, restricted calibres, etc.) | **82** | 82–89 (protecting vulnerable individuals) | Categorical prohibitions on particularly dangerous weapon types. User explicitly corrected that this is **not** a complete ban on firearms of any kind. Treated as narrow conditional prevention of harm / protection of vulnerable (society at large from military-grade weapons in civilian hands). |
| **Children/minors excluded** | **82** | 82–89 (protecting vulnerable individuals) | Categorical bar on possession for persons below the minimum age (generally 18). Explicit protection of children (true vulnerable individuals incapable of informed consent or risk assessment). Direct match to the 82–89 band. |

### B. Immigration Act 2005 (Zuwanderungsgesetz / Residence Act) — Dissected Core 1A (Xenos) Components

Classified as domestic law exercising national control (not Core 2A surrender of sovereignty). Only the Core 1A (Xenos) parts that create observable restrictions on voluntary residence/movement for non-citizens were scored.

| Component | Score | Band | Full Reasoning & Justification |
|-----------|-------|------|--------------------------------|
| **General residence title requirement + eligibility conditions + administrative/procedural rules** | **48** | 44–50 (early ideological/identity-based controls) | Mandatory positive state approval for any non-EU/EEA foreigner to enter or remain. Creates ongoing identity-based control on residence and presence. Matches the same band and logic as Compulsory residence registration (Meldepflicht) at 48. |
| **Temporary residence permit (Aufenthaltserlaubnis – Section 7)** | **48** | 44–50 (early ideological/identity-based controls) | Residence is purpose-tied and time-limited with no general right to indefinite stay. Ongoing conditional restriction on voluntary residence and movement for non-citizens. |
| **Permanent settlement permit (Niederlassungserlaubnis – Section 9) and EU long-term residence permit (Section 9a)** | **48** | 44–50 (early ideological/identity-based controls) | Strict conversion thresholds (duration, livelihood, integration) keep the default state for foreigners as conditional/temporary. Prolonged barrier to full residence autonomy based on identity/status. |
| **Integration obligations (Sections 43–45) — mandatory courses + sanctions** | **64** | 60–69 (betterment of society restrictions) | Mandatory language/civics courses with enforceable sanctions (fines, reduced benefits, denial of title extension). Enforces societal conformity on the out-group; fits the betterment-of-society / active policing band. |
| **Residence allocation / residence rule (Section 12a)** | **38** | 37–43 (societal/cultural/behavioural conformity) | State can oblige certain foreigners to reside in a specific allocated location for up to 3 years. Direct restriction on voluntary movement and choice of residence for a designated out-group. |
| **Expulsion and obligation to leave (Section 50)** | **45** | 37–43 (societal/cultural/behavioural conformity) | Automatic obligation to leave when title expires or is invalid, with enforceable deportation. Enforceable limit on continued residence and movement for foreigners outside approved criteria. |
