### Broad Summary of the Current State of American Politics (Cultural 1A)

American politics on Cultural 1A is polarised. The Republican Party, under clear MAGA primacy, grounds legitimacy overwhelmingly in popular sovereignty and the concrete interests of living American citizens, producing a strong mundane profile. The Democratic Party remains mixed: its progressive and democratic-socialist wings treat systemic justice and equity as binding moral requirements, while its managerial and centrist currents emphasise competence, evidence and electability. The Green Party holds one of the strongest transcendent profiles through ecological and intergenerational duties. The Libertarian Party occupies a clear mundane position centred on individual consent and non-aggression.

### Democratic Party (as a totality)
**Final Score: 49**

The Democratic Party positions itself as the defender of fairness, opportunity and inclusive progress within a constitutional framework. Its core aims are reducing inequality, expanding rights and opportunity, and demonstrating competent governance, while managing deep internal tensions between transformative justice currents and more managerial ones.

#### Moral Justification
* Justice, fairness and the moral imperative to expand opportunity and reduce inequality appear regularly and often function as higher-order moral requirements.
* Evidence-based policy, economic outcomes, fiscal sustainability and governability remain central to the detailed defence of programmes.
* Natural-rights and constitutional language is retained but frequently subordinated to progressive fairness and equity claims.
* International and domestic policy blends moral-duty language with pragmatic assessments of national interest and institutional capacity.

The party’s language is mixed and contested. It claims both moral seriousness on justice and the practical capacity to govern. Justice and fairness are presented as substantive duties by its left, while competence, evidence and electability dominate the centre. Neither source fully displaces the other, producing a genuinely mixed profile.

#### Why this produces a final score of 49
Both transcendent (justice and fairness as moral goods) and mundane (evidence, outcomes, pragmatic governance) sources are active and recurring. Transcendent framing is more prominent than in the Republican Party but does not fully dominate detailed justification.

### Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, Progressive Left, Justice Democrats (Primary Faction)
**Final Score: 65**

This current treats systemic justice and the correction of structural inequalities as binding moral requirements. Its aim is transformative change pursued through the Democratic Party rather than pure outsider rupture.

#### Moral Justification
* Systemic justice and the moral imperative to correct structural inequalities are central.
* Equity, inclusion and solidarity with marginalised groups are treated as binding moral requirements.
* Existing economic and political arrangements are frequently criticised in moral terms.
* Evidence and feasibility are acknowledged but usually subordinated to the prior ethical framework of justice.
* Dignity-based and universalist language is prominent.

The language is moralised and justice-centred. Structural inequalities are framed as moral failures that demand correction, not merely as policy problems to be managed. The faction presents itself as the ethical core of the Democratic Party, insisting that competence and electability must not become substitutes for moral purpose.

#### Why this produces a final score of 65
Transcendent sources (systemic justice and equity as moral imperatives) carry substantial weight. Mundane considerations of evidence and governability are present but secondary, producing a clear transcendent lean.

### Bernie Sanders / DSA-aligned, Democratic Socialists, Hard Left (Primary Faction)
**Final Score: 72**

This current frames capitalism and concentrated economic power as fundamentally unjust systems requiring structural transformation. Its aim is economic rights, social ownership and solidarity as moral requirements.

#### Moral Justification
* Capitalism and concentrated economic power are treated as fundamentally unjust systems.
* Economic rights, social ownership and solidarity are framed as moral requirements rather than policy preferences.
* Class analysis and systemic critique supply a strong moral framework.
* Pragmatic and electoral considerations exist but are secondary to the ethical rejection of the current economic order.

The language is systemic and condemnatory. The existing economic order is presented as a moral failure that incrementalism cannot adequately address. The faction presents itself as the bearer of transformative justice against both capitalism and moderate accommodation within the Democratic Party.

#### Why this produces a final score of 72
The transcendent source is stronger than in the broader Progressive Left because the critique of capitalism is framed as a fundamental moral and systemic indictment.

### Hakeem Jeffries / Moderate Centrist Democrats (Primary Faction)
**Final Score: 42**

This current emphasises pragmatism, electability and workable governance. Fairness language is retained but tempered by feasibility and broader electoral appeal.

#### Moral Justification
* Pragmatism, electability and workable governance are central.
* Fairness and opportunity language is retained but tempered by concerns about feasibility and broader appeal.
* Evidence, outcomes and institutional stability carry substantial weight.
* Progressive moral claims are accepted selectively rather than as overriding imperatives.

The language is managerial and electoral. Justice claims are present but filtered through the requirements of winning and governing. The faction presents itself as the serious, electable face of the Democratic Party against both left maximalism and Republican populism.

#### Why this produces a final score of 42
Mundane sources (pragmatism, evidence, outcomes, institutional stability) are stronger than on the party’s left. Residual fairness language prevents a strongly mundane score.

### Institutional / Party Establishment (Secondary Faction)
**Final Score: 45**

This current prioritises institutional stability, legislative effectiveness and protection of the party’s governing capacity.

#### Moral Justification
* Institutional stability and legislative effectiveness are central.
* Moral and justice language is used but frequently subordinated to considerations of what can actually be passed and sustained.
* Donor, organisational and coalition-management realities shape justificatory emphasis.

The language is institutional and power-focused. The faction presents itself as the guardian of the party’s ability to hold and use power within the existing system.

#### Why this produces a final score of 45
Mundane sources (institutional effectiveness, pragmatic coalition management) are stronger than in the progressive primary factions. Residual justice language keeps the score from becoming strongly mundane.

## Green Party
**Final Score: 80**

The Green Party advocates systemic transformation grounded in ecological and social justice. Its core aim is to subordinate present economic preferences and short-term majorities to duties owed to the climate, future generations and non-human nature.

#### Moral Justification
* Ecological justice and duties to future generations and non-human nature are presented as binding moral requirements.
* Present democratic majorities and short-term economic preferences are treated as subordinate.
* Social and economic policy is framed as part of a comprehensive moral project of systemic transformation.
* Scientific evidence is used extensively but functions primarily as diagnostic support for the ethical imperative.

The language is strongly duty-based and future-oriented. Ecological and intergenerational obligations are treated as moral requirements that existing institutions and majorities have no right to ignore. The party presents itself as the political expression of an overriding moral obligation that conventional politics has failed to honour.

#### Why this produces a final score of 80
The transcendent source is primary and organising. Mundane sources are present but clearly subordinate, producing a strong transcendent lean.

## Libertarian Party
**Final Score: 21**

The Libertarian Party centres individual liberty, non-aggression and voluntary cooperation. Its core aim is to maximise personal autonomy and minimise state coercion.

#### Moral Justification
* Individual rights are frequently framed in natural-rights terms but operationalised through consent and non-aggression.
* Opposition to state coercion is justified by the value of individual freedom and the practical consequences of intervention.
* Free markets and voluntary exchange are treated as both morally preferable and empirically superior.
* Popular sovereignty is accepted but limited; the legitimacy of majoritarian coercion is constrained by prior individual rights.

The language is individualist and consent-centred. Higher moral abstraction is present but secondary to claims about individual agency and reciprocal non-interference. The party presents itself as the defender of personal liberty against both progressive and populist forms of majoritarianism.

#### Why this produces a final score of 21
Although natural-rights language appears, the dominant practical justification rests on individual consent, non-aggression and the outcomes of voluntary interaction, producing a clear mundane lean.

### Republican Party (as a totality)
**Final Score: 18**

The Republican Party emphasises national interest, popular sovereignty, individual responsibility and cultural continuity. Under MAGA primacy its dominant justificatory pattern centres on the democratic will and concrete interests of living American citizens.

#### Moral Justification
* Popular sovereignty and the interests of living American citizens are treated as primary sources of legitimacy across immigration, trade and many domestic domains.
* Tradition and established institutions are defended largely as empirically successful arrangements that serve current citizens.
* Individual responsibility, work and market outcomes feature heavily in economic justification.
* Natural-rights and constitutional language remains available but is secondary to sovereignty and reciprocity claims in most core policy areas.

The party’s language is demos-centred and citizen-interest focused. Elite, transnational and abstract universalist claims are frequently criticised as overrides of the American people. Residual institutional, religious and natural-rights language exists but does not organise the party’s centre of gravity.

#### Why this produces a final score of 18
The transcendent source has limited weight in substantive justification. Moral authority is derived primarily from popular will, national interest of living citizens, and pragmatic or reciprocal claims, producing a strong mundane lean.

### Donald Trump, MAGA, America First Populists (Primary Faction)
**Final Score: 14**

This current treats popular sovereignty and the national interest of living American citizens as the decisive sources of legitimacy. Its aim is to restore control of borders, trade and institutions to the American demos.

#### Moral Justification
* Popular sovereignty is treated as the primary and decisive source of political legitimacy.
* The national interest of living citizens (immigration, trade, security, industrial capacity) functions as a core moral and political standard.
* Elite institutions, transnational commitments and abstract universalist claims are frequently criticised as overriding the consent and interests of the demos.
* Natural-rights and constitutional language appears but is generally subordinated to sovereignty and citizen-interest arguments.

The language is confrontational and demos-centred. Institutions and elites are framed as having failed or betrayed ordinary Americans. The faction presents itself as the authentic vehicle for the neglected interests and democratic will of the American people.

#### Why this produces a final score of 14
The transcendent source has very limited organisational weight. Moral authority derives overwhelmingly from popular sovereignty and the concrete interests of living American citizens.

### JD Vance, National Conservatives, Post-Liberal Right (Secondary Faction)
**Final Score: 25**

This current treats the nation and its historical continuity as important sources of political meaning and obligation. Order, social cohesion and the common good of the political community are given higher priority than pure procedural or market liberalism.

#### Moral Justification
* The nation and its historical continuity are treated as important sources of political meaning and obligation.
* Order, social cohesion and the common good of the political community are prioritised.
* Popular sovereignty remains central but is interpreted through a more substantive national and cultural lens.
* Market outcomes and individual rights are accepted but subordinated to national and communal considerations.

The language is thicker and more particularist than pure MAGA populism. The faction presents itself as correcting both pure liberalism and pure populism in favour of a substantive national community.

#### Why this produces a final score of 25
Mundane sources remain dominant. The stronger emphasis on the nation as a moral community introduces a modestly thicker layer of non-individualist justification than pure MAGA populism.

### Traditional Fusionist Conservatives (Secondary Faction)
**Final Score: 28**

This current retains emphasis on individual responsibility, free markets, limited government and constitutionalism as valuable inheritances.

#### Moral Justification
* Individual responsibility, free markets and limited government remain central.
* Constitutionalism and institutional stability are frequently invoked as valuable inheritances.
* Social and moral traditionalism is usually framed as the defence of proven social arrangements.
* Natural-rights language appears more readily than in the MAGA current.

The language is institutional and economic-liberal. The faction presents itself as the steward of proven American institutions and economic freedom.

#### Why this produces a final score of 28
Mundane sources (individual responsibility, market outcomes, institutional pragmatism) are clearly primary. Residual natural-rights and traditional-moral language keep the score from falling as low as pure populist sovereignty currents.

### Mike Johnson, Religious Right, Christian Conservatives (Secondary Faction)
**Final Score: 40**

This current treats Christian moral teaching and religious liberty as fundamental goods that political authority should respect and, in some versions, actively support.

#### Moral Justification
* Christian moral teaching and religious liberty are treated as fundamental goods.
* Many social issues are justified by reference to religious and natural-law understandings of human flourishing and moral order.
* Popular sovereignty and constitutionalism remain important but are often interpreted through a religious-moral lens.
* The nation is frequently understood as having a Christian heritage that carries ongoing moral weight.

The language introduces a clearer transcendent element than other Republican factions. Religious and natural-law sources are regularly invoked as independent moral authorities, though they usually operate alongside rather than fully displacing popular sovereignty.

#### Why this produces a final score of 40
This faction shows a clear transcendent element because religious and natural-law sources are regularly invoked as independent moral authorities. These claims usually operate alongside popular sovereignty, producing a moderate transcendent lean relative to other Republican factions.

### Libertarian Conservatives (Secondary Faction)
**Final Score: 22**

This current prioritises individual rights, personal autonomy, consent and non-aggression, with greater scepticism toward both progressive and populist majoritarianism.

#### Moral Justification
* Individual rights and personal autonomy are central, often framed in natural-rights terms but operationalised through consent and non-aggression.
* Opposition to state coercion is justified by individual freedom and the practical consequences of intervention.
* Free markets and voluntary exchange are treated as morally preferable and empirically superior.
* Popular sovereignty is accepted but limited by prior individual rights.

The language is individualist and consent-centred. The faction presents itself as the defender of liberty against both left and right forms of majoritarian or state overreach.

#### Why this produces a final score of 22
Although natural-rights language is more prominent than in pure MAGA populism, the dominant practical justification rests on individual consent, non-aggression and the outcomes of voluntary interaction.
