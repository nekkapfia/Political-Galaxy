### Broad Summary of the Current State of French Politics (Cultural 1A)

French politics on Cultural 1A is structured by a strong popular-sovereignty / national-preference pole (Rassemblement National), a mixed republican and managerial centre (Renaissance and parts of Les Républicains), and left currents that combine systemic justice and ecological claims with varying degrees of popular-sovereignty language (La France Insoumise and the Socialist Party). Rassemblement National produces one of the strongest mundane profiles in Europe through national preference and the concrete interests of French citizens. La France Insoumise carries clearer transcendent weight on justice and ecology while still deploying popular-sovereignty rhetoric.

---

## La France Insoumise
**Final Score: 66**

La France Insoumise presents itself as the force of rupture with liberal economics, Atlanticist foreign policy and elite governance. Its core aims are social justice, ecological planning, popular sovereignty via institutional change, and solidarity with the disadvantaged.

#### Moral Justification
* The existing economic order is frequently criticised as systemically unjust.
* Ecological emergency is framed as a moral duty that requires planning and constraint on present consumption.
* Popular sovereignty is asserted against elites, European constraints and established institutions (often via calls for a new republic and citizen initiative).
* Solidarity and anti-austerity claims carry independent moral weight.
* National language (“chez nous”) is sometimes appropriated in social rather than ethnic terms.

The language mixes systemic justice, ecological duty and popular-sovereignty claims. The movement presents itself as the authentic voice of the people against a self-serving elite, while grounding much of its programme in moralised critiques of capitalism and ecological destruction.

#### Why this produces a final score of 66
Transcendent sources (systemic justice and ecological duty) are strong and organising, while popular-sovereignty language adds a mundane element. The overall profile remains clearly on the transcendent side of the French spectrum.

## Les Républicains
**Final Score: 26**

Les Républicains present themselves as the party of order, controlled immigration, economic competence and the defence of French republican institutions and identity. Their core aim is responsible government that prioritises security, national cohesion and fiscal seriousness.

#### Moral Justification
* Immigration and security are justified by the need to protect the concrete interests and safety of French citizens.
* Institutional continuity and governing competence are presented as practical necessities.
* National identity and republican order are defended as the framework that has historically served France.
* Residual Gaullist sovereignty language appears, but is usually secondary to order and competence.
* Elite and European constraints are criticised when seen to undermine national control.

The language is primarily mundane: national interest of living French citizens, order, competence and institutional stewardship. Residual republican and civilizational references exist but do not organise the core justificatory pattern.

#### Why this produces a final score of 26
Mundane sources (citizen interest, order, competence, institutional pragmatism) are primary. Transcendent sources are limited, producing a clear mundane lean, though less pure than Rassemblement National.

## Parti Socialiste
**Final Score: 47**

The Socialist Party retains social-democratic justice language within a republican and European framework. Its core aims are fairness, social protection and competent management of the welfare state.

#### Moral Justification
* Inequality and social vulnerability are framed as problems of justice that government has a duty to address.
* Public services and social protection are justified through both fairness and practical solidarity.
* Competence and evidence remain important to the party’s governing self-image.
* Republican and European commitments provide residual higher-order framing.

The language is mixed. Justice and solidarity claims are real but operate alongside strong managerial and institutional elements. The party presents itself as the responsible centre-left alternative to both radical left rupture and right-wing national preference.

#### Why this produces a final score of 47
Both transcendent (justice and solidarity) and mundane (competence, institutional continuity) sources are active, producing a mixed score.

## Rassemblement National
**Final Score: 15**

Rassemblement National presents itself as the party of national preference and restored French sovereignty. Its core aims are strict control of immigration, priority to French citizens in welfare, housing and employment, and the defence of French demographic and cultural continuity.

#### Moral Justification
* Immigration policy is justified by the right of the French people to decide who joins the national community and by the concrete costs imposed on housing, wages, security and services.
* “Priorité nationale” and reciprocity (“the French first”) function as organising principles.
* Elite, judicial and European constraints are criticised as overrides of democratic will and national interest.
* Cultural and civilizational continuity is defended as the inheritance of the living French population rather than as sacred or natural-law authority.
* Quantifiable benefits to current French citizens are central to justification.

The language is demos-centred, reciprocal and anti-elite. Virtually every major claim is grounded in the concrete interests and democratic will of living French citizens. Higher-law or sacred claims are almost entirely absent. The party presents itself as the straightforward vehicle for the neglected interests of the French people against institutions that have ceased to prioritise them.

#### Why this produces a final score of 15
The transcendent source is almost entirely absent. Moral authority is derived overwhelmingly from popular sovereignty and the concrete interests of living French citizens, producing an extremely strong mundane lean.

## Renaissance (Macronist centre)
**Final Score: 34**

Renaissance and the broader Macronist centre present themselves as the party of competence, European integration, economic modernisation and liberal social values. Their core aims are effective government, selective and controlled immigration, and the defence of a liberal republican order within Europe.

#### Moral Justification
* Competence, evidence and pragmatic problem-solving are central justificatory themes.
* European integration is frequently presented as both practical necessity and higher project.
* Individual opportunity and republican universalism provide residual framing.
* Immigration is justified through selective economic needs and republican integration rather than open-ended moral duty or pure national preference.

The language is technocratic and European-oriented, with residual republican universalism. The movement presents itself as the rational, competent alternative to both populist national preference and radical left rupture.

#### Why this produces a final score of 34
Mundane sources (competence, evidence, pragmatic governance) are strong. Residual republican and European higher-order language prevents a strongly mundane score, producing a moderate mixed-to-mundane profile.
