### Broad Summary of the Current State of German Politics (Cultural 1A)

German politics on Cultural 1A is structured by a strong mundane/popular-sovereignty pole (AfD), a mixed institutional and competence-oriented centre-right (CDU/CSU), liberal individualist currents (FDP), a strongly transcendent ecological and intergenerational pole (Greens), and left currents that combine systemic justice claims with social-democratic or anti-capitalist language (SPD and Left Party). AfD produces one of the purest mundane profiles in Europe through national interest of living German citizens and rejection of what it regards as elite overrides on immigration and identity.

---

## AfD
**Final Score: 12**

The AfD presents itself as the party of national self-preservation and democratic reassertion. Its core aims are an immediate halt to irregular immigration, the deportation of those without legal status, and the defence of the concrete interests and cultural continuity of the existing German population.

#### Moral Justification
* Immigration policy is justified by the right of the German people to control their borders and by the concrete costs and security risks imposed on citizens.
* National interest of living Germans and reciprocity function as organising principles.
* Elite, judicial, media and European constraints are criticised as overrides of democratic will.
* Cultural continuity is defended as the inheritance and cohesion of the existing population rather than as sacred authority.
* Recent internal adjustments have softened certain terms while retaining the substance of demographic and cultural self-preservation.

The language is demos-centred, reciprocal and anti-elite. Higher-law or sacred claims are almost entirely absent. The party presents itself as the vehicle for ordinary Germans whose interests have been subordinated to migration and elite projects.

#### Why this produces a final score of 12
The transcendent source is effectively absent. Moral authority is derived overwhelmingly from popular sovereignty and the concrete interests of living German citizens, producing an extremely strong mundane lean.

---

## CDU/CSU
**Final Score: 27**

The CDU/CSU present themselves as the party of order, controlled migration, economic competence and defence of the constitutional and social-market order. Their core aim is responsible government that maintains stability while tightening control over immigration.

#### Moral Justification
* Immigration and security are justified by the need to protect the concrete interests and safety of German citizens and to preserve social cohesion.
* Institutional continuity, the Basic Law and governing competence are presented as practical necessities.
* Residual Christian-democratic and social-market language remains, but is secondary to order and competence.
* Elite and European constraints are criticised when seen to undermine national control capacity.

The language is primarily mundane: citizen interest, order, competence and institutional stewardship. Residual Christian-democratic and constitutional-patriotic references exist but do not organise the core pattern.

#### Why this produces a final score of 27
Mundane sources (citizen interest, order, competence, institutional pragmatism) are primary. Transcendent sources are limited, producing a clear mundane lean.

## FDP
**Final Score: 29**

The FDP centres individual liberty, market mechanisms, fiscal responsibility and civil rights. Its core aim is to maximise personal autonomy and economic dynamism within a liberal constitutional order.

#### Moral Justification
* Individual liberty and personal autonomy are treated as fundamental.
* Market outcomes and limited government are justified by both principle and empirical results.
* Evidence, competence and fiscal responsibility are central.
* Rights language is operationalised through consent, procedure and non-interference.

The language is individualist, evidence-oriented and anti-statist. The party presents itself as the defender of personal and economic freedom against both progressive moralism and populist majoritarianism.

#### Why this produces a final score of 29
Mundane sources (individual agency, market outcomes, evidence, procedural rights) are primary. Residual rights language does not rise to independent higher-law status.

---

## Greens
**Final Score: 76**

The Greens present ecological breakdown and intergenerational injustice as defining moral crises. Their core aim is systemic transformation to protect the climate, future generations and the natural world.

#### Moral Justification
* Ecological and intergenerational duties are treated as binding moral requirements that can override present majorities and short-term economic preferences.
* Economic arrangements are criticised in moral terms for their destructive impact.
* Scientific evidence is used extensively but functions primarily as support for a prior ethical imperative.
* Progressive social justice claims operate alongside the ecological core.

The language is strongly duty-based and future-oriented. The party presents itself as the political expression of moral obligations that conventional democratic and economic decision-making has failed to honour.

#### Why this produces a final score of 76
The transcendent source is primary and organising. Present democratic will and economic interests are regularly subordinated, producing a strong transcendent lean.

---

## Left Party (Die Linke)
**Final Score: 67**

The Left Party frames capitalism and militarism as fundamental injustices. Its core aims are radical redistribution, public ownership, peace and solidarity with the disadvantaged.

#### Moral Justification
* The existing economic order is criticised as systemically unjust.
* Redistribution, public ownership and social rights are presented as moral requirements.
* Anti-militarism and international solidarity carry independent moral weight.
* Incrementalism is often viewed as inadequate.

The language is systemic and justice-centred. The party presents itself as the bearer of anti-capitalist and peace-oriented moral purpose against both mainstream social democracy and the right.

#### Why this produces a final score of 67
Transcendent sources (systemic justice and anti-militarism) are strong, producing a clear transcendent lean.

---

## SPD
**Final Score: 48**

The SPD presents itself as the party of social justice, solidarity and competent welfare-state management. Its core aims are fairness, social protection and stable governance.

#### Moral Justification
* Inequality and social vulnerability are framed as problems of justice that government has a duty to address.
* Public services and social protection are justified through fairness and practical solidarity.
* Competence, evidence and coalition governance remain important.
* Residual social-democratic moral language operates alongside strong managerial elements.

The language is mixed. Justice and solidarity claims are real but operate alongside managerial and institutional constraints. The party presents itself as the responsible centre-left force capable of governing.

#### Why this produces a final score of 48
Both transcendent (justice and solidarity) and mundane (competence, institutional continuity) sources are active, producing a mixed score.
