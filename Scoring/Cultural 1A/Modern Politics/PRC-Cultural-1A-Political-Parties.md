### Broad Summary

The PRC is a single-party system. The Chinese Communist Party is the sole ruling party; other legally existing parties operate under its leadership and do not function as independent competitors. Analysis therefore centres on the CCP itself. Under Xi Jinping the Party has deliberately reasserted ideological, historical and civilizational claims while retaining performance legitimacy, producing a mixed profile with significant transcendent elements.

## Chinese Communist Party (CCP)
**Final Score: 47**

The Chinese Communist Party presents itself as the necessary leadership force for the great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation, the continuation of the historical mission of national salvation and modernisation, and the guarantor of stability, development and civilizational confidence. Its core claim is that without the Party there would be no New China and no national rejuvenation.

#### Moral Justification
* “Realizing the great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation is the greatest dream of the Chinese nation in modern times.” The Chinese Dream is presented as both a collective historical mission and a personal aspiration linked to national strength.
* Party leadership is described as “the most essential characteristic of socialism with Chinese characteristics,” “the foundation and lifeblood of the party and the country,” and a historical choice of the people. “China’s success hinges on the party.”
* Marxism-Leninism, Mao Zedong Thought and Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era are treated as guiding truth and the moral-political compass for the nation.
* Performance legitimacy (growth, poverty reduction, technological strength, stability, common prosperity) is retained but explicitly subordinated to and integrated with the ideological and civilizational mission.
* Civilizational self-confidence and the right of China to its own path are regularly asserted against Western universalist claims.
* Popular sovereignty in the liberal-democratic sense is not a primary source; the Party claims to embody the fundamental interests of the Chinese people and the nation across time.

The language fuses historical necessity, civilizational destiny and performance. The Party presents itself as the indispensable agent chosen by history and the people to achieve national rejuvenation. Transcendent-style claims (historical laws, civilizational mission, ideological guidance, the Party as embodiment of the people’s fundamental interests across generations) have been deliberately re-elevated under Xi and now organise the overall justificatory framework, while concrete results remain indispensable supporting evidence of the Party’s correct leadership.

#### Why this produces a final score of 47
Transcendent sources (historical necessity of Party leadership, civilizational mission, ideological guidance) are significant and organising. They operate alongside strong performance/mundane sources. The result is a mixed profile with clearer transcendent weight than in the high-performance reform era of the 1990s–2000s, but still far from the totalising ideological scores of the Maoist period. The score reflects a genuine dual structure rather than pure performance legitimacy or pure ideological totalism.
