### Broad Summary of the Current State of Russian Politics (Cultural 1A)

Russian politics on Cultural 1A is dominated by a state-centric, sovereignty-focused formation (United Russia and the presidential vertical) that grounds legitimacy in the defence of Russian sovereignty, civilizational identity and historical continuity against external hostility. Residual Soviet justice language persists in the CPRF, while the LDPR emphasises nationalist popular sovereignty and great-power interest. Transcendent civilizational and traditional-values language is present but heavily fused with, and subordinated to, state interest and popular support for strong leadership.

---

## United Russia / Presidential Vertical
**Final Score: 13**

United Russia and the broader presidential vertical present themselves as the defenders of Russian sovereignty, civilizational identity, historical continuity and the state’s capacity to protect the nation against external hostility. Their core aim is the preservation and strengthening of a sovereign Russian state capable of defending its interests, values and way of life.

#### Moral Justification
* Sovereignty is treated as existential and non-negotiable.
* Civilizational distinctiveness of Russia and resistance to Western imposition of alien norms are regularly invoked.
* Historical continuity, traditional values (family, faith, patriotism) and unity of the people under strong leadership are presented as sources of strength.
* Performance in delivering stability, security and great-power status remains important.
* External threat and the need for national solidarity function as organising frames.

The language is state-centric, civilizational and sovereignty-focused. Higher claims about Russia’s historical destiny and traditional values appear, but they are tightly fused with the practical requirements of state power and popular support for the leadership. The formation presents itself as the indispensable guardian of Russia’s existence as a sovereign civilization-state.

#### Why this produces a final score of 13
Transcendent sources (civilizational mission, historical destiny, traditional values) are present but heavily subordinated to and fused with state interest and popular support for strong leadership. The overall profile remains strongly mundane/state-centric, with a civilizational overlay that does not displace popular-sovereignty and state-interest primacy.

## Communist Party of the Russian Federation (CPRF)
**Final Score: 35**

The CPRF mixes residual Soviet-era social justice and anti-oligarchic claims with state-patriotic and social-protection language. Its core aims are social guarantees, nostalgia for Soviet power and status, and defence of a strong Russian state.

#### Moral Justification
* Residual Soviet justice language frames oligarchic capitalism and social inequality as moral failures.
* Social protection and public ownership retain moral weight.
* State-patriotic and great-power claims are prominent and often primary in practice.
* Historical continuity with the Soviet period is treated as a source of legitimacy and pride.

The language is mixed. Soviet-era justice claims persist but operate alongside, and are frequently overshadowed by, patriotic and state-strength justifications. The party presents itself as the defender of social rights and Soviet historical achievement within a strong Russian state.

#### Why this produces a final score of 35
Transcendent sources (residual Soviet justice / historical mission) are present but mixed with strong patriotic and state-centric mundane sources, producing a moderate mixed-to-mundane profile.

## Liberal Democratic Party (LDPR)
**Final Score: 19**

The LDPR emphasises nationalist popular sovereignty, great-power interest and defence of the Russian people and Russian world. Its core aims are a strong state, controlled migration, and assertion of Russian interests.

#### Moral Justification
* Nationalist popular sovereignty and the interests of the Russian people are central.
* Great-power status and resistance to external weakness are organising themes.
* Criticism of uncontrolled migration and defence of Russian cultural space appear regularly.
* Higher civilizational or sacred claims are limited; the emphasis is on power, people and state.

The language is nationalist, popular-sovereignty and state-power oriented. The party presents itself as a more assertive and nationally focused force within the managed system.

#### Why this produces a final score of 19
The transcendent source is limited. Moral authority is derived primarily from nationalist popular sovereignty and great-power interest, producing a strong mundane lean.
