## AfD (Alternative für Deutschland) – Final Score: 5

The AfD presents itself as the party defending German sovereignty and national identity against what it describes as elite-driven policies on immigration, climate, and cultural change. It positions itself as the voice of ordinary German citizens who feel their concerns have been ignored by the political mainstream.

### Moral Justification Sources

The party grounds its positions almost exclusively in mundane sources centred on the interests and will of the existing German population:

- It repeatedly emphasises the sovereignty and democratic will of the German people as the ultimate source of legitimacy. Policies are justified on the grounds that they reflect what the majority of citizens want or what serves their practical interests.
- Immigration policy is framed primarily through the lens of protecting German citizens’ access to welfare, housing, and security. The party argues that uncontrolled immigration places unsustainable burdens on the existing population and undermines social cohesion.
- Climate and energy policies are criticised on empirical grounds of economic cost, energy security, and loss of industrial competitiveness, rather than through moral or transcendent claims about the planet or future generations.
- Opposition to gender ideology, multiculturalism, and supranational institutions is presented as resistance to elite projects that override the preferences and identity of the German people.

Transcendent sources play virtually no role in the party’s public justification. Even references to German culture and identity are treated as practical goods worth preserving for current citizens rather than as sacred or intrinsic values with independent moral standing. There are no meaningful appeals to divine authority, natural law, or duties that override the interests of the living German population.

### Why This Produces a Final Score of 5

The transcendent source is almost entirely absent from moral reasoning across the party’s core issue areas. All justification flows from popular sovereignty, national self-interest, and the tangible consequences of policies for living German citizens. According to the rubric, this level of practical absence of the transcendent source places the party at the highest end of mundane dominance short of the theoretical extreme. The Final Score of 5 reflects an extremely strong mundane lean.

## Bündnis 90/Die Grünen – Final Score: 82

Bündnis 90/Die Grünen positions itself as the party of ecological transformation and social justice. Its core identity rests on the conviction that environmental protection, social equity, democratic renewal, and peace are deeply interconnected and require fundamental systemic change.

### Moral Justification Sources

The party’s Grundsatzprogramm places strong and consistent emphasis on transcendent foundations:

- “Im Mittelpunkt unserer Politik steht der Mensch in seiner **Würde und Freiheit**.” This statement is presented as the starting point from which all other values derive.
- The five core values are explicitly listed as **Ökologie, Gerechtigkeit, Selbstbestimmung, Demokratie und Frieden**. These are framed not merely as policy preferences but as the fundamental moral framework guiding the party.
- Ecology is elevated to a moral responsibility. The protection of the natural environment is justified as essential for human dignity and as a duty toward future generations. The programme states that current generations must act in ways that do not compromise the ability of future generations to live in dignity and freedom.
- Human dignity and universal human rights are treated as non-negotiable foundations that extend across policy domains, including climate action, migration, gender equality, and social rights. These rights are presented as inherent rather than granted by the state or current majority opinion.

While scientific evidence on climate change, biodiversity loss, and ecosystem collapse is frequently cited, it functions primarily as supporting material. The moral “ought” — the necessity of radical transformation, acceptance of significant costs to the current generation, and reordering of economic priorities — derives from the transcendent ethical framework of ecological justice and intergenerational responsibility, not from empirical optimisation or short-term voter preferences alone.

This pattern is consistent across the party’s major policy areas. Climate and energy policy is justified through the moral duty to protect the planet and future generations. Social and economic policy is linked to the same overarching values of justice and self-determination. Even foreign policy positions are often framed through the lens of global justice and responsibility.

### Why This Produces a Final Score of 82

The transcendent source is dominant and organising across virtually all domains of moral justification. Moral authority flows primarily from ecological justice, duties to future generations, and the intrinsic value of human dignity and nature. Mundane sources (science, evidence, and pragmatic considerations) are actively present and used for diagnosis and implementation, but they remain subordinate to the higher ethical framework. According to the rubric, this constitutes significant practical absence of the mundane source as a co-equal foundation for the party’s most distinctive positions. The Final Score of 82 reflects a strong transcendent lean.

## CDU (Christlich Demokratische Union) – Final Score: 14

The CDU presents itself as the party of responsible governance, the social market economy, and institutional stability. It positions itself as a pragmatic centre-right force that draws on Christian-democratic traditions while prioritising economic competence and social cohesion in a modern Germany.

### Moral Justification Sources

The party’s Grundsatzprogramm references values such as **Freiheit, Solidarität und Gerechtigkeit**, with explicit roots in Christian social teaching. However, in contemporary documents and public argumentation, these values are framed in largely pragmatic terms:

- Moral justification is primarily grounded in the need for institutional continuity, economic stability, and responsible governance that serves the current population.
- Christian and cultural heritage is acknowledged as historically important in shaping German society and values, but it is usually presented as a source of social cohesion and ethical orientation rather than as active transcendent authority that overrides practical considerations.
- Policy positions on the economy, security, and European integration are typically justified through arguments of what works, what maintains Germany’s reliability as a partner, and what preserves social peace and prosperity for existing citizens.
- Tradition is invoked as a proven inheritance that has delivered stability and success, rather than as a sacred or divinely grounded order.

Transcendent elements from the party’s Christian-democratic roots have limited substantive presence in current moral reasoning. Even when values such as human dignity or solidarity are mentioned, they tend to function as general ethical background rather than as binding moral sources that can override empirical outcomes or the interests of the living population.

### Why This Produces a Final Score of 14

The transcendent source has only marginal influence in substantive justification. Moral authority rests overwhelmingly on pragmatic tradition, institutional stability, economic competence, and the practical interests of the current German population. According to the rubric, this level of practical absence of the transcendent source from core domains of moral reasoning places the party firmly in the upper range of mundane dominance. The Final Score of 14 reflects a very strong mundane lean.

## Die Linke – Final Score: 68

Die Linke positions itself as the party of social justice and solidarity with the marginalised. It seeks fundamental change to what it views as an unjust and exploitative capitalist system, advocating for a more egalitarian and democratic economic order.

### Moral Justification Sources

The party draws heavily on transcendent moral sources in its core argumentation:

- Social justice and solidarity are presented as fundamental moral imperatives rather than merely desirable policy outcomes.
- Capitalism is criticised not only for generating inequality and precariousness but as inherently unjust and exploitative in its structure.
- The party appeals to the inherent dignity of all people and frames its political project as a moral obligation to protect the weak and dismantle systemic forms of domination.
- Concepts such as “systemic injustice” and the need for “solidarity with the oppressed” carry strong ethical weight that goes beyond empirical measurement of poverty or inequality.

While the party frequently uses data on wealth distribution, precarious employment, and the effects of neoliberal policies, these serve primarily to support a deeper ethical claim: that the current economic order violates fundamental principles of justice and human dignity. The moral force of Die Linke’s positions comes largely from this condemnation of systemic exploitation rather than from pragmatic arguments about what is achievable within the existing system.

### Why This Produces a Final Score of 68

Transcendent sources (social justice and solidarity as moral imperatives against systemic injustice) carry primary weight in the party’s justification. Mundane analysis of economic outcomes and policy effects is present and used to illustrate problems, but it remains secondary to the ethical rejection of capitalism as an unjust system. According to the rubric, this constitutes a noticeable absence of the mundane source from meaningful portions of moral reasoning, where transcendent claims about justice and dignity predominate. The Final Score of 68 reflects a clear transcendent lean.

## FDP (Freie Demokratische Partei) – Final Score: 28

The FDP presents itself as the party of freedom, self-determination, and personal responsibility. It advocates for a liberal order that maximises individual liberty while emphasising that freedom must be paired with accountability and rational decision-making.

### Moral Justification Sources

The party’s Grundsatzprogramm (“Verantwortung für die Freiheit”) centres on a clear mundane framework:

- **Freiheit und Verantwortung** are presented as the central paired values. Freedom is understood primarily as individual self-determination, while responsibility is framed as personal accountability for one’s choices and their consequences.
- Moral justification is grounded in the principle that individuals should be free to shape their own lives, provided they bear the consequences of their actions. This leads to strong support for performance-based justice, reduced state intervention, and clear rules that enable predictable individual planning.
- The rule of law, evidence-based policy, and rational discourse are treated as essential foundations of a free society. The party argues that the state’s role is to protect individual freedom and create the conditions for self-responsibility rather than to pursue collective moral projects or redistribute outcomes according to abstract notions of justice.
- Human rights are defended, but they are generally grounded in the liberal tradition of individual liberty and the rule of law rather than in transcendent or inherent moral claims independent of a functioning legal order.

Transcendent sources play a very limited role. Even when the party speaks of dignity or fairness, these concepts are usually tied to individual autonomy and responsible behaviour rather than to higher moral orders or duties that override personal choice and empirical outcomes.

### Why This Produces a Final Score of 28

The party relies primarily on mundane sources: individual freedom, personal responsibility, rational self-determination, and the rule of law. Transcendent moral claims are minimal and do not carry significant weight in justification. According to the rubric, this constitutes a noticeable but not extreme absence of the transcendent source. The Final Score of 28 reflects a mild mundane lean.

## SPD (Sozialdemokratische Partei Deutschlands) – Final Score: 38

The SPD presents itself as the party of social justice and solidarity within a democratic framework. It seeks to combine traditional social democratic values with pragmatic governance in a modern, globalised economy.

### Moral Justification Sources

The party’s core values are **Freiheit, Gerechtigkeit und Solidarität**. These are presented as equally important and interdependent:

- Justice and solidarity are frequently invoked in strong moral terms, particularly when discussing social inequality, labour rights, the welfare state, and the need to protect vulnerable groups. The party often frames its mission as correcting systemic disadvantages and ensuring that all citizens can participate fully in society.
- At the same time, the SPD places significant emphasis on pragmatic, evidence-based policy. It argues that social justice must be pursued in ways that are economically sustainable and compatible with a competitive market economy.
- The Hamburg Programme and subsequent documents combine moral appeals to justice and solidarity with practical arguments about what is achievable through responsible governance, social partnership, and targeted state intervention.

The party therefore operates with a genuine mix of sources. Transcendent language around justice and solidarity provides moral force, while mundane considerations of economic feasibility, evidence, and governability carry substantial weight in actual policy justification.

### Why This Produces a Final Score of 38

Both transcendent (justice and solidarity as moral imperatives) and mundane (pragmatic governance, evidence, and economic sustainability) sources are actively used and integrated. Mundane reasoning tends to carry more consistent weight in detailed policy development, while transcendent moral language strengthens the party’s claims on fairness and social cohesion. According to the rubric, this represents functional integration of both sources with only minor variation in emphasis. The Final Score of 38 reflects a mild mundane lean.
