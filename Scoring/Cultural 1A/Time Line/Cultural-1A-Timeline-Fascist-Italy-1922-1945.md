# Cultural 1A: Eras of Moral Justification in Fascist Italy (1922–1945)

Italian Fascism rejected both liberal individualism and Marxist class struggle in favour of a totalitarian conception of the ethical state and the historical mission of the Italian nation. Authority was located in the state as a spiritual and ethical entity and in the Duce as its interpreter. The regime’s twenty-year history shows a clear movement from early consolidation and revolutionary rhetoric toward increasing totalitarian radicalisation and, finally, wartime subordination to the German alliance.

## **1922–1929: Seizure of Power and Early Consolidation – Final Score: 68**

The March on Rome and the early years of the regime established the core Fascist claim that the liberal state had failed and that a new ethical and national order was required. Mussolini still governed within a formally monarchical framework and relied on traditional elites, the Church, and pragmatic appeals to order.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* The liberal parliamentary system was rejected as weak, corrupt, and incapable of expressing the true will of the nation.
* The state was increasingly presented as an ethical entity that stood above individuals and classes.
* National renewal, order, and the restoration of Italian greatness after the “mutilated victory” of the First World War supplied powerful mobilising language.
* The Duce was already positioned as the unique interpreter of the nation’s needs, though the full cult of personality and totalitarian structure were not yet complete.
* Pragmatic appeals to economic recovery, social peace, and the ending of political violence remained important in consolidating support.

The transcendent source (ethical state, national historical mission, leader as interpreter) was already primary, but residual traditional, monarchical, Catholic, and pragmatic elements kept the profile from reaching later intensity.

**Key Shift:** This era installed the ethical-state and national-mission framework while still operating within a hybrid institutional environment.

### Why this produces a final score of 68
Transcendent sources were dominant but not yet totalising. Residual pragmatic and traditional justifications remained visible and functionally important.

---

## **1929–1936: Totalitarian Turn and Imperial Ambition – Final Score: 73**

The Lateran Pacts, the construction of the corporative state, the cult of the Duce, and the turn toward imperial expansion marked a clear radicalisation. The regime sought to penetrate and remake society more thoroughly.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* The state as ethical and spiritual entity was asserted more comprehensively; intermediate institutions and individual rights were further subordinated.
* The cult of the Duce intensified; Mussolini was presented as the embodiment of the nation’s will and historical destiny.
* Corporatism was justified as the ethical organisation of the nation rather than as pure economic pragmatism.
* Imperial expansion (most clearly in Ethiopia) was framed as the recovery of Roman greatness and the historical right of a young, vital nation.
* Anti-liberal and anti-Bolshevik language hardened into a more systematic rejection of both Western democracy and Marxist internationalism.

The transcendent source strengthened. The regime’s self-presentation shifted further from pragmatic order toward spiritual and historical regeneration.

**Key Shift:** This era completed the move from hybrid authoritarian consolidation to a more fully totalitarian and imperial self-understanding.

### Why this produces a final score of 73
Transcendent sources (ethical state, historical destiny, leader cult) became more organising. Mundane and traditional residues declined in relative weight.

---

## **1936–1943: Axis Alignment and Wartime Radicalisation – Final Score: 77**

Alliance with Nazi Germany, the introduction of racial laws, and entry into the Second World War pushed Fascist justificatory language to its highest intensity. The regime increasingly adopted a more biological and civilizational framing while remaining distinct from National Socialism.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Historical destiny and the mission of the Italian nation were asserted with greater urgency in the context of European war.
* Racial legislation (1938 onward) introduced a biological element previously less central to Italian Fascism, justified as defence of the nation’s integrity.
* The war was framed as a struggle against the decadent democratic and plutocratic powers and for a new European order.
* The ethical state and the Duce’s authority continued to override ordinary legal and popular constraints.
* Performance and military success were interpreted as confirmation of the regime’s historical correctness.

The transcendent source reached its peak within the Fascist period. The regime’s dependence on German power and the military disasters that followed began to erode practical legitimacy, but the official justificatory language remained highly ideological.

**Key Shift:** This era brought Fascist Italy closest to a full transcendent mobilisation of historical destiny, racial integrity, and anti-liberal mission.

### Why this produces a final score of 77
Transcendent sources were primary and organising across foreign policy, racial legislation, and wartime mobilisation. The score remains below the Nazi extreme because Italian Fascism never achieved the same degree of biological totalisation or eliminationist consistency.

---

## **1943–1945: Italian Social Republic – Final Score: 71**

After the fall of Mussolini and the German occupation of the north, the Italian Social Republic (Salo) attempted to revive a more “revolutionary” and anti-monarchical Fascism under German protection. The context of civil war and foreign domination limited its autonomy.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Residual Fascist ideology of the ethical state, anti-liberalism, and national destiny continued to be proclaimed.
* The monarchy and the “betrayal” of 1943 were denounced as proof of the need for a purer Fascist order.
* German power and the continuing war against the Allies and the Italian Resistance framed all practical choices.
* Popular consent and ordinary welfare were largely irrelevant; survival of the Fascist remnant and loyalty to the alliance dominated.

The transcendent language of Fascism persisted but was hollowed out by military defeat, foreign control, and civil war. The regime’s ability to generate independent moral authority collapsed.

**Key Shift:** This final phase preserved Fascist ideological claims while revealing their dependence on power and their inability to survive military catastrophe.

### Why this produces a final score of 71
Transcendent Fascist sources remained formally primary, but the extreme circumstances and loss of autonomy reduced the regime’s capacity to function as an independent justificatory system. The score reflects residual ideological intensity under conditions of collapse.
