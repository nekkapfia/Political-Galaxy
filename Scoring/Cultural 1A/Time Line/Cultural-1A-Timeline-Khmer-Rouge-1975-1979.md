# Cultural 1A: Eras of Moral Justification under the Khmer Rouge / Democratic Kampuchea (1975–1979)

Democratic Kampuchea lasted less than four years, yet it produced one of the most extreme transcendent profiles of the twentieth century. The regime fused radical Maoist-inspired communism with intense agrarian utopianism, racial-national purification, and the total rejection of the previous society. “Year Zero” ideology treated the destruction of urban, commercial, educated, and “contaminated” elements as a moral and historical necessity. Because of the regime’s brevity and internal consistency, a single primary era is analysed, with a brief note on the final collapse.

## **1975–1978: Year Zero and Total Revolutionary Purification – Final Score: 93**

The seizure of Phnom Penh and the immediate evacuation of cities inaugurated a project of unprecedented social destruction. Angkar (the Organisation) claimed total authority as the embodiment of the correct revolutionary line. Individual lives, intermediate institutions, money, markets, religion, and family autonomy were treated as obstacles to purity.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Revolutionary purity and the creation of a classless, agrarian, self-sufficient society were treated as absolute moral and historical imperatives.
* The previous society—urban, educated, commercial, foreign-influenced—was framed as irredeemably contaminated; its destruction was required for national and revolutionary rebirth.
* Angkar’s authority was total and self-justifying; no independent legal, moral, or popular standard was recognised.
* Racial and national purification (targeting Vietnamese, Cham Muslims, and “impure” Khmers) was fused with class ideology.
* Immediate human cost, technical expertise, and ordinary welfare were systematically disregarded. Death on a massive scale was absorbed into the logic of purification.
* Foreign models (even other communist ones) were increasingly rejected in favour of a pure, self-reliant Khmer revolutionary path.

The transcendent source reached an extreme intensity rarely matched in modern history. Moral authority derived entirely from the claimed historical and revolutionary necessity of total purification. Mundane considerations of popular survival, consent, competence, or even the basic reproduction of society were actively suppressed.

**Key Shift:** This era implemented one of the purest and most destructive fusions of communist historical necessity with agrarian utopianism and racial-national purification.

### Why this produces a final score of 93
Transcendent sources (revolutionary purity, Year Zero ideology, elimination of contamination) were overwhelmingly dominant. Mundane sources were not merely secondary; they were treated as obstacles to be destroyed. The score reflects near-total subordination of every human and practical consideration to the purity project.

---

## **1978–1979: Purges, War, and Collapse – Final Score: 88**

Border conflict with Vietnam, internal purges of suspected traitors, and the final Vietnamese invasion occurred while the regime continued to apply its purity logic. The justificatory framework did not moderate; it simply failed under external military power.

### Moral Justification Sources – Expanded Analysis

* Internal purges were justified as the continuing necessity of rooting out contamination and treason within the revolutionary ranks.
* The war with Vietnam was framed in both ideological and national-racial terms.
* Angkar’s claim to total authority and the purity project remained formally intact until the regime’s flight from Phnom Penh.
* Practical military and administrative collapse did not produce a visible turn toward pragmatic or popular-consent justifications.

The transcendent purity logic persisted until the end. The lower score relative to the peak years reflects the regime’s loss of control rather than any internal moderation of its justificatory sources.

**Key Shift:** The final phase demonstrated that the regime’s transcendent framework contained no mechanism for pragmatic self-correction even in the face of total defeat.

### Why this produces a final score of 88
Transcendent sources remained dominant in official and practical reasoning until military collapse. The slight reduction from the peak score reflects the disintegration of the regime’s capacity to implement its vision rather than a change in the vision itself.
