# Guide: Creating and Scoring an Era Section

This guide explains how to define an era and produce a clear, consistent, and methodologically sound scoring section for sliders in the 14-Axis Political Slider System (e.g. Cultural 1A: Foundation). It is designed to produce clean, exportable documents while maintaining rigour and consistency across the framework.

## 1. Preparation

Before writing anything:

- Confirm you are using the **exact definition** of the active slider.
- Review the **Universal Slider Rules** (empirical reality only, totality judgment, non-linear bands, etc.).
- Decide the chronological scope of the era. Eras should be defined by meaningful shifts in the observable phenomenon being measured.
- Gather primary evidence focused on the slider’s specific domains or components.

## 2. Define the Era

Give the era a clear, thematic name that reflects its character.

**Recommended format**:
> **Era X: [Thematic Name] (Date Range)**

Place the **final score** directly below the era title.

**Example**:
```markdown
# Era 1: Post-War Period (Church of England) (1945–1966)
**Score: 86**
```

## 3. Create the Domain/Component Table

Break the era down into the slider’s defined domains or components. This provides transparency.

**Recommended table columns**:
- Domain
- Individual Score
- Key Observable Evidence

Score each domain individually using the slider’s bands as reference. Treat these scores as **guideposts**, not as a rigid mathematical average.

## 4. Write the Era Summary (Holistic Assessment)

This is the core of the section. The summary should:

- Describe the **overall tone and tenor** of the era.
- Clearly state the **predominant pattern**.
- Explain why the era belongs in a particular band, using domain scores as supporting evidence.
- Remain holistic — avoid turning it into a domain-by-domain list.

**Suggested structure**:
- Paragraph 1: Overall tone and predominant pattern.
- Paragraph 2: Supporting institutional, cultural, or policy context.

Refer to the era simply as “the post-war period” (or equivalent) inside the summary text.

## 5. Select the Band and Assign an Exact Score

After completing the holistic assessment:

1. Compare the era against the **band descriptors** of the active slider.
2. Check the bands immediately above and below.
3. Choose an **exact numerical score** (not just a band range).

**Key principles**:
- Prioritise the holistic character of the era.
- Use domain scores as supporting indicators.
- Justify why the score sits in the upper, middle, or lower part of the chosen band.

## 6. Recommended Final Document Structure

```markdown
# Era X: [Thematic Name] (Date Range)
**Score: XX**

## Domain Scoring Table – Era X

| Domain | Individual Score | Key Observable Evidence |
|--------|------------------|-------------------------|
| ...    | ...              | ...                     |

## Era Summary

[Holistic narrative — usually 2 paragraphs]
```

## 7. Best Practices

| Practice                    | Recommendation                                                                 | Why It Matters |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| Holistic first             | Write the summary from an overall perspective before finalising the score       | Prevents mechanical averaging |
| Evidence-based             | Ground claims in observable enforcement within the slider’s defined domains     | Maintains empirical integrity |
| Band justification         | Explicitly compare with the band above and below                                | Ensures correct placement |
| Conciseness                | Aim for 2 well-written paragraphs in the summary                                | Improves readability |
| Consistency                | Use similar structure and tone across all eras                                  | Enables easy comparison |
| Avoid rhetoric             | Never rely on claims, intentions, or moral framing — only observable patterns   | Follows universal rules |

## 8. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Turning the Era Summary into a list of what happened in each domain.
- Justifying the score solely through domain averages instead of holistic character.
- Using vague language instead of referencing specific band descriptors.
- Forgetting to check the bands immediately above and below.
- Making era names overly long or descriptive.

## 9. Quick Workflow Summary

1. Define era name and date range.
2. Score each domain individually with supporting evidence.
3. Write a holistic Era Summary describing tone and predominant pattern.
4. Compare against band descriptors (and adjacent bands).
5. Assign an exact score.
6. Format cleanly using the recommended structure.

---

**Notes**:
- This guide can be adapted for any slider in the framework.
- Keep final documents clean by moving detailed band comparisons and scoring reasoning into separate notes if needed.
- Consistency across eras is more important than perfection in any single section.
