# Political Parties

Modern Britain presents a divided picture on Cultural 2B. The major parties display markedly different emotional and cultural stances toward the nation’s history, symbols, founding myths, people, and collective identity. Restore Britain and Reform maintain strongly affirmative positions, treating national pride as legitimate and healthy while resisting shame-based narratives. The Conservatives occupy a more mixed position, combining rhetorical support for national identity with frequent qualifications and limited institutional promotion of everyday pride. Labour and the Liberal Democrats lean toward discomfort with traditional national pride, with the latter showing greater willingness to constrain its public expression where they hold power. The Green Party represents the clearest and most systematic orientation toward shame and atonement, framing British history primarily through the lens of exploitation and advocating deconstruction of traditional national identity. Overall, Britain exhibits a fragmented cultural stance, with growing political contestation over whether national history and identity should be sources of pride, qualified acknowledgement, or active critique and atonement.

## Conservatives – Final Score: 52

The Conservative Party maintains a mixed and often qualified stance toward British national identity and history. While it has generally defended traditional symbols and resisted the most aggressive forms of decolonisation, it has not developed a consistently affirmative approach to national pride.

### Emotional and Cultural Stance – Expanded Analysis

- Record in government shows general protection of national symbols and resistance to large-scale statue removals, but limited active promotion of everyday national pride.
- Rhetoric frequently combines affirmations of British history with shame-introducing caveats (“proud of our history, but we must acknowledge the difficult parts”).
- Local government record is mixed, with many Conservative councils taking a relatively low-key approach to national symbolism outside of official or sporting occasions.
- Opposition to certain “woke” initiatives in education and institutions has been inconsistent, with some figures defending national pride more robustly than others.
- No strong institutional push to make national pride a central feature of public life or education during periods in power.

The Conservatives display a pattern of defensive rather than assertive national pride. They have generally sought to protect existing symbols and historical narratives from the most radical challenges, but have not pursued a coherent project of strengthening everyday national identity or treating pride as a central cultural good. Their approach remains reactive and qualified rather than proactive and affirmative.

## Greens – Final Score: 11

The Green Party adopts the most consistently shame-oriented stance toward British national identity and history among the major parties. It frames British history primarily through narratives of exploitation, colonialism, and moral failure, while advocating significant deconstruction of traditional national identity.

### Emotional and Cultural Stance – Expanded Analysis

- Strong and consistent support for decolonisation of education and public institutions, with British history presented largely through the lens of oppression and atonement.
- Active support for critical reframing or removal of statues and monuments associated with British history in areas where they have influence.
- Frequent use of comparative framing that positions other countries (particularly Germany and Nordic nations) as morally superior models for confronting historical guilt.
- Rhetoric that treats traditional expressions of British national pride as outdated, exclusionary, or tied to harmful legacies.
- Clear intent, where they have had local influence, to shift institutional and cultural emphasis away from uncomplicated national pride toward narratives of historical responsibility and atonement.

The Greens have developed a coherent ideological position in which British national history and identity are treated as sources of shame requiring active critique and transformation. Their approach subordinates positive national narratives to frameworks of guilt and deconstruction. While they have not yet had national power to implement these policies at scale, their record in areas of local influence and their consistent public positioning indicate they would pursue systematic efforts to diminish traditional national pride and promote atonement-focused approaches if they gained greater power.

## Labour – Final Score: 33

Labour maintains a mixed but shame-leaning stance toward British national identity. While the current leadership has made efforts to express positive patriotism, the party’s broader record and internal dynamics reveal significant discomfort with traditional expressions of national pride.

### Emotional and Cultural Stance – Expanded Analysis

- Leadership has issued positive statements about British identity and symbols, but these are frequently balanced with critical or qualifying language.
- In areas of local government control, Labour administrations have often supported critical reframing of historical statues through explanatory plaques and have shown limited enthusiasm for grassroots national flag campaigns.
- Significant internal and activist pressure within the party favours decolonisation narratives and critical approaches to British history.
- Record in national government shows reluctance to strongly champion everyday national pride, with greater emphasis on inclusive and internationalist framings.
- Tolerance of institutional initiatives that treat aspects of British history as inherently problematic.

Labour’s position reflects an ongoing tension between attempts to reclaim patriotic language at the leadership level and deeper institutional and activist currents that view traditional British national identity with suspicion. The party has not developed a coherent affirmative stance toward national history and pride, and its actions in areas it controls suggest a preference for critical rather than celebratory approaches to the national past.

## Liberal Democrats – Final Score: 29

The Liberal Democrats display a consistently negative stance toward traditional expressions of British national pride. They have shown willingness to constrain public displays of national identity and support critical reframing of British history where they have held power.

### Emotional and Cultural Stance – Expanded Analysis

- In areas of local government influence, Liberal Democrat councillors and administrations have attempted to restrict flag flying and expressed discomfort with prominent displays of the national flag.
- Rhetoric routinely qualifies national pride with internationalist framing and frequently portrays strong expressions of British identity as outdated or exclusionary.
- Support for critical reinterpretation of historical symbols and figures through plaques and contextualisation.
- Comparative framing that occasionally positions other European countries as more progressive or morally advanced models.
- Limited enthusiasm for traditional national commemorations or symbols as sources of collective pride.

The Liberal Democrats treat traditional national pride as something requiring qualification or restraint. Their record in local government demonstrates a willingness to use institutional power to limit everyday expressions of national identity, particularly through restrictions on flag flying. This pattern, combined with their broader ideological preference for internationalist and critical approaches to British history, indicates they would continue to de-centre and constrain traditional national pride if they gained greater power.

---

## Reform – Final Score: 77

Reform maintains a strongly affirmative stance toward British national identity and history. The party actively resists shame-based narratives and positions national pride as a legitimate and positive force.

### Emotional and Cultural Stance – Expanded Analysis

- Clear and consistent opposition to guilt-based interpretations of British history, including resistance to decolonisation initiatives in education and culture.
- Strong rhetorical defence of British achievements, symbols, and continuity.
- Local government record of opposing measures that seek to critically reframe or remove national symbols and historical monuments.
- Emphasis on restoring national pride and confidence as a central political objective.
- Framing of shame-oriented approaches to British history as divisive and harmful to social cohesion.

Reform has developed a coherent position that treats national pride as healthy and necessary. The party actively challenges shame-based narratives around British history and supports the protection and celebration of national symbols. Its record in areas of local influence and its national platform indicate that, if it gained significant power, it would pursue policies aimed at strengthening rather than diminishing traditional national identity and pride.

---

## Restore Britain – Final Score: 84

Restore Britain holds the most consistently affirmative position on British national identity among the six parties. It explicitly defends the legitimacy of national pride and frames the protection and restoration of traditional British identity as a central political goal.

### Emotional and Cultural Stance – Expanded Analysis

- Explicit rhetorical affirmation that “it is ok to be British” and that national pride should not be treated as suspect.
- Strong emphasis on generational continuity, stewardship of the land, and the value of traditional British identity.
- Clear opposition to shame-based and decolonisation narratives in education, culture, and public institutions.
- Local and campaign activity focused on resisting measures that seek to critically reframe or diminish British history and symbols.
- Framing of efforts to erode national identity (particularly through mass immigration and cultural change) as a form of national betrayal.

Restore Britain has developed the clearest and most unapologetic defence of British national pride and historical continuity among the major parties. Its positions and rhetoric indicate that, if it gained national power, it would actively promote national pride, protect traditional symbols and historical narratives, and resist institutional efforts to impose shame or deconstruction on British identity. The party’s approach is affirmative rather than merely defensive.
