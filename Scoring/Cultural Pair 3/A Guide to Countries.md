# Guide: How to Produce Cultural 3A & 3B Country Timeline Documents

This guide explains how to create high-quality documents analysing shifts in the definition and permeability of the core in-group according to **ethnic/ancestral criteria (3A)** and **cultural adoption/transformation requirements (3B)**. It produces consistent, well-structured, and analytically rigorous results.

## 1. Preparation and Scope Definition

### Define the time period
- Choose a clear starting point that marks a significant baseline (e.g. 1945/1948 for Britain, or a major constitutional/legal change for other countries).
- Decide whether the document will run to the present day.

### Define the analytical focus clearly
- The documents analyse **observable mechanisms** defining full membership in the core in-group.
- **3A Central question**: To what extent is full membership determined by shared ethnicity, ancestry, and descent versus other criteria?
- **3B Central question**: To what extent is full membership contingent on cultural adoption, immersion, transformation, severance of prior ties, and loyalty?

### Strict scope boundaries (critical for both)
Do **not** analyse:
- Treatment of resident non-members, immigration volume, borders, or deportation (Core 2A / Sovereignty).
- Individual vs. group allocation of outcomes/rights (Cultural 2A).
- Explanations of disparities or equity interventions (Cultural 4B).
- Moral/ideological justifications or foundational sources (Cultural 1).
- Mere existence of national pride or culture (only *requirements* for others to adopt it matter for 3B).
- For **3A**: Any cultural, language, loyalty, or behavioural requirements (those belong to 3B).
- For **3B**: Any ethnic/ancestral criteria (those belong to 3A).

**Pairing note**: Score and analyse 3A and 3B together where they interact, but keep evidence cleanly separated.

## 2. Research and Data Collection

Gather evidence focused on **observable criteria for full membership** (law, institutions, public definitions, and practice), not rhetoric. Prioritise:
- Citizenship, naturalisation, and nationality laws.
- Constitutional or legal definitions of "the people," nationality, or in-group status.
- Institutional practices regarding recognition of full membership (e.g., in governance, military, inheritance of status).
- Court rulings or official policies that alter membership criteria.
- Historical shifts in ancestry/cultural requirements (e.g., jus sanguinis vs. jus soli evolutions, assimilation policies).
- Party platforms on citizenship and national identity (mainly for the Political Parties section).

Focus on **what was actually required or used** in practice for full membership.

## 3. Segmenting History into Eras (High Granularity First)

**Critical rule**: Start by creating **more eras than you initially think are necessary**. It is far easier to merge similar eras later than to split an era that has become too broad.

**Good reasons to create a new era**:
- Major changes in ethnic/ancestral membership criteria (3A) or cultural transformation requirements (3B).
- Shifts in citizenship/naturalisation laws (e.g., from ancestry-based to culture-based or vice versa).
- Significant expansions or relaxations of purity requirements, assimilation demands, or loyalty tests.
- Major legal or institutional changes affecting in-group boundaries.
- Periods of sustained contestation over membership definitions.

Aim for relatively short, coherent periods.

## 4. Scoring Each Era

Use the official **Cultural 3A bands** and **Cultural 3B bands (0–100)**.

**Scoring approach**:
- Focus on the **routine and structural** role of ethnic ancestry (3A) or cultural transformation (3B) in defining full membership.
- Assess predominance, consistency, and impact across core domains (law, institutions, official definitions).
- For mixed cases, apply primacy: weigh the dominant observable pattern.
- Justify scores directly against the specific band descriptions and examples.
- Score 3A and 3B independently but note interactions.

## 5. Writing the Era Sections

Use a consistent structure for every era. Maintain similar depth across all eras.

### Recommended Structure

**Era X: Year–Year – Era Name**  
**(3A Final Score: XX | 3B Final Score: XX)**

**Brief Overview** (3–5 sentences)  
Summarise the character of in-group boundary definition during this period.

**Membership Mechanisms – 3A (Ethnic Exclusivity)**  
Describe key observable ethnic/ancestral criteria with bullets and specific examples.

**Membership Mechanisms – 3B (Cultural Exclusivity)**  
Describe key observable cultural adoption/transformation requirements with bullets and specific examples.

**Why This Produces These Scores**  
Explain both scores with direct reference to the 3A/3B bands. Tie to extent, consistency, and impact of the criteria.

**Writing Standards**:
- Consistent analytical depth.
- Observable mechanisms only.
- Vary phrasing to avoid repetition.
- Precise and evidence-based.

## 6. Refining the Eras

After completing a first draft:
- Review the full set of eras and ask: Can any eras be merged without losing important nuance? Does any era contain distinct phases that would be better separated?
- Merge eras that show very similar membership patterns.
- Keep eras that represent meaningful shifts in ethnic or cultural boundary definitions.
- Aim for the smallest number of eras that still preserves the important turning points and changes in direction.

## 7. Writing the Political Parties Section

Include a Political Parties section at the end of the document. Parties should always be arranged alphabetically.

**Structure**:
- Short overall summary of the current state of in-group boundary definitions.
- Summary table with party names and 3A/3B Final Scores.
- Individual party entries using this format:
  - Brief Overview
  - Membership Mechanisms – 3A & 3B (with bullets)
  - Why This Produces These Scores

Base this section on current party platforms and positions, focusing on what each party proposes regarding citizenship, naturalisation, assimilation, and national identity criteria.

## 8. Final Assembly and Quality Control

**Include these elements in the final document**:
- Clear title (e.g. "Cultural 3A & 3B Timeline – [Country]")
- Era Summary Table (Era, Era Name, Period, 3A Score, 3B Score, One-line Summary)
- Basic Analysis / Overview of the Timeline (should be analytical, not just descriptive)
- Era sections in chronological order
- Political Parties section

**Quality Standards**:
- All eras should be written to a similar depth and analytical standard.
- The Overview section should identify the overall trajectory and main drivers of change in group boundaries.
- Repetitive phrasing across eras should be minimised.
- Later eras must receive the same level of analytical attention as earlier eras.
- Scoring justifications must be tied to observable membership criteria and the official bands.

## 9. Recommended Number of Eras

| Time Span     | Recommended Number of Eras | Guidance                  |
|---------------|----------------------------|---------------------------|
| 30–50 years  | 6–10                      | Start with more, then merge |
| 70–80+ years | 10–14                     | Start with 12–15, then refine |

## Summary Workflow

1. Define the time period and starting point.  
2. Research observable membership criteria (law and institutional practice).  
3. Create an initial high-granularity set of eras.  
4. Score each era using the Cultural 3A and 3B bands.  
5. Write the era sections to a consistent standard.  
6. Review and refine the eras (merge where appropriate).  
7. Write the Political Parties section.  
8. Write the Overview section (make it analytical).  
9. Review the full document against the quality standards above.  
10. Edit for repetition, consistency of depth, and analytical clarity.

This process is designed to produce rigorous, consistent, and analytically strong Cultural 3A & 3B timeline documents.
